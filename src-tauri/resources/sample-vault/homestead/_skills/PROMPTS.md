List all maintenance tasks that are overdue or due within the next 7 days, any utility or service bills due this week, and any contractor follow-ups or pending quotes. Flag any safety-related items (smoke/CO detectors, HVAC filter, water issues) with highest priority. Output a short, actionable weekly checklist sorted by urgency — no item without a clear next action."

2. **Home Monthly Review:** "Run a comprehensive home monthly review from vault/home/00_current/, vault/home/00_current/, vault/home/00_current/, and vault/home/00_current/. Cover: (1) maintenance completed this month and cost; (2) maintenance due next month; (3) current month utility spend vs. same month last year — flag any bill more than 15% above the prior-year equivalent; (4) seasonal checklist progress for the current season; (5) any contractor work in progress or outstanding invoices. Close with a prioritized action list for the next 30 days. Output one section per area."

3. **Home Brief:** "Generate a concise home brief in under 300 words covering: the single most urgent maintenance item, any utility anomaly this month (bill more than 15% above same month last year), the next seasonal checklist milestone, and one longer-term capital item to start planning (CapEx). Format as executive bullet points — no tables, just the essentials I need to act on today."

4. **Home Action Items:** "Pull all open maintenance tasks, seasonal checklist items, contractor follow-ups, and budget concerns from vault/home/00_current/, vault/home/00_current/, and vault/home/00_current/. Sort by urgency: safety items first, overdue items second, due within 7 days third, due within 30 days fourth. For each item show: category, description, deadline, estimated cost, and recommended next action. Output a numbered checklist I can work through in order."

## Maintenance

5. **Full Maintenance Schedule:** "Build a comprehensive home maintenance schedule from vault/home/00_current/. List all recurring maintenance tasks with: task name, system or area, recommended frequency, last completed date, next due date, and estimated cost. Flag any task that is overdue (past scheduled date) or due within the next 14 days. Apply system lifespan benchmarks to flag any major system approaching end of useful life: roof (20–25 years), HVAC (15–20 years), water heater (8–12 years), appliances (10–15 years). Output a maintenance calendar sorted by next due date."

6. **System Age and End-of-Life Tracker:** "Using installation dates and service records from vault/home/00_current/, calculate the current age and estimated remaining life for each major home system. Apply these lifespan benchmarks: roof 20–25 years, HVAC 15–20 years, water heater 8–12 years, dishwasher 9–10 years, refrigerator 10–15 years, washer/dryer 10–13 years, windows 15–20 years, exterior paint 5–10 years. For any system within 2 years of its expected end of life, flag as a CapEx planning item and include replacement cost range. Output a system age table with traffic-light status (green/yellow/red) and a CapEx planning list."

7. **HVAC Maintenance Review:** "Pull HVAC service history from vault/home/00_current/ and assess current status. Verify: last professional service date (should be annual — spring for AC, fall for furnace), current filter change status (standard 1-inch filters should be changed every 1–3 months; 5-inch media filters every 6–12 months), any unusual repair history in the last 2 years. If HVAC age is over 12 years, flag as approaching replacement — average replacement cost is $5,000–$10,000. If the system is over 15 years old, recommend getting a replacement quote now before emergency failure. Output a service history summary and a recommendation."

8. **Overdue Maintenance Report:** "Scan vault/home/00_current/ for all maintenance tasks that are past their scheduled date. For each overdue item show: task name, system, originally scheduled date, days overdue, estimated cost to address, and risk of deferral (e.g., 'deferred gutter cleaning increases risk of fascia rot and basement water intrusion'). Sort by risk severity: safety items first, structural items second, cosmetic items last. Output an overdue maintenance report with a recommended completion sequence."

For each task show whether it has been completed this season, estimated cost if a contractor is needed, and the recommended contractor from vault/home/00_current/ if applicable."

10. **Summer Home Checklist:** "Generate my summer home maintenance checklist using vault/home/00_current/. Also flag: if the AC has not been serviced this spring, mark that as urgent before peak summer heat. Output a checklist with completion status and contractor referrals where needed."

11. **Fall Home Checklist:** "Generate my fall home maintenance checklist using vault/home/00_current/ and vault/home/00_current/. Fall tasks: furnace service and filter replacement before heating season, chimney inspection and cleaning if applicable (flag if wood-burning fireplace — annual cleaning required by NFPA 211), gutter cleaning post-leaf-fall (second cleaning of the year), weatherstripping inspection and replacement on exterior doors and windows, programmable thermostat scheduling for heating season, exterior water shutoff and hose storage before first freeze, dryer vent cleaning (flag if last cleaned more than 12 months ago — leading cause of household fires). Output a seasonal checklist with completion status and urgency flags."

12. **Winter Home Checklist:** "Generate my winter home maintenance checklist using vault/home/00_current/. 13. **Seasonal Maintenance Review:** "Using vault/home/00_current/ and the current month, determine what season we are in and assess seasonal checklist completion. ## Expenses

14. **Home Expense Summary:** "Build a complete home expense summary from vault/home/00_current/ for the current year to date. Categorize all expenses: mortgage/rent (PITI or monthly rent), utilities (electricity, gas, water, internet, trash), maintenance and repairs, landscaping, HOA fees, cleaning services, and one-time capital improvements. For each category show: total YTD spend, monthly average, and percent of total home budget. Apply the 1% rule benchmark: annual maintenance and repair costs should be budgeted at 1% of home value — flag if YTD spend is tracking more than 50% above or below that benchmark. Output a categorized expense table."

15. **Utility Trend Analysis:** "Using utility data from vault/home/00_current/, analyze my utility bills for the last 12 months. For each utility (electricity, gas, water), compare current month to the same month in the prior year. Flag any utility bill that is more than 15% higher than the same month last year. Also flag if the trailing 3-month average of any utility is trending up more than 10% vs. the prior 3-month average. For flagged utilities, list likely causes (e.g., HVAC inefficiency, water leak, rate increase) and recommended diagnostic steps. Output a utility trend table with year-over-year comparison per utility."

16. **Maintenance Budget Tracker:** "Compare actual maintenance spend from vault/home/00_current/ to the annual maintenance budget. The baseline budget target is 1% of home value per year (adjust if a different target is stored). Show: YTD actual spend, annualized pace, target budget, variance in dollars and percent, and months remaining in the year. Break down which expense categories are running over or under budget. Flag if maintenance spending is on track to exceed budget by more than 20%. Also flag if spending is unusually low — deferred maintenance often leads to larger costs later. Output a budget vs. actual table."

17. **Home Improvement ROI Analysis:** "For any completed or planned home improvements in vault/home/00_current/ or vault/home/00_current/, analyze return on investment at time of sale using industry benchmarks. Standard ROI ranges: kitchen remodel 60–80%, bathroom remodel 60–70%, deck addition 65–80%, landscaping and curb appeal 100%+, new roof 60–70%, fresh paint (exterior) 150%+, master suite addition 50–60%, finished basement 60–75%. For each improvement: show cost, estimated resale value added, ROI percent, and whether the improvement was primarily for personal enjoyment vs. resale value. Recommend future improvements that maximize ROI for my specific home type."

## Contractors

18. **Contractor Directory Review:** "Pull all contractor records from vault/home/00_current/ and build a current contractor directory. For each contractor show: trade (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, roofing, landscaping, general contractor, etc.), company name, contact name, phone, email, license number, insurance status (verified yes/no), last date used, rating (1–5), and any notes on quality or reliability. Flag any contractor where license or insurance status is unverified — always verify before hiring for jobs over $500. Flag any trade where I do not have a vetted contractor on file — these are gaps to fill before a need arises."

19. **Contractor Bid Tracker:** "For any project in vault/home/00_current/ or vault/home/00_current/ where bids have been requested, show a side-by-side comparison. For each bid include: contractor name, bid amount, scope of work differences from other bids, timeline, warranty offered, payment terms, and license/insurance verified status. Apply the 3-bids rule: any project over $1,000 should have at least 3 bids — flag if fewer than 3 bids have been collected. Flag the lowest bid if it is more than 20% below the median — anomalously low bids warrant additional scrutiny. Recommend which contractor to hire based on the full comparison."

20. **Repair vs. Replace Analysis:** "For a specified home system or appliance with a known repair cost, help me decide whether to repair or replace it. Apply the 50% rule: if the repair cost exceeds 50% of the replacement cost of a new system, replacing is generally more cost-effective, especially for systems over half their expected lifespan. Show: system name, age, estimated remaining lifespan, repair cost, full replacement cost (with installation), annual energy savings if replacement is more efficient (e.g., high-efficiency HVAC vs. older unit), and break-even timeline for replacement. Also flag any rebates or tax credits available (e.g., federal 25C energy efficiency credit for qualifying HVAC systems). Output a clear repair-vs.-replace recommendation."
