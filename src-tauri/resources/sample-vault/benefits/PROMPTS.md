# AI Ready Life: Benefits — Prompt Reference

## Weekly & Monthly Reviews

1. **Monthly Benefits Review:** "Run my monthly benefits review using vault/benefits/00_current/. Check 401k contribution pace against the 2025 IRS limit ($23,500 / $31,000 if age 50+) and flag if I am on track to max by December 31. Verify HSA contributions are on pace for the 2025 limit ($4,300 individual / $8,550 family) and that employer contributions are being received as expected. Check ESPP: confirm the current contribution rate, note the next purchase date, and compute the expected return at 15% discount. Review any upcoming vesting events for RSUs in the next 60 days. Output a benefits status dashboard: one status line per benefit type (401k, HSA, ESPP, RSU, Health Coverage) with a Green/Yellow/Red rating and one action item each."

2. **Benefits Annual Snapshot:** "Produce my annual benefits snapshot by reading vault/benefits/00_current/ and vault/benefits/00_current/. For each benefit — 401k, HSA, health insurance, dental, vision, life insurance, disability insurance, ESPP, and equity grants — report: current election, employer contribution (dollar value), employee contribution YTD, and total value received from employer this year. Compute the total employer-provided benefits value as a dollar amount to be added to my total compensation view. Flag any benefit I am enrolled in that is suboptimal (e.g., not capturing full 401k match, HDHP elected without HSA funded). Output a benefits value table and a list of 3 optimization opportunities."

3. **Benefits Action Items This Month:** "Identify all benefits-related action items that must be completed this month by reading vault/benefits/00_current/ and vault/benefits/00_current/. Items to check: 401k contribution rate adjustment needed to hit year-end max, HSA contribution catch-up needed, ESPP enrollment window open or closing, upcoming RSU vest requiring a hold vs. sell decision, FSA spending deadline approaching (FSA is use-it-or-lose-it, unlike HSA which rolls over), and any benefits documents requiring annual renewal or acknowledgment. For each action item, specify the deadline, the financial impact of inaction, and the exact step to complete it. Output an action list sorted by deadline."

## 401(k)

4. **401k Contribution Pace Analysis:** "Analyze my 401k contribution pace for the current year using vault/benefits/00_current/. Extract: YTD employee contributions (traditional and Roth), YTD employer match received, total YTD contributions, and the 2025 IRS employee elective deferral limit ($23,500 for under-50, $31,000 if age 50+ including $7,500 catch-up). Compute: monthly contribution rate, months remaining in the year, projected year-end contributions at the current rate, and the gap to the IRS limit. Calculate the bi-weekly or monthly payroll contribution increase needed to max out by December 31. Flag if current pace leaves >$1,000 on the table at year-end. Output: contribution table, gap analysis, and required contribution adjustment."

5. **Employer Match Capture Verification:** "Verify I am capturing my full employer 401k match using vault/benefits/00_current/. Extract the employer match formula (e.g., 100% of first 3% + 50% of next 2% of compensation, or flat match percentage). Compute: the minimum employee contribution percentage required to receive the full match, the dollar value of the full annual match at my current salary, the YTD match I have actually received, and whether the match is on track vs. the expected annual amount. Flag if my contribution rate is below the threshold to receive the full match — this is forfeiting free money. Flag also if my 401k elections are front-loaded in a way that could cause me to stop contributing mid-year, causing missed match (check for match true-up policy). Output: match formula, full match value, YTD received, and recommendation."

6. **Roth vs. Traditional 401k Decision Analysis:** "Analyze whether I should be contributing to Roth or traditional 401k using vault/benefits/00_current/ and vault/tax/00_current/. Pull my current projected AGI, marginal tax bracket, and expected retirement income. Decision framework: if current marginal rate (22–24%) is lower than expected retirement rate, prefer Roth; if current marginal rate (32%+) is higher than expected retirement rate, prefer traditional; at 22–24% with uncertainty, split contributions. Consider state income tax: if my state has income tax now but I plan to retire to a no-income-tax state, favor traditional. Note Roth 401k has no income limit (unlike Roth IRA). Output: current vs. projected tax rate comparison, recommendation (Roth / Traditional / Split), and optimal split percentage if applicable."

7. **401k Allocation & Fund Audit:** "Audit my 401k investment allocation using vault/benefits/00_current/ or linked 401k statement in vault/wealth/00_current/. List all fund options I am invested in with: fund name, expense ratio, current allocation percentage, and asset class. Flag any fund with an expense ratio >0.5% as a high-cost drag. Compare my current allocation to a target glide path appropriate for my retirement horizon (e.g., aggressive at 30–45 years to retirement: 90% equity; moderate at 15–30 years: 70–80% equity). Identify the lowest-cost index fund alternatives available in my 401k plan for each current holding. Compute the annual fee savings from switching to lower-cost alternatives. Output: holdings table with expense flags, target vs. actual allocation, and specific fund swap recommendations."

## HSA

8. **HSA Contribution & Balance Review:** "Review my HSA status using vault/benefits/00_current/. Extract: HSA account balance, YTD employee contributions, YTD employer contributions, and total YTD combined vs. the 2025 IRS limit ($4,300 individual / $8,550 family). Calculate: remaining contribution room, monthly payroll contribution needed to max by December 31, and annual tax savings from maxing the HSA (contributions × marginal tax rate + FICA rate, since HSA contributions via payroll are exempt from both income tax and FICA). Confirm that my health plan qualifies as a High Deductible Health Plan (HDHP): deductible ≥$1,650 individual / $3,300 family for 2025. Flag if I have any non-HDHP coverage that disqualifies HSA contributions. Output: balance snapshot, gap to max, tax savings calculation, and HDHP eligibility status."

9. **HSA Investment Strategy:** "Assess my HSA investment strategy using vault/benefits/00_current/. Many HSA administrators require a minimum cash balance ($1,000–$2,000) before allowing investments. Pull my current HSA cash balance and estimate my expected annual out-of-pocket medical expenses based on vault/health/00_current/ claims history. If HSA balance exceeds 2× expected annual medical spend, the excess should be invested rather than held in cash (triple-tax advantage is maximized when HSA assets compound tax-free for decades). Identify the investment options available in my HSA plan and their expense ratios. Recommend a specific dollar amount to invest vs. keep in cash. Output: current balance, estimated annual medical spend, investable surplus, and a recommended investment allocation within the HSA."

10. **HSA Qualified Expense Tracker:** "Review vault/benefits/00_current/ and vault/health/00_current/ for medical expenses paid out-of-pocket in the current year. Identify all qualified HSA expenses (IRS Publication 502 eligible): doctor visits, prescription copays, dental and vision care, medical equipment, mental health services, and other eligible costs. For any expense paid with personal funds (not HSA), note the amount and date — these can be reimbursed from the HSA at any future date (no deadline for reimbursement as long as the expense occurred after the HSA was opened). Compute total unreimbursed qualified expenses YTD. Flag if out-of-pocket medical spend is approaching the out-of-pocket maximum. Output: expense log, total eligible unreimbursed amount, and reimbursement recommendation."

## Health Coverage

11. **Health Insurance Coverage Summary:** "Build my health insurance coverage summary using vault/benefits/00_current/. Extract and document: plan name and type (HDHP/PPO/HMO/EPO), carrier, plan year dates, annual deductible (individual and family), out-of-pocket maximum (individual and family), deductible progress YTD, copay structure (PCP / specialist / urgent care / emergency room / telehealth), coinsurance percentage after deductible, prescription drug tiers (tier 1 through tier 4 copay/coinsurance), in-network vs. out-of-network coverage, and mental health parity compliance. Confirm HDHP status for HSA eligibility. Flag any cost-sharing element that has changed from the prior plan year. Output a clean coverage card and a plan year cost-tracking table."

12. **Deductible Progress Tracker:** "Track my progress toward my health insurance deductible and out-of-pocket maximum using vault/benefits/00_current/ and vault/health/00_current/. Extract from my plan: individual deductible, family deductible, individual out-of-pocket maximum, and family out-of-pocket maximum. Pull YTD claims data or EOB (Explanation of Benefits) records to compute: amount applied to deductible YTD, remaining deductible balance, amount applied to out-of-pocket maximum YTD, and remaining OOP max balance. Forecast whether I will hit the deductible before year-end based on current utilization pace. If close to hitting the OOP max, flag elective procedures that would be 100% covered if scheduled before year-end. Output: deductible and OOP progress bars and a year-end coverage projection."

13. **Dental & Vision Benefits Audit:** "Audit my dental and vision benefits using vault/benefits/00_current/. For dental: extract annual maximum benefit (typically $1,000–$2,000), plan year period, preventive care coverage (typically 100%), basic service coverage (fillings — typically 80%), and major service coverage (crowns — typically 50%). Compute remaining dental benefit for the year and flag if I have more than $200 of unused benefit expiring before year-end. For vision: extract allowance for frames/lenses/contacts (typically $150–$200/year), whether it resets calendar year or plan year, and exam copay. Flag any unused vision benefit expiring. Output: dental and vision benefit summary, amounts used and remaining, and recommended actions before year-end."

## ESPP

14. **ESPP Return Analysis:** "Analyze my ESPP participation using vault/benefits/00_current/. Extract: current contribution rate (%), annual salary, offering period start and end dates, purchase price discount (target 15%), lookback provision (purchase price is the lower of stock price at offering start or purchase date × 85%), and number of shares purchased in the most recent period. Calculate the guaranteed return at purchase under the lookback: if stock is flat, return = 17.6% (buying at 85% of price); if stock rose, the lookback increases the return further. Assess disqualifying disposition risk: selling within 2 years of offering date or 1 year of purchase date converts the discount gain from capital gains to ordinary income. Recommend: immediate sell at purchase (capture the guaranteed discount), or hold for favorable tax treatment. Output: return calculation, tax treatment comparison for both paths, and a recommendation."

15. **ESPP Enrollment & Contribution Optimization:** "Optimize my ESPP contribution rate using vault/benefits/00_current/. The ESPP statutory limit is $25,000 of stock per calendar year at fair market value. At a 15% discount with lookback, participating at the maximum rate within cash flow constraints is typically the highest-return investment available. Extract my current contribution rate and compute: annual ESPP stock purchase dollar value, approximate annual guaranteed return in dollars (discount value), and the cash flow impact of current vs. maximum contribution rate. Flag if I am below the maximum allowable contribution rate and have sufficient monthly cash flow to increase participation. Note that ESPP contributions must be elected during the enrollment window — missing the window locks you out for the full offering period. Output: current vs. maximum contribution comparison, annual return differential, and an enrollment action recommendation."

## Equity Grants

16. **RSU Vesting Schedule & Tax Impact:** "Build a comprehensive RSU vesting tracker using vault/benefits/00_current/. For each RSU grant, extract: grant date, total shares granted, grant price, vesting schedule (cliff and thereafter), shares vested to date, shares unvested, and current stock price. Compute the fair market value of unvested shares at the current stock price. For each vesting event in the next 12 months, calculate the estimated ordinary income tax impact (shares × FMV at vest × marginal tax rate + Medicare surtax if applicable). Determine whether tax withholding at vest (typically 22% federal supplemental rate) is sufficient given my marginal rate. Flag any vest event where supplemental withholding undersells the actual liability. Output: vesting schedule table with tax estimates and a withholding gap analysis."

17. **RSU Hold vs. Sell Decision Framework:** "Evaluate whether to hold or sell RSUs immediately at vest using vault/benefits/00_current/ and vault/wealth/00_current/. RSUs are taxed as ordinary income at vest regardless of the hold decision — the hold vs. sell question is purely an investment decision post-vesting. Compute: current concentration of employer stock as a percentage of total investment portfolio. Flag if employer stock exceeds 10% of total portfolio value as concentration risk (single-stock risk is uncompensated). If concentration is high, recommend an automatic sell-at-vest policy for a portion of each grant. If holding for long-term capital gains treatment, compute the required holding period (>1 year from vest date) and the tax rate differential (ordinary income rate vs. 15–20% LTCG rate). Output: concentration analysis, tax rate comparison, and a hold/sell recommendation for the next vest event."

18. **Equity Compensation Total Value Summary:** "Build a total equity compensation summary using vault/benefits/00_current/. Include all equity grants: RSUs (unvested shares × current stock price), options (in-the-money options: shares × (current price − strike price), out-of-the-money options: $0 intrinsic value), and ESPP shares held. Compute: total unvested equity value, total vested but unsold equity value, and total ESPP holdings. Project equity income for the next 12 months based on the vesting schedule. Flag any grant with a cliff date in the next 90 days — cliff vesting is a 'use it or lose it' moment if employment is terminated before the cliff date. Output: equity summary table by grant type and a 12-month vesting income projection."

## Open Enrollment

19. **Open Enrollment Preparation Checklist:** "Prepare my open enrollment strategy using vault/benefits/00_current/ and vault/benefits/00_current/. Open enrollment is typically a 2–4 week window — missing it locks elections for the full plan year. For each benefit category, assess whether the current election should change: (1) Health plan — HDHP vs. PPO cost-benefit based on expected utilization and HSA eligibility; (2) 401k contribution rate — adjust to maximize match and progress toward IRS limit; (3) HSA contribution — maximize to 2025 limit; (4) FSA — only elect if confident in spending amount (use-it-or-lose-it); (5) Life insurance — confirm coverage = 10× salary target; (6) Disability — confirm short-term and long-term coverage is elected. Output a per-benefit election recommendation with the dollar impact of each change."

20. **HDHP vs. PPO Trade-off Analysis:** "Analyze whether I should elect the HDHP or PPO during open enrollment using vault/benefits/00_current/. For each plan, extract: monthly premium (employee portion), annual deductible, out-of-pocket maximum, coinsurance, and prescription drug costs. Compute the break-even analysis: total HDHP annual cost (premium × 12 + expected out-of-pocket) vs. total PPO annual cost. Offset the HDHP total cost by the HSA tax savings (HSA contribution × combined marginal tax rate + FICA) — this is the unique advantage of the HDHP. For a healthy individual with low expected utilization, the HDHP with a maxed HSA typically wins. For a chronic condition or expected surgery, the PPO may break even or win. Output: side-by-side cost table under 3 scenarios (low/medium/high utilization), HSA tax savings offset, and a recommendation."

21. **Life & Disability Insurance Adequacy Review:** "Review my life and disability insurance coverage using vault/benefits/00_current/. For life insurance: the standard recommendation is 10× annual income in coverage. Compare my current coverage (employer-provided + any supplemental or private policy) to this target. Flag if coverage is below 10× income. Assess whether increasing supplemental life coverage through the employer (often guaranteed-issue during open enrollment, no medical underwriting) is cost-effective vs. a private term policy. For disability insurance: confirm I have both short-term disability (typically covers 60 days to 6 months) and long-term disability (covers 60–70% of income until retirement age). Flag if long-term disability benefit caps out at a dollar amount that is less than 60% of my current gross monthly income. Output: coverage gaps, cost of closing each gap via employer plan vs. private market, and a prioritized recommendation."

## Open Loops

22. **Benefits Open Loop Audit:** "Read vault/benefits/00_current/open-loops.md and cross-reference with vault/benefits/00_current/ to assess each unresolved benefits item. Classify by urgency: Critical (approaching enrollment deadline or a benefit being forfeited), High (financial impact >$500 per year if not resolved), Medium (optimization not yet actioned), Low (informational). Flag any item involving an enrollment window closing within 30 days as Critical regardless of dollar amount. For each open loop, specify: the benefit affected, the financial impact of inaction, the exact step to resolve, and the deadline. Output a ranked open loop list with a one-line action for each item."

23. **Year-End Benefits Deadline Sweep:** "Identify all benefits actions that must be completed before December 31 by reading vault/benefits/00_current/. Time-sensitive items: (1) FSA spending — use-it-or-lose-it funds must be spent by plan year end (flag any FSA balance >$100 unspent with <60 days left in the year); (2) HSA contributions — last chance to contribute for the current year before April 15 tax filing deadline, but employer HSA contributions via payroll must be elected by year-end; (3) ESPP enrollment — confirm next offering period enrollment is in place; (4) 401k contribution rate — final pay periods to maximize the IRS limit; (5) Dependent care FSA — use it or lose it. For each item, compute the financial impact of missing the deadline. Output a deadline countdown list sorted by date."
