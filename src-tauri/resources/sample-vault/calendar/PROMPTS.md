# AI Ready Life: Calendar — Prompt Reference

## Weekly Agenda

1. **Weekly Agenda Build:** "Build my weekly agenda for the coming 7 days. Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for all scheduled meetings, appointments, and commitments this week. Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for all hard deadlines due this week or requiring prep work this week. Calculate my meeting-to-work ratio: total scheduled meeting time divided by available working hours (assume 40 hours per week) and flag if meetings exceed 40% of working hours. Identify any day with more than 4 context switches (switching between different project types or domains) and flag it as a high cognitive-load day. Suggest at least 2 focus blocks of 90 minutes or more, ideally placed in the 9–11am morning window. Output a day-by-day agenda with meetings, deadlines, and recommended focus block placements."

2. **Next Week Preview:** "What does next week look like and should I adjust anything before it starts? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for all events scheduled for next week and group by day. Identify any day with back-to-back meetings lasting more than 2 hours total — suggest adding a 15-minute buffer after any single meeting longer than 60 minutes. Check vault/calendar/00_current/ for any deadline falling next week and verify that prep work for that deadline is already calendared the week before. Flag any day where I have zero protected focus time. Cross-reference with vault/chief/00_current/ for my top 3 priorities this quarter and check whether at least one focus block next week is assigned to a quarterly priority. Output a next-week preview with an optimization checklist."

3. **Weekly Priority Calendar Check:** "What are my top priorities this week and are they actually on my calendar? Read vault/chief/00_current/ for my current top 3 quarterly priorities. Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for this week's schedule and check whether each priority has at least one dedicated time block assigned this week. If a priority has no calendar time this week, flag it as at risk of being crowded out by reactive work. Assess whether my calendar reflects my stated priorities or whether my time is being consumed by low-priority meetings. Calculate the percentage of this week's scheduled working time that is directed toward quarterly priorities (target: at least 30%). Output a priority-calendar alignment score, a list of any priority with no blocked time, and 2 specific calendar changes to better reflect my priorities."

## Deadlines

4. **30-Day Deadline Sweep:** "What deadlines are coming up in the next 30 days and am I adequately prepared for each? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ and all domain open-loops.md files for any item with a due date in the next 30 days. For each deadline, extract: domain, item description, due date, estimated prep time, and whether prep work is already calendared. Apply these lead-time rules: surface tax deadlines 14 days early, bill payments 5 days early, and travel document requirements 30 days early. Flag any deadline where the prep time required exceeds the available days before it is due. Sort all deadlines by urgency (days remaining divided by days needed) so the tightest deadlines appear first. Output a ranked deadline table with days remaining, prep time needed, prep calendared (yes/no), and a recommended calendar action per item."

5. **Cross-Domain Deadline Collection:** "Collect all deadlines from my active domains and build a unified deadline calendar. Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for calendar-specific deadlines. Then read deadline sections from vault/tax/ (quarterly estimated tax due dates, filing deadlines), vault/business/00_current/ (annual reports, BOI filings), vault/wealth/ (any investment or account action deadlines), vault/health/ (any appointment follow-up deadlines), vault/career/ (any work deliverable deadlines), and vault/content/ (publishing schedule commitments). Consolidate all deadlines into a single master list. Group by urgency: due in 0–7 days (critical), 8–14 days (urgent), 15–30 days (planned). Output a consolidated deadline calendar with domain, item, due date, urgency tier, and a recommended prep-start date for each item."

6. **Monthly Deadline Planner:** "What is due this month and how should I sequence my work to hit every deadline? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for all items due in the current calendar month. For each deadline, determine the optimal start date by working backwards from the due date minus the estimated prep time. Check vault/calendar/00_current/ for any week this month where my meeting load is above 40% of working hours, and avoid scheduling deadline prep during those weeks. Identify any week where two or more high-stakes deadlines cluster, creating a potential crunch. Recommend a sequenced work plan for the month that front-loads deadline prep into weeks with available capacity. Output a monthly planner with deadline start dates, prep windows, and flagged crunch weeks."

## Focus Time

7. **Focus Time Analysis:** "How much focus time did I have this week and is it enough to make real progress on my priorities? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for this week's completed schedule and estimate focus time as any uninterrupted block of 90 minutes or more with no scheduled meetings. Sum total focus time for the week in hours. Compare to the minimum of 2 focus blocks per week (3 hours total minimum). Flag if focus time fell below 3 hours as critically low. Analyze the distribution of focus time across days — is it concentrated in the morning (9–11am, which is optimal) or scattered throughout the day? Cross-reference with vault/chief/00_current/ for my top priorities and assess whether focus time this week was directed at high-value priorities or consumed by reactive tasks. Output a focus time scorecard and 2 recommendations for improving focus block quality next week."

8. **Deep Work vs. Meeting Load Review:** "Analyze my focus time versus my meeting load for this month and flag any imbalance. Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for all events in the past 30 days. Classify each event as: deep work (90+ minute uninterrupted focus block), shallow work (email, admin, short tasks), meeting (scheduled interaction with another person), and personal (appointments, errands). Calculate the percentage of working hours spent in each category. Flag the week with the highest meeting-to-focus ratio as the most problematic. Assess whether my meeting load is trending upward over the past 4 weeks. Recommend a specific recurring meeting that could be eliminated, shortened, or converted to async. Output a time distribution chart in text, a meeting-to-focus ratio trend, and 3 structural changes to protect more deep work time."

9. **Focus Block Recommendation:** "Where should I schedule focus blocks next week to maximize my deep work output? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for next week's scheduled commitments and identify all 90-minute or longer uninterrupted windows. Cross-reference with vault/chief/00_current/ for my current top 3 priorities and estimate how much focused time each priority needs this week. Recommend the 3 best focus block placements for next week, using these rules: prefer 9–11am slots, avoid placing focus blocks the hour immediately following a meeting longer than 60 minutes (cognitive recovery time needed), and ensure at least one focus block is on a day with minimal other commitments. For each recommended block, specify the day, time, duration, and which priority it should be dedicated to. Output 3 specific focus block recommendations with rationale."

## Deadline Planning

10. **Tax Deadline Planning:** "Help me plan my work schedule around the upcoming tax deadlines. Read vault/tax/ for all relevant tax deadlines in the next 90 days, including quarterly estimated tax payment due dates, filing extension deadlines, and any state-specific deadlines. For each deadline, calculate the prep time required (document gathering, CPA coordination, payment scheduling) and work backwards to set a start date — at minimum 14 days before the due date. Check vault/calendar/00_current/ for any week within the prep window that already has heavy meeting load (above 40%), and shift prep start earlier if needed. Identify whether any tax deadline overlaps with a major work or personal commitment and flag the conflict with a specific resolution. Output a tax deadline planning calendar with start dates, prep tasks, and conflict flags."

11. **Project Deadline Reverse Planning:** "What needs to happen before a specific deadline and is my calendar set up to make it possible? Identify the nearest high-stakes deadline across vault/calendar/00_current/ and vault/chief/02_briefs/. For that deadline, list every prerequisite task and estimate the time required for each. Work backwards from the deadline to assign each task to a specific week, ensuring the final task is completed 2 business days before the deadline as a buffer. Check vault/calendar/00_current/ for each assigned week to confirm capacity exists. Flag any week where the assigned prep task exceeds available focus time. Adjust the plan by moving lower-priority items off the calendar during crunch weeks. Output a reverse-engineered deadline plan with week-by-week task assignments and a risk flag for any capacity constraint."

12. **Deadline Lead-Time Audit:** "Am I starting work on my deadlines early enough? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for all deadlines completed in the last 60 days. For each, calculate how many days before the deadline I actually started preparing (first log entry) versus the recommended lead time (tax: 14 days, bill payments: 5 days, travel docs: 30 days, compliance filings: 21 days, content publishing: 7 days). Identify any deadline where I started preparing fewer than 3 days before it was due. Calculate my average actual lead time versus recommended lead time across all deadline categories. Flag any category (e.g., tax, compliance) where I am consistently late to start. Output a lead-time compliance table by category, an average lead time score, and 2 specific system changes to improve early-start behavior."

## Alerts

13. **7-Day Deadline Alert:** "Alert me about any deadlines within the next 7 days that require immediate action. Read vault/calendar/00_current/ and all domain open-loops.md files for items due within 7 calendar days. For each item, extract: the exact due date, the domain, the consequence of missing it (financial penalty, compliance violation, relationship damage), and the estimated hours needed to complete it. Calculate whether I have enough unscheduled hours in the next 7 days to complete all of them. If total required hours exceed available capacity, recommend which items to prioritize and which (if any) can be safely deferred or delegated. Output a 7-day alert table sorted by due date, plus a capacity check showing required hours versus available hours."

14. **Next Week Radar Report:** "What should I put on my radar for next week that I might not have thought about? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ and vault/calendar/00_current/ for next week's known commitments. Then read all domain open-loops.md files for any item that will need attention next week but may not yet have a formal calendar entry — for example, a monthly review due, a bill payment window opening, a content publish due, or a follow-up that was deferred to next week. Identify any hidden time demand not currently on my calendar. Flag any day next week that appears light on the calendar but actually has significant background work due. Output a radar report with known commitments, hidden time demands, and 3 calendar actions I should take today to prepare for next week."

## School & Family

15. **School & Family Events Review:** "What school events and family commitments are coming up in the next 30 days? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ and filter for any events tagged as school, family, or personal. List all upcoming school events (recitals, conferences, holidays, early dismissals, curriculum nights), family appointments, and personal obligations with their dates and any prep required. Flag any school holiday or closure that falls during a week when I have heavy work commitments, and identify the childcare or coverage plan needed. Check whether any family event conflicts with a work deadline or travel commitment and flag it as a scheduling conflict. Output a family and school calendar for the next 30 days with conflict flags and a recommended action for each conflict."

16. **School Holiday and Break Planning:** "Are there any school closures or breaks coming up that will affect my work schedule? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for the school calendar over the next 90 days and identify all multi-day closures, breaks, early dismissals, and teacher in-service days. For each closure lasting more than 1 day, check whether I have work commitments that day (meetings, deadlines, travel) that will require alternative childcare or schedule adjustment. Calculate the total number of school closure days in the next 90 days. Identify the longest consecutive closure period and assess whether it overlaps with any major work deliverable or tax deadline. Output a school closure calendar with overlap flags, a total impact day count, and a recommended contingency plan for the most conflicted closure period."

## Integrations

17. **Calendar and Deadline Sync Audit:** "Are my vault deadlines and actual calendar entries in sync? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for all logged deadlines and compare against vault/calendar/00_current/ to verify that each deadline has a corresponding calendar event or prep block. Flag any deadline logged in vault that does not have a corresponding entry in the events calendar — these are invisible deadline risks. Identify any calendar event that appears to be deadline prep (e.g., a blocked 'Tax Prep' event) but does not correspond to any deadline in the vault, suggesting a documentation gap. Calculate the percentage of vault deadlines that have a matching calendar event. Output a sync audit table with deadline name, vault status (logged/not logged), calendar status (scheduled/not scheduled), and a reconciliation action per item."

18. **Domain Deadline Pull:** "Pull the complete deadline list from all my domain vaults and verify nothing is missing from my main calendar. Read vault/tax/ for estimated payment dates and filing deadlines. Read vault/business/00_current/ for annual reports, BOI, and registered agent renewal dates. Read vault/wealth/ for any required minimum distribution or account action deadlines. Read vault/health/ for follow-up appointment deadlines. Read vault/content/00_current/ for content publishing commitments. Read vault/career/ for any performance review or work deliverable dates. Cross-reference all findings against vault/calendar/00_current/ and flag any domain deadline not yet captured in the master deadline list. Output a domain-by-domain deadline pull with a column indicating whether each item is already in the master calendar or is newly discovered."

## Open Loops

19. **Calendar Open Loop Triage:** "What calendar action items are unresolved and are they creating invisible risks? Read vault/calendar/open-loops.md and extract every open item with its creation date, category (scheduling, deadline planning, focus blocks, integrations, family), and any last-update note. Calculate days open per item. Flag any item open more than 14 days. Assess each item's operational risk: does the unresolved item create a scheduling conflict, a missed deadline, or a gap in my time protection strategy? For each high-risk open loop, recommend a specific action with a 'complete by' date. For low-risk items open more than 30 days, recommend closing or deferring to a future review. Output a triage table sorted by risk level and days open, with a resolution recommendation per item."

20. **Weekly Review and Next Week Setup:** "Run my weekly review and set up next week. First, assess the prior week: read vault/calendar/00_current/ for last week's schedule and check how many of my stated top 3 priorities had dedicated focus time (target: all 3). Count meetings that could have been async communications. Calculate whether my meeting-to-focus ratio stayed below the 40% threshold. Then set up next week: pull vault/calendar/00_current/ for next week's items, identify where focus blocks should go (prefer 9–11am, minimum 90 minutes), and flag any day with zero protected time. Identify the single most important outcome I need to achieve next week and confirm it is blocked on the calendar. Output a prior-week scorecard and a next-week setup checklist."
