# AI Ready Life: Real Estate — Prompt Reference

## Monthly Reviews

1. **Real Estate Monthly Review:** "Pull all active market data from vault/real-estate/00_current/ and all saved listings from vault/real-estate/00_current/ to produce my monthly real estate brief. For each target market report: median price change month-over-month, days on market trend, active inventory level, and months of supply. Flag any market where months of supply dropped below 3 (seller's market threshold) or rose above 6 (buyer's market threshold). For saved listings, flag any where price has changed more than 2% since saved or where DOM exceeds 45 days — both are negotiation signals. Output a market dashboard table followed by a one-paragraph strategic outlook."

2. **Real Estate Brief:** "Generate a focused real estate brief using vault/real-estate/00_current/ as the primary source. Structure it in four sections: (1) Portfolio status — any owned properties and their current estimated value, occupancy, and cash flow. (2) Market watch — top 2 target markets and their current supply/demand signal. (3) Pipeline — saved listings with status and next action. (4) Decisions pending — any open analysis or pre-approval items requiring action this month. Keep the brief under 400 words and close with a single 'Top Priority This Month' callout."

3. **Quarterly Market Reassessment:** "Read vault/real-estate/00_current/ and compare current market data against the snapshot saved 90 days ago. For each tracked market calculate: median price delta (%), months of supply delta, average days on market delta, and price-to-rent ratio change. Apply the price-to-rent threshold: a ratio below 15 favors buying, above 20 favors renting. Flag any market that has crossed a threshold since the last assessment. Output a before-and-after comparison table per market and a one-sentence buy/hold/wait verdict for each."

## Market Scan

4. **Multi-Market Supply and Demand Scan:** "Using vault/real-estate/00_current/, pull the latest data for all tracked markets and score each on a supply/demand index: months of supply (weighted 40%), DOM trend over 90 days (30%), and list-to-sale price ratio (30%). Classify each market as Seller's (supply < 3 months), Balanced (3–6 months), or Buyer's (> 6 months). For any market classified as Buyer's, flag it as an opportunity window and estimate how long the window may persist based on trend direction. Output a ranked market scorecard with composite scores."

5. **Investment Market Deep Scan:** "For my top investment target market in vault/real-estate/00_current/, run a full fundamental analysis. Report: population growth rate (3-year trend), unemployment rate vs. national average, median household income growth, rental vacancy rate, rent growth rate year-over-year, and median cap rate for the asset class I am targeting. Flag any market where cap rates are below 6% for stable markets or below 8% for secondary markets — these fail my investment threshold. Output a one-page market fundamentals brief with a Go / Watch / Avoid verdict."

6. **Inventory Trend Alert:** "Pull historical and current inventory data from vault/real-estate/00_current/ for each tracked market. Calculate the 90-day inventory change percentage. Flag any market where active listings have increased more than 15% in 90 days — this is an early cooling signal. Conversely, flag any market where inventory has decreased more than 10% in 90 days — this signals accelerating competition. For each flagged market, calculate the new estimated months of supply and update the buy/wait verdict. Output a trend alert summary per market."

7. **Price Reduction Rate Analysis:** "For each target market in vault/real-estate/00_current/, calculate the price reduction rate — the percentage of active listings that have had at least one price cut. A rate above 25% signals market softening and buyer leverage. A rate below 10% signals tight competition. Compare the current rate to the 6-month historical average for the same market. Flag any market where the current rate is more than 5 percentage points above its historical average as a potential opportunity to negotiate. Output a rate comparison table with strategic implications."

## Affordability Analysis

8. **Personal Affordability Calculator:** "Using my income and debt data from vault/real-estate/00_current/ and current mortgage rate inputs, calculate my maximum affordable purchase price using the 28/36 DTI framework. Front-end DTI: housing costs (PITI — principal, interest, taxes, insurance) must not exceed 28% of gross monthly income. Back-end DTI: total debt obligations must not exceed 36% of gross monthly income. For my current down payment savings, calculate the maximum loan, required monthly payment, and effective purchase price ceiling. If my available down payment is below 20%, calculate the annual PMI cost at 0.85% of the loan and add it to the monthly payment. Output a clean affordability summary with three purchase price scenarios: conservative, target, and stretch."

9. **Buy vs. Rent Break-Even Analysis:** "Read vault/real-estate/00_current/ for my current rent, target market data in vault/real-estate/00_current/, and financial parameters. Calculate the price-to-rent ratio for my target market: divide median purchase price by annual rent for a comparable unit. If the ratio is below 15, buying favors financially; if above 20, renting favors. Then calculate the true break-even horizon: account for closing costs (typically 3–4% of purchase price), annual appreciation assumption, opportunity cost of down payment capital, and tax benefits of mortgage interest deduction. Output the break-even year and a buy/rent recommendation with the key assumptions stated."

10. **Down Payment Runway Analysis:** "Read vault/real-estate/00_current/ for my current down payment savings balance, monthly savings rate, and target down payment percentage (20% to avoid PMI). Calculate: (1) months to reach 20% down payment at current savings rate, (2) months to reach a minimum 10% down payment, (3) the annual PMI cost if I buy at 10% down — using 0.85% of the loan as the PMI rate, and (4) the break-even point where the PMI savings from waiting justify the delay. Flag if reaching 20% down takes more than 36 months — at that point, an earlier purchase with PMI may be financially superior. Output a savings runway table with the optimal entry point highlighted."

11. **Mortgage Payment Sensitivity Table:** "Using the formula that every $10,000 in loan principal costs approximately $50/month at 7% on a 30-year fixed, build a payment sensitivity table across three dimensions: loan amounts from $300k to $700k in $50k increments, interest rates from 6% to 8% in 0.5% steps, and loan terms of 15-year and 30-year. For each scenario show monthly P&I, total interest paid over the loan term, and the interest-to-principal ratio. Highlight the cell matching my current pre-approval terms. Output the table and a one-line summary of how a 1% rate change affects my monthly payment at my target purchase price."

## Listings

12. **Saved Listings Scorecard:** "Read vault/real-estate/00_current/ and score every saved listing on my investment or personal-use criteria. For each listing evaluate: price per square foot vs. market median, days on market relative to area average, price change history (any reductions), estimated property tax burden as a percentage of price, and cap rate if it is an investment property. Assign each listing a composite score from 1–10 and categorize as Hot (score 8–10), Qualified (5–7), or Pass (below 5). Output a ranked scorecard and flag the top 2 listings for immediate action."

13. **Listing Staleness and Leverage Audit:** "Scan vault/real-estate/00_current/ for any listing where days on market exceeds 30 days. Research has shown that seller motivation increases significantly after 30 days, and most agents recommend requesting a 3–5% price concession when DOM exceeds 45 days. For each stale listing calculate: how far below asking a 3% and 5% offer would be in dollar terms, whether a recent price reduction has already occurred, and whether the stale DOM aligns with a broader market cooling trend from vault/real-estate/00_current/. Output a negotiation brief per stale listing with a suggested opening offer range."

14. **New Listing Alert Filter:** "Using my investment criteria from vault/real-estate/00_current/, define the exact filter parameters: target market, price range, asset class, minimum cap rate (6% for stable market, 8% for secondary), maximum price per square foot, and minimum square footage. Scan vault/real-estate/00_current/ for any new additions in the last 7 days that match all hard filters. For those that pass, run a quick 5-minute underwrite: estimated gross rent, vacancy allowance at 7%, operating expense ratio at 40%, NOI, and implied cap rate at asking price. Output a pass/fail summary for each new listing."

## Investment Analysis

15. **Cap Rate and NOI Analysis:** "For a target investment property in vault/real-estate/00_current/, perform a full Net Operating Income analysis. Calculate: gross scheduled rent (annual), vacancy allowance at 7% of gross, effective gross income, operating expenses at 40% of effective gross (excluding mortgage), and NOI. Then calculate cap rate as NOI ÷ purchase price × 100. If the cap rate is below 6% in a stable market or below 8% in a secondary market, flag the deal as below my investment threshold. If above threshold, proceed to cash-on-cash return: annual cash flow after debt service ÷ total cash invested × 100. Target cash-on-cash above 8%. Output a full underwrite table."

16. **Cash-on-Cash Return Sensitivity Analysis:** "Using investment parameters from vault/real-estate/00_current/ and a target property in vault/real-estate/00_current/, run a cash-on-cash return sensitivity analysis. Hold NOI constant and vary: purchase price in 5% increments (from -10% to +10% of asking), down payment from 20% to 30%, and interest rate from 6.5% to 8%. For each combination calculate annual cash flow after debt service and cash-on-cash return. Flag any scenario achieving cash-on-cash above 8% as viable. Output a sensitivity matrix highlighting the viable cells and identify the minimum price reduction or down payment increase needed to make the deal work at current market rates."

17. **1031 Exchange Timeline Tracker:** "If any property sale is in vault/real-estate/00_current/, calculate the critical 1031 exchange deadlines. From the close-of-sale date: Day 45 is the identification deadline — list of replacement properties must be submitted to the qualified intermediary. Day 180 is the close deadline — replacement property purchase must be complete. For each deadline calculate days remaining from today and flag if within 14 days as urgent. Output a 1031 timeline dashboard showing: sale date, identification deadline, close deadline, days remaining for each, and the identified replacement properties if logged. Alert if either deadline is within 30 days."

18. **Rental Property Cash Flow Review:** "Read vault/real-estate/00_current/ for all owned rental properties. For each property calculate the trailing 12-month cash flow: gross rents collected, vacancy rate actual vs. the 7% benchmark, operating expenses actual vs. the 40% benchmark, mortgage payment, and net cash flow. Calculate cash-on-cash return on original equity invested and compare to the 8% target. Flag any property where cash-on-cash has fallen below 6% — these warrant a rent review or refinance analysis. Output a trailing performance table per property and flag properties requiring action."

## Pre-Approval

19. **Pre-Approval Expiry Monitor:** "Read vault/real-estate/00_current/ for my current pre-approval details: lender, approved amount, interest rate locked in (if any), and issue date. Pre-approvals are valid for 90 days — calculate days remaining from the issue date and flag if within 21 days of expiry as Renewal Required, within 30 days as Warning, and beyond 30 days as Active. If expiry is within 30 days and I am actively searching, output a lender outreach template to request renewal. Note that each pre-approval renewal triggers a hard credit inquiry that may reduce my score by approximately 5 points."

20. **Pre-Approval Gap Analysis:** "Compare my current pre-approval amount from vault/real-estate/00_current/ to my target purchase price range. If the pre-approval is below the target range, calculate: the income increase needed to qualify, the debt payoff needed to improve DTI to qualify, and whether a larger down payment would bridge the gap. Also assess whether a co-borrower scenario would change the qualification outcome. If the gap exceeds $75,000, flag it as a significant barrier and output a 90-day action plan to close the gap through debt paydown or savings rate increase."

## Strategy

21. **Buy vs. Continue Renting Decision Framework:** "Using my financial profile from vault/real-estate/00_current/ and market data from vault/real-estate/00_current/, run a structured buy vs. rent decision with 5 weighted factors: (1) Price-to-rent ratio in target market (20% weight), (2) Front-end DTI impact of buying (20%), (3) Break-even horizon in years (20%), (4) Life stability — probability of staying 5+ years (25%), (5) Opportunity cost of down payment capital (15%). Score each factor 1–5 and calculate a weighted composite. A score above 3.5 favors buying; below 2.5 strongly favors renting; in between is situation-dependent. Output the scored matrix and a final recommendation with the top 2 reasons."

22. **Market Selection for My Budget:** "Read vault/real-estate/00_current/ for my confirmed purchase budget and vault/real-estate/00_current/ for all tracked markets. Filter markets where my budget achieves at least a 6% cap rate on a representative investment property or where my budget covers the median 3-bedroom home at a front-end DTI below 28%. Rank the passing markets by: investment yield potential (40%), population and job growth trend (35%), and personal preference or proximity factors from vault/real-estate/00_current/ (25%). Output the top 3 viable markets ranked with score breakdowns and a one-paragraph case for each."

23. **Refinance Opportunity Assessment:** "Read vault/real-estate/00_current/ for any existing mortgages — current rate, original loan balance, remaining term, and years held. For each loan calculate: the interest rate delta between current market rate and my existing rate, the new monthly payment at market rate, monthly savings, closing costs to refinance (estimate 2–3% of remaining balance), and break-even months (closing costs ÷ monthly savings). If break-even is under 24 months and I plan to hold the property for at least 36 more months, flag the loan as a strong refinance candidate. Output a refinance analysis table per mortgage."

## Open Loops

24. **Real Estate Open Loops Triage:** "Scan vault/real-estate/ across all subdirectories for any flagged action items, unresolved analyses, or decision points. Categorize each by type: (1) Pre-approval expiring — needs renewal within 21 days, (2) Stale listing — saved but no update in 30+ days, needs keep or remove decision, (3) Missing underwrite — listing saved but no cap rate or affordability analysis run, (4) Market reassessment due — last market snapshot older than 90 days, (5) 1031 deadline — within 30 days of identification or close deadline. Output a triage list sorted by urgency with estimated resolution time and next action per item."

25. **Real Estate Decision Log:** "Review vault/real-estate/00_current/ for any properties previously analyzed but not pursued. For each declined property log: address, ask price at time of analysis, reason for passing (price, market, DTI, cap rate), and what the property would require today to meet my investment criteria. Compare current market conditions from vault/real-estate/00_current/ against the time of each pass decision — flag any instance where market conditions have changed enough to warrant re-evaluation. This produces a decision learning log to refine future underwriting criteria."
