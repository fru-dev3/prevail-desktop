# AI Ready Life: Chief — Prompt Reference

## Daily Brief

1. **Morning Daily Brief:** "Run my daily brief. Read vault/chief/02_briefs/ for any carry-forward notes from yesterday's brief. Then scan all domain open-loops.md files and surface any item with a deadline within the next 48 hours. Check vault/calendar/00_current/ for any tax, bill payment, or legal deadline due within 5 days and flag at the top. Pull vault/wealth/ for any account movement above $1,000 since yesterday. Scan vault/health/open-loops.md for any overdue medical appointments or lab follow-ups. Identify the top 3 most urgent items across all domains using the priority hierarchy: financial/health/legal deadlines outrank productivity or admin items. Output a morning brief with a prioritized top-3 action list, a financial pulse (any overnight account changes), and a calendar preview of today's commitments."

2. **Top 3 Priorities Today:** "What needs my attention most urgently today? Read all domain open-loops.md files (health, wealth, tax, career, business, brand, content, calendar, chief) and extract every item marked urgent or flagged with a deadline within 72 hours. Score each item using this priority hierarchy: legal and compliance deadlines score highest, then financial deadlines and health flags, then work deliverables, then admin and productivity items. Break ties by deadline proximity — the closer the deadline, the higher the rank. Return exactly the top 3 items with: item description, owning domain, deadline or urgency reason, estimated time to resolve, and one specific first action I can take in the next 15 minutes to make progress."

3. **Cross-Domain Urgent Alerts:** "What is the most urgent item across all my domains right now? Read vault/chief/00_current/ for my current priority list and any standing alerts. Then check vault/tax/ for any tax filing or payment deadline within 30 days, vault/wealth/ for any investment allocation that has drifted more than 5% from target, vault/health/ for any flagged lab result or overdue appointment, vault/calendar/00_current/ for any calendar conflict affecting a hard deadline, and vault/business/00_current/ for any compliance filing due within 30 days. Rank all findings by severity (financial penalty risk, health risk, legal risk, reputational risk) and present the single most urgent item with a full explanation of the consequence of inaction and the recommended next step."

4. **Evening Debrief and Next-Day Setup:** "Run my evening debrief and set me up for tomorrow. Read vault/chief/02_briefs/ for today's morning brief and assess which of the top-3 items were completed, partially completed, or not started — note why if possible. Scan vault/calendar/00_current/ for tomorrow's scheduled commitments and flag any back-to-back meetings that will need buffer time. Pull any new items added to domain open-loops.md files today that I should be aware of before tomorrow. Identify one carry-forward item from today that should be tomorrow's first priority. Output a brief debrief (done/not done on today's top 3), tomorrow's calendar preview, and a next-day priority #1."

## Weekly Preview

5. **Weekly Preview Brief:** "Build my weekly preview for the coming 7 days. Read vault/calendar/00_current/ for all scheduled commitments this week and calculate my meeting load as a percentage of working hours (flag if above 40%). Pull vault/calendar/00_current/ for all deadlines due within the next 7 days and classify each by domain and severity. Check vault/chief/02_briefs/ for any multi-week projects that have a milestone this week. Count open loops per domain using each domain's open-loops.md and flag any domain with more than 5 unresolved items as needing dedicated attention this week. Output a weekly brief with: daily calendar summary, this week's top 3 deadlines, meeting load health indicator, and one focus recommendation for the week."

6. **Weekly Domain Status Sweep:** "What's coming up this week across all of my life domains? Pull the following from each domain vault: any deadline or time-sensitive item due in the next 7 days, any flagged item in that domain's open-loops.md, and any review or recurring task that is due this week (monthly review, bill payment, content publish). Domains to sweep: health, wealth, tax, career, business, brand, content, calendar, estate, insurance, benefits. For each domain with an active item this week, output one bullet summarizing the key action. For domains with no active items, note as clear. Deliver a per-domain weekly status table with domain name, key action this week, and urgency level (high/medium/low)."

7. **This Week's Deadlines Report:** "What deadlines are due this week and what is the consequence of missing each? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ and all domain open-loops.md files for items with a due date within the next 7 days. For each deadline, extract: item name, owning domain, exact due date, estimated time to complete, and the consequence of missing it (financial penalty, compliance violation, relationship damage, or missed opportunity). Sort by consequence severity, then by due date proximity. For any deadline I cannot complete myself this week (due to time constraints), suggest a delegation or deferral option. Output a ranked deadline list with all fields, plus a weekly capacity check: estimated total hours needed versus available working hours."

## System Health

8. **System Health Check:** "Run my system health check across all life domains. For each active domain (health, wealth, tax, career, business, brand, content, calendar, estate, insurance, benefits, chief), check: when the last review was completed (from vault/{domain}/00_current/ or domain state files), how many open loops are currently unresolved, and whether any critical item has been sitting in open loops for more than 21 days. Flag any domain where the last review is more than 30 days ago as overdue. Flag any domain with more than 5 unresolved open loops as overloaded. Output a system health dashboard: one row per domain, with last-review date, open loop count, health status (green/yellow/red), and a recommended action for any red or yellow domain."

9. **Domain Review Cadence Audit:** "Which domains are behind on their scheduled reviews? Read vault/chief/00_current/ for my review cadence schedule (which domains are reviewed monthly, quarterly, or on-demand). Check the last-updated date in each domain's state file and compare to the scheduled cadence. Calculate how many days overdue each review is. Flag any domain where the review is more than 15 days past the scheduled date. Assess whether any domain that is overdue also has a high open loop count, which would indicate compounding neglect risk. Output a cadence compliance table with domain name, scheduled frequency, last review date, days overdue, and a prioritized catch-up sequence for the next two weeks."

10. **Open Loop Count Dashboard:** "Give me a count and severity breakdown of open loops across all my life domains. Read each domain's open-loops.md file and for each domain, count: total open items, items open more than 14 days, items open more than 30 days, and items flagged as urgent or high-priority. Calculate a total cross-domain open loop count. Rank domains by total open loop burden. Flag any domain with more than 3 items open past 30 days as critically backlogged. For the 3 most backlogged domains, extract the 2 oldest items and recommend whether each should be acted on, delegated, or closed as no-longer-relevant. Output a domain-by-domain open loop count table, a total system burden number, and a resolution plan for the top 5 oldest items."

## Cross-Domain Alerts

11. **Critical Alerts Sweep:** "Collect critical alerts from all domains and surface anything I should not let fall through the cracks. Read vault/chief/02_briefs/ for any carry-forward alerts from prior briefs. Then check each domain for items meeting any of these escalation criteria: financial account movement above $5,000, investment allocation drift above 5% from target, any tax deadline within 30 days, any legal or compliance filing within 14 days, any medical flag marked urgent, any overdue invoice above $1,000, or any brand mention marked negative with high reach. For each alert found, include: domain, alert description, severity (critical/high/medium), and the specific consequence of inaction. Output a consolidated alert list sorted by severity."

12. **Cross-Domain Conflict Detection:** "Are there any conflicts between my domains that could create compounding problems? Read vault/calendar/00_current/ and vault/calendar/00_current/ for all commitments and deadlines in the next 30 days. Cross-reference with vault/tax/ for any tax payment or filing deadline in that window, vault/health/ for any scheduled medical appointments, vault/career/ for any work travel or major deliverables, and vault/business/00_current/ for any compliance deadlines. Identify any week where I have three or more high-stakes commitments competing for the same time. Identify any specific conflict: e.g., a travel week that overlaps a tax deadline, a focus sprint that conflicts with a medical appointment. For each conflict, recommend a specific resolution: reschedule, delegate, or front-load."

13. **Overdue Items Across All Domains:** "What items have been open for more than 2 weeks across my entire life system? Read all domain open-loops.md files and extract every item with a creation date more than 14 days ago that is not marked as intentionally deferred. Group results by domain. For each item, assess: is it blocked (waiting on a third party), deferred (intentionally postponed), or overdue (should already be done)? For overdue items, calculate the financial or risk impact of continued inaction. Rank all overdue items by impact. Output a table of all items older than 14 days with domain, item description, age in days, status (blocked/deferred/overdue), and a recommended resolution for the top 10 by impact."

## Domain Status

14. **Full Domain Status Pull:** "Pull status from all active life domains and give me a single consolidated health view. For each domain, read the most current state file and the open-loops.md and extract: the domain's overall status (stable, needs attention, or critical), the count of open loops, the most recently completed action, and the next scheduled review date. Domains to cover: health, wealth, tax, career, business, brand, content, calendar, estate, insurance, benefits, chief. Flag any domain where status is critical or needs attention and surface the single most important item from each flagged domain. Output a 12-domain status table with a color-coded health indicator and a total system health score (number of green domains out of 12)."

15. **Financial Status Summary:** "What is my financial status summary across all wealth, tax, and business domains? Read vault/wealth/ for current investment account balances and any allocation drift beyond 5% from target. Read vault/tax/ for estimated tax payments due in the next 30 days and year-to-date tax payments already made. Read vault/business/00_current/ for this month's business revenue versus goal. Check vault/business/00_current/ for total expenses and net operating income. Sum all income sources and categorize as W-2 employment income, self-employment income, investment income, and rental income if applicable. Flag any financial metric outside its healthy range. Output a one-page financial snapshot with income, expense, net income, investment status, and tax obligation summary."

16. **Health and Wellbeing Status:** "What is my health status summary? Read vault/health/00_current/ for my active health records, any flagged lab results, and the list of active providers. Check vault/health/open-loops.md for any overdue appointments, outstanding lab follow-ups, or unfilled prescriptions. Flag any item marked as requiring follow-up within 30 days. Identify any preventive care item (annual physical, dental, vision, age-appropriate screenings) that is more than 1 year overdue based on the last-completed dates in vault/health/. Cross-reference with vault/benefits/ to confirm my insurance coverage is current and active. Output a health status brief with a green/yellow/red indicator per category (preventive care, specialist follow-up, prescriptions, mental wellness) and a prioritized next-step list."

## Priorities

17. **Quarterly Priority Progress Check:** "Am I focused on the right priorities this quarter and how are they progressing? Read vault/chief/00_current/ for my stated quarterly priorities and their success metrics. Then read each owning domain's state file to find evidence of progress against each priority. For each quarterly priority, assess: what specific progress was made in the last 30 days, whether it is on track (yes/no), what the main obstacle is if off-track, and what the single most important next action is. Calculate a progress score for each priority: percentage of planned milestones completed to date. Flag any priority that is below 40% completion at the midpoint of the quarter. Output a priority tracker with progress score, on-track status, main obstacle, and next action per priority."

18. **Quarterly Goal at Risk Assessment:** "What quarterly goal is most at risk and what can I do about it? Read vault/chief/00_current/ for all active quarterly goals and their measurable targets. Pull progress data from each owning domain's state file and calculate the percent complete for each goal. Identify the goal with the lowest progress-to-timeline ratio (percent complete divided by percent of quarter elapsed). For that at-risk goal, analyze: what caused the delay, what the cost of not achieving it is (financial, career, health, or personal impact), and what a realistic recovery plan looks like for the remaining time in the quarter. Output a risk assessment for the most at-risk goal with a root cause analysis, impact statement, and a 3-step recovery plan."

## Review Brief

19. **Chief Monthly Review Brief:** "Run my chief monthly review brief. Read vault/chief/02_briefs/ for any prior month's carry-forward items. Then aggregate monthly performance data from each domain: brand channel growth, business revenue vs. goal, content publishing cadence, calendar commitment vs. execution rate, and any health or financial flags that arose. Assess each domain using a green/yellow/red status based on whether it hit its monthly targets. Count total new items added to the system this month versus total items resolved, to compute a net open loop trend (am I getting ahead or falling behind?). Identify the single best month-over-month improvement and the single most disappointing result. Output a monthly review brief with domain scorecards, a net open loop trend, and 3 system-level adjustments for next month."

20. **Cross-Domain Synthesis:** "Give me a cross-domain synthesis of the most important signals from the last 30 days. Read the state files from all active domains and identify: the top 3 wins (moments where a domain performed above expectation), the top 3 risks (domains or items where something is trending the wrong direction), and the top 1 cross-domain insight (a pattern that spans two or more domains). For example: a connection between a busy travel schedule (calendar) and missed content publishing (content), or a tax deadline (tax) that coincides with a peak revenue period (business). Synthesize these into a 500-word narrative report with an executive summary, wins, risks, and the cross-domain insight section. End with 3 prioritized system recommendations."

21. **Life System Overall State:** "What is the overall state of my life system? Read vault/chief/00_current/ for my active priorities, my system review cadence, and any standing commitments I have made to myself. Pull a health indicator from each domain's open-loops.md and state file. Calculate: total open loops system-wide, number of domains in green vs. yellow vs. red status, number of overdue reviews, and progress against my top 3 quarterly goals. Assess the overall system direction — is my life system becoming more organized and less reactive, or are open loops accumulating faster than they are being resolved? Provide an overall system health score (0–10) with the reasoning behind it, and 3 specific system changes that would most improve that score over the next 60 days."
