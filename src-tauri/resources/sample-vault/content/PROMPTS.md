# AI Ready Life: Content — Prompt Reference

## Weekly & Monthly Reviews

1. **Content Weekly Review:** "Run my content weekly review. Read vault/content/00_current/ for this week's planned publishing commitments and check which items were actually published on time versus missed or delayed. Flag any active platform where no content was published in the last 14 days — this is a cadence break that risks algorithmic suppression. Check vault/content/00_current/ for any post published this week and extract early engagement signals: views in the first 24 hours, CTR if available, and comments within the first 48 hours. Compare to my rolling 4-week average for early engagement. Identify the one piece of content that overperformed this week and the one that underperformed. Output a weekly review brief with a cadence health check, early engagement table, and 2 specific actions for next week's content."

2. **Content Monthly Review:** "Run my content monthly review. Read vault/content/00_current/ for the trailing 30 days and extract: YouTube channel CTR (benchmark: 4–8% is healthy), average view duration as a percentage of video length (benchmark: above 40% is good), subscriber growth rate (flag if below 1%/month), newsletter open rate (benchmark: above 35%), newsletter click rate (benchmark: above 3%), newsletter unsubscribe rate (flag if above 0.3%), and Gumroad monthly revenue with conversion rate. Cross-reference with vault/content/00_current/ to assess whether publishing cadence was maintained across all active platforms. Flag any metric that fell outside its benchmark range. Output a monthly content brief with a per-platform scorecard, a revenue summary, and 3 strategic recommendations for next month."

3. **Content Business Performance Brief:** "How is my content business performing this month? Read vault/content/00_current/ for all revenue and engagement data this month. Aggregate: total YouTube ad revenue, Gumroad product revenue by product, newsletter-driven revenue (affiliate or product referrals), and any sponsorship income. Compare total content revenue to last month and to my monthly revenue goal from vault/content/00_current/. Calculate the revenue split by channel (YouTube vs. Gumroad vs. other). Read vault/content/00_current/ for publishing output this month and calculate content produced versus planned. Compute a content business efficiency ratio: revenue generated per piece of content published. Output a one-page business performance brief with revenue by channel, total revenue vs. goal, efficiency ratio, and the top 3 growth levers for next month."

## YouTube

4. **YouTube Channel Performance Report:** "How is my YouTube channel performing this month? Read vault/content/00_current/ for the current month's YouTube data and extract: total views, average CTR (benchmark: 4–8%), average view duration percentage (benchmark: above 40%), net new subscribers, subscriber growth rate (benchmark: above 1%/month), and top 5 videos by views. For each top video, note its topic, thumbnail style, and title structure. Benchmark all metrics against my own trailing 3-month average to identify meaningful trend changes. Flag any metric that has declined more than 15% month-over-month versus the prior 3-month average. Identify the one video with the highest CTR and the one with the highest average view duration and analyze what they have in common. Output a channel health scorecard with all benchmarks and a recommended focus for next month."

5. **Top Videos Analysis:** "What are my top videos by views this month and what can I learn from them? Read vault/content/00_current/ for YouTube data and extract the top 5 videos by view count in the trailing 30 days. For each video, record: title, views, CTR, average view duration %, subscriber conversion rate (subscribers gained divided by views), and publish date. Identify patterns across the top performers: common topic categories, title formulas (list, how-to, question, comparison), thumbnail style (face vs. no face, text overlay amount), and video length (short under 8 min, medium 8–15 min, long above 15 min). Compare these patterns to my bottom 5 videos by views and identify the key differentiators. Output a comparative analysis with the top 5 video metrics table and a 3-point formula for my next high-performing video."

6. **Subscriber Growth Trend Review:** "What is my subscriber growth trend and am I on track for my annual goal? Read vault/content/00_current/ for weekly subscriber counts over the past 12 weeks. Calculate week-over-week subscriber growth rate and the 4-week rolling average growth rate. Compare to the 1%/month healthy benchmark and to my stated annual subscriber goal in vault/content/00_current/. Project the current growth rate forward to estimate where I will land at year end, and calculate the required weekly growth rate to hit my annual goal on time. Identify the 2-week period with the highest subscriber growth and what content drove it. Flag if current trajectory puts me more than 20% below my annual goal. Output a subscriber growth chart in prose, a goal-projection table, and 2 growth acceleration tactics."

## Newsletter

7. **Newsletter Performance Analysis:** "Analyze my last 4 newsletter issues and surface what is working. Read vault/content/00_current/ for the metrics on the last 4 newsletter sends: issue name, send date, subject line, list size, open rate (benchmark: above 35%), click rate (benchmark: above 3%), and unsubscribe rate (flag if above 0.3% for any issue). Calculate my 4-issue rolling average for each metric. Identify the issue with the highest open rate and analyze: was it the subject line, the send day, the topic, or the list segment? Identify the issue with the highest click rate and analyze what the call-to-action structure looked like. Flag any issue where the unsubscribe rate spiked and assess whether the topic or tone may have contributed. Output a metrics table for all 4 issues and a 3-point formula for improving the next issue's performance."

8. **Newsletter Subscriber Growth Check:** "How are my newsletter subscribers trending and am I growing the list fast enough? Read vault/content/00_current/ for subscriber count data over the past 8 weeks. Calculate net new subscribers per week (gross new minus unsubscribes), the 4-week rolling average growth rate, and total list size trend. Compare growth rate to my stated newsletter goal in vault/content/00_current/ and calculate whether I am on track to hit the target by the deadline. Identify the week with the highest subscriber growth and what drove it (e.g., a YouTube video that mentioned the newsletter, a specific lead magnet, a LinkedIn post with a subscribe CTA). Flag any 2-week period where net subscribers were negative (unsubscribes exceeded new subscribers). Output a growth trend table, goal tracking summary, and 2 list-building tactics to test next month."

9. **Best-Performing Newsletter Issue Deep Dive:** "What was my best-performing newsletter issue in the last 90 days and why did it work? Read vault/content/00_current/ for all newsletter issues published in the past 90 days. Rank them by a composite performance score: weighted average of open rate (40%), click rate (40%), and low unsubscribe rate (20%). Select the top-ranked issue and perform a deep analysis: subject line structure (length, use of numbers or questions, personalization), topic category, email length (word count), number of links and CTAs, send day and time, and whether a new lead magnet was promoted. Compare each of these variables to the bottom-ranked issue from the same period. Identify the 3 variables that most distinguish the top performer. Output a winner profile and 3 specific elements to replicate in future issues."

## Revenue

10. **Monthly Revenue Summary:** "Build my revenue summary for this month across all content channels. Read vault/content/00_current/ for this month's revenue data and extract: YouTube ad revenue (RPM rate and total), Gumroad revenue by product (units sold, average order value, total revenue), newsletter-related revenue (affiliate commissions, sponsored issue payments), and any course or consulting revenue. Sum all sources into a total content revenue figure. Compare to last month and to my monthly revenue goal. Calculate the revenue contribution percentage of each channel. Identify the single highest-revenue activity and assess whether I am investing proportional time in it relative to its contribution. Output a revenue breakdown table by channel, a total vs. goal comparison, and a revenue mix analysis with a recommendation on where to focus more energy."

11. **Gumroad Product Performance Review:** "How are my Gumroad products performing and where is my conversion rate losing customers? Read vault/content/00_current/ for Gumroad data: total store visits this month, unique product page views per product, number of sales per product, conversion rate per product (sales divided by page views, flag if below 2%), average order value, refund rate per product (flag if above 5%), and total monthly Gumroad revenue. Identify my highest-converting product and my lowest-converting product. For the lowest-converting product, assess potential causes: is the product page description compelling, is the price point appropriate, is the product well-suited to my current audience? Calculate total revenue by product and rank the product catalog by revenue generated. Output a product performance table and 3 specific optimization recommendations for the lowest-converting product."

12. **Revenue Goal Tracking:** "How am I tracking against my monthly content revenue goal and what will close the gap? Read vault/content/00_current/ for my stated monthly revenue target across all content channels. Read vault/content/00_current/ for all revenue received and recognized this month through today's date. Calculate: total revenue to date, revenue required to hit goal by month end, daily run rate needed versus current run rate, and the revenue gap in dollars. Break the gap down by channel — how much would each channel need to increase to close the gap? Identify the single fastest path to closing the revenue gap this month: promote a specific Gumroad product, publish a YouTube video optimized for high RPM keywords, or send a dedicated newsletter with a product CTA. Output a gap analysis table and a specific 3-action plan to close the gap."

## SEO

13. **SEO Gap Analysis:** "What are my SEO gaps and where am I leaving organic traffic on the table? Read vault/content/00_current/ for Google Search Console data: impressions by query, average position, and CTR for the past 30 days. Identify all queries where I rank between positions 5 and 15 (high opportunity zone — one rank improvement can double traffic). Flag any query in the opportunity zone where my CTR is below 3% (suggesting a title or meta description issue). Identify any high-volume query (above 100 impressions per month) where I have no content targeting that keyword directly. Read vault/content/00_current/ for my top 5 target keywords and check whether each is in my top 20 ranking queries. Output a prioritized SEO opportunity table with query, current position, impressions, CTR, and recommended action (optimize title, build supporting content, or create new page)."

14. **Keyword Targeting Strategy Review:** "What keywords should I be targeting and am I building content around the right ones? Read vault/content/00_current/ for my defined content niche and target audience. Read vault/content/00_current/ for my current top 20 queries by impressions from Google Search Console. Identify the 5 highest-potential keywords I am not yet ranking in the top 10 for — high-potential means high search relevance to my niche, moderate to high monthly search volume, and a current ranking between positions 11 and 30 (reachable with targeted content). For each of the 5 gap keywords, recommend a specific content piece (YouTube video, newsletter issue, or blog post) that could realistically move me into the top 10 within 90 days. Output a keyword opportunity table and 5 specific content briefs tied to the gap keywords."

15. **SEO Monthly Report:** "Build my SEO summary for this month and compare it to last month. Read vault/content/00_current/ for Google Search Console data for the current month and the prior month. Extract: total impressions, total clicks, average position across all queries, and CTR. Calculate month-over-month change for each metric. Identify the top 5 queries by click growth (largest increase in clicks month-over-month) — these are emerging traffic drivers. Identify the top 5 queries by impression loss — these may indicate ranking drops requiring immediate attention. Check whether any of my top 5 target keywords from vault/content/00_current/ declined in average position this month. Output a monthly SEO comparison table, a top-5 emerging queries list, a top-5 declining queries alert, and 2 specific optimization actions."

## Publishing Schedule

16. **Publishing Cadence Check:** "Am I publishing consistently across all active platforms and is my cadence sustainable? Read vault/content/00_current/ for my committed publishing cadence on each platform (e.g., 1 YouTube video per week, 1 newsletter per week, 3 LinkedIn posts per week). Then read vault/content/00_current/ for actual publish dates over the past 6 weeks to calculate the actual publishing frequency per platform. Flag any platform where more than 2 weeks have passed since the last publish — this is a cadence break. Calculate my on-time publishing rate (scheduled posts published on time divided by total scheduled posts) for the past 6 weeks. Identify the platform where I am most inconsistent and assess whether the cadence target is realistic given my current bandwidth. Output a cadence compliance table and a recommendation to either protect the current cadence or adjust it to something sustainable."

17. **Content Due This Week:** "What content is due this week and is everything on track? Read vault/content/00_current/ for all content items scheduled for publication this week, including the platform, format, topic, and current production status. For each item, assess whether it is: ready to publish, in production (draft exists), or not yet started. Flag any item scheduled to publish within 48 hours that is not yet in a ready state. Calculate the total content production hours needed to complete all outstanding items this week and compare to available time. For any at-risk item (not started and publishing within 3 days), recommend: delay the publish date, simplify the content format, or repurpose existing content to fill the slot. Output a content due-this-week tracker with status, risk flag, and recommended action per item."

18. **Content Calendar Review:** "Review my content calendar for the next 4 weeks and identify any gaps or over-clustering. Read vault/content/00_current/ for all planned content over the next 28 days. For each week, check: does each active platform have at least one scheduled piece, are there any weeks with more than 3 pieces scheduled on the same day (publishing over-clustering), and are there any weeks with zero content scheduled on any active platform? Flag gaps (any active platform with no scheduled content in a 14-day window) and flag over-clustered days. Cross-reference topics planned for different platforms in the same week and flag any where I'm missing a repurposing opportunity (e.g., a YouTube video that could become a newsletter summary that week). Output a 4-week calendar visualization in text with gap and clustering flags, plus repurposing opportunities."

## Analytics

19. **Channel Conversion Analysis:** "What content is driving the most conversions from audience to customer? Read vault/content/00_current/ for conversion data across all channels. For YouTube: identify which videos drove the most clicks to my Gumroad products or newsletter sign-up links using end screen or description link CTR. For newsletters: identify which issues had the highest link click rate and which links were clicked most. For LinkedIn: identify posts that included a CTA and track profile or link visits resulting from them. For Gumroad: note whether any product spike correlates with a specific piece of content published that week. Rank all content by conversion efficiency (conversions generated per piece) rather than by views or reach. Output a top-10 content pieces by conversion with the conversion action, number, and the content format and topic pattern that drove it."

20. **Cross-Channel Engagement Comparison:** "Which of my channels has the best engagement rate and what does the top channel do differently? Read vault/content/00_current/ for the past 30 days of engagement data across all active content channels. Calculate engagement rate per channel: for YouTube (comments + likes + shares divided by views), for newsletter (reply rate + click rate), for LinkedIn (reactions + comments + shares divided by impressions). Benchmark each against healthy thresholds: YouTube engagement above 4% is strong, newsletter click rate above 3%, LinkedIn engagement above 2%. Rank channels by engagement rate. For the top-ranked channel, extract the top 3 content posts by engagement and identify the common characteristics (topic, format, length, CTA style). Assess whether those characteristics can be translated to the lowest-ranked channel. Output a cross-channel engagement table and a 2-tactic translation plan."

21. **Content Repurposing Opportunity Map:** "Map my content repurposing opportunities for this month to maximize reach from every piece I create. Read vault/content/00_current/ for all content planned this month and read vault/content/00_current/ for my top 5 performing pieces from the last 60 days. For each planned YouTube video, identify what it could become: a newsletter summary (pull the key takeaways into a 300-word digest), a LinkedIn post (distill the single most valuable insight into a hook-driven text post), and a Twitter/X thread (break the structure into 5–7 numbered points). For each top-performing past piece, check whether all three repurposed formats were published. Calculate my current repurposing rate: pieces repurposed into at least 2 formats divided by total pieces published. Output a repurposing plan for each planned video this month plus a current repurposing rate and a target of 80% repurposing coverage."

For items open more than 30 days, recommend a decision: execute this week, delegate, defer to next quarter, or close as no-longer-relevant. Output a triage table sorted by category risk and age, with a recommended action and target completion date for the top 5 items."
