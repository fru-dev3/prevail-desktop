## Prompt

I run a 6-person consulting firm. I need senior front-end capacity. Should I hire a full-time engineer at $180k base, or keep using a $200/hr contractor for the next year?

## Context

- Current contractor utilization: ~30 hours/week, billing me ~$320k/yr
- Sales pipeline: confident in 18+ months of comparable demand
- The contractor is great but has signaled she's going to take a full-time role somewhere within 6 months
- Hiring a full-timer: ~$180k base + ~$45k benefits/payroll tax = $225k all-in
- I bill the front-end work out at ~$280/hr; a full-timer at 1,800 productive hrs/yr is ~$504k of billable capacity
- Onboarding ramp: about 2 months before they're net-productive

## Notes

Real decision: **hire full-time**. The contractor at $320k delivers 1,560 hrs/yr (30 × 52). The full-timer at $225k all-in delivers ~1,800 productive hrs (after the 2-month ramp). On gross margin: full-timer is 55% cheaper per productive hour, generating roughly $280k more annual contribution at the same demand level. With 18+ months of pipeline confidence the payback period on the ramp and the FTE risk is under 4 months. The contractor's signaled departure also removes the "stable contractor option" entirely, the real choice is full-time hire vs scramble. A good answer computes the per-hour cost gap, names the 18-month pipeline as the precondition that makes the hire safe, and flags the contractor's departure signal. A bad answer either picks contractor for "flexibility" without computing the cost gap, or picks full-time without naming pipeline as the precondition.
