## Prompt

We have a 30-year mortgage at 6.8%. Should we make extra principal payments to pay it down faster, or invest that surplus in the market?

## Context

Home equity is ~$10,800 (early in the mortgage). S&P 500 historical average return ~10%/year. No PMI. Tax deduction on mortgage interest is limited due to standard deduction.

## Notes

A good answer should recommend investing the surplus over extra mortgage payments when the expected market return (10%) exceeds the mortgage rate (6.8% post-tax), acknowledge the risk/certainty tradeoff, and note the psychological value of debt paydown as a secondary consideration.
