## Prompt

Read the attached year-end tax packet (preliminary 1040 summary, W-2, 1099-DIV, current brokerage positions). What three moves should I make before December 31, 2026?

## Notes

The packet is designed so a competent reader catches FOUR specific opportunities and ONE trap:

**Opportunities:**

1. **Tax-loss harvest** ~$56k of losses: META (-$34k), BLUE (-$16k), BND (-$6k). Combined with the unused $50k carryforward, can offset future capital gains without paying tax until 2031+.

2. **Mega-backdoor Roth** ~$45k of after-tax 401k space available, can be converted to Roth in-plan. This is free Roth space the employer's plan supports — most people don't know it exists.

3. **DEFER the NVDA sale to January 2027.** Selling NVDA in December crystallizes $114k of long-term gain in 2026; deferring 30 days pushes the tax liability to 2027 and gives 12 extra months of compounding on the deferred $17k of tax.

4. **The harvested losses + carryforward absorb the realized gain.** $84k of unrealized VTI gain stays unrealized (no action). The $24,400 already realized + $4,200 cap gain distributions get offset by harvested losses + carryforward.

**The trap:** Doing a Roth conversion at the same time. The packet's AGI is already $383k. A $50k conversion would push into the 32% bracket AND cross the IRMAA tier ($206k MAGI for joint, but actually look-back period — at $400k+ MAGI, IRMAA surcharge on Medicare premiums in 2028 would add $4,000+/yr). Roth conversions should be done in a LOWER-income year, not when already in the 24% bracket near the 32% ceiling.

A good answer:
- Names tax-loss harvest specifically (META, BLUE, BND)
- Names mega-backdoor Roth specifically
- Defers the NVDA sale rather than executing in 2026
- Explicitly REJECTS a Roth conversion this year (IRMAA / bracket reasoning)
- Bonus: notes that the qualified dividends ($9,100) get LTCG treatment (15%), not ordinary (24%)

A bad answer:
- Recommends a Roth conversion (most common trap — looks "tax-efficient" but isn't)
- Misses one or more of the three real moves
- Doesn't notice the $50k carryforward
- Hedges with "consult your tax advisor" without naming the specific moves
