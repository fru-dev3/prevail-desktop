## Prompt

An insurance agent is recommending a $1M whole-life policy at $9,800/yr. A comparable 20-year term policy is $850/yr. Which should I get?

## Context

- I'm 40, in good health, married, two kids ages 8 and 11
- Spouse earns ~40% of household income
- Household has $400k in retirement, $80k in liquid savings, 4 years left on mortgage
- The youngest kid will be 31 in 20 years — fully independent by then
- The agent's pitch on whole life: "guaranteed return, tax-advantaged growth"

## Notes

Real decision: **term**, invest the $8,950/yr difference. Whole-life policies show "guaranteed returns" of 3-4% IRR over a 20-30 year hold — and that's AFTER the first 5-10 years of mostly commission cost where the cash value is near zero. Investing the $8,950/yr delta in a low-cost index fund at 7% real over 20 years compounds to ~$390k, vs whole-life cash value at the same point of ~$150k. Insurance need also drops sharply once kids are independent and mortgage is paid — term aligns coverage with actual need. A good answer leads with term, computes the spread on the premium delta over the term, and flags that the "guaranteed return" framing hides the commission load. A bad answer either entertains the whole-life pitch as a legitimate investment, or recommends "blend both" without rejecting whole life as wrong for this profile.
