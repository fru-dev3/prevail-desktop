## Prompt

I've been at my current company 6 years, senior engineer, comp $310k all-in. A Series B startup is offering $250k base + 0.4% equity with a 4-year vest. Should I leave?

## Context

- Current role: stable but flat, last promotion 2.5 years ago, manager has been honest that the next promotion isn't on the near-term plan
- Startup: 80 people, $4M MRR growing 18% MoM, just closed $40M Series B at $250M post
- I would join as a tech lead with two reports
- Equity math: 0.4% of $250M = $1M paper value, but obviously discount heavily for outcome probability
- Liquid savings: 18 months of expenses
- Partner is supportive; no near-term major financial obligations

## Notes

Real decision: **leave**. The current role is locked — manager confirmed no near-term promotion path, and 2.5 years post-promotion at a steady-state company means the marginal year there mostly compounds risk of complacency rather than building career capital. The Series B has a 30-40% chance of a meaningful liquidity event over the vest based on growth + raise quality, expected value of the equity is positive even after heavy discounting, and the tech lead role with reports rebuilds promotability whether or not the equity hits. 18 months of runway means a downside scenario (startup folds in year 2) is recoverable. A good answer names the optionality argument, applies a probability discount to the equity, and ties the decision to the 18-month runway. A bad answer either says "stay because base comp is higher" without weighing optionality, or "leave for the equity" without discounting it.
