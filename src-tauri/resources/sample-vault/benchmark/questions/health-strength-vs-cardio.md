## Prompt

I'm 42, deskbound, average fitness, no injuries. I have about 4 hours a week to train. Should I prioritize strength or cardio?

## Context

- Goal: live well into my 80s with full mobility and independence
- Family history: father had a hip replacement at 68, mostly due to muscle loss + falls
- Current bodyfat: ~22%, mildly elevated
- I dislike running but can tolerate cycling and rowing
- Have access to a basic gym at home (barbell, dumbbells, rower)

## Notes

Real decision: **prioritize strength**, hit the WHO cardio baseline as the secondary goal. Sarcopenia (age-related muscle loss) is the single biggest predictor of late-life independence, strength work directly counters it, while cardio doesn't. The strength benefit is also concentrated in the first 2 sessions per week; beyond that, marginal returns fall. So the optimal allocation in 4 hours: 2-3 sessions of full-body strength at 45-60 min, then 1-2 cardio sessions of 30-45 min to hit ~150 min/wk moderate intensity. A good answer leads with strength, cites the sarcopenia argument given the family history, and names the WHO 150 min/wk threshold for cardio. A bad answer either treats both as equal ("balance is best") or recommends cardio-first because it's more obviously linked to mortality stats without engaging the late-life independence angle.
