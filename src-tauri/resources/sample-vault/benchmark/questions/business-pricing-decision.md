## Prompt

Read the attached pricing analysis. The board wants to know whether to raise the Enterprise tier 25% starting January 1, 2027. Should we, and how?

## Notes

The packet is designed so a competent reader sees four things:

**1. Yes, raise, but tie it to value (the SLA).** Customer interviews said two Enterprise customers would pay 30-40% more for a guaranteed 99.9% uptime SLA. Raising price alone reads as a money-grab; raising price + adding a specific value (SLA) gets approved with much less friction.

**2. Don't break existing Q1 renewals.** Three Enterprise renewals are in Q1: Acme Foods ($9.6k), Ledger Insurance ($14k), Pinetree ($18k). Hitting them with a 25% increase mid-relationship is the highest-churn path. **Grandfather them at current rates** through the Q1 cycle; apply the new pricing to new sales immediately, and to renewals starting Q2.

**3. Drop Pinetree.** They're 67 days late on the last 3 invoices. The pricing-raise conversation with them is actually an exit conversation. Either accept payment-on-renewal at the OLD rate (cash positive) or refuse renewal.

**4. The CFO's churn concern is overblown for Enterprise.** Enterprise LTV/CAC is 12+. Customer interviews explicitly signaled willingness to pay more. The real churn risk is at Pro tier from Competitor 1's aggressive discounting, but the question is about Enterprise.

**Numbers the answer should engage:**
- 6 Enterprise customers × 25% × $9,600 avg = +$14k incremental ARR. Modest absolute number, but Enterprise represents the 73% gross margin tier, every dollar here is high-quality revenue.
- Net retention 108% means current pricing is sticky; people aren't downgrading. There's headroom.
- AR aging concern (Pinetree) is a SEPARATE problem from pricing; the pricing question shouldn't blur them.

A good answer:
- Says YES with conditions (raise + SLA bundle)
- Grandfathers Q1 renewals OR delays effective date to Q2
- Explicitly addresses Pinetree as a separate AR problem
- Cites the customer-interview signal (two said they'd pay more)
- Quantifies the upside (~$14k incremental ARR, high-margin)

A bad answer:
- Recommends "raise 25% effective Jan 1 for everyone" without grandfathering
- Hedges on whether to raise at all
- Treats the Pinetree AR problem as part of the pricing decision rather than separate
- Doesn't bundle the SLA, leaves the increase looking like pure price extraction
