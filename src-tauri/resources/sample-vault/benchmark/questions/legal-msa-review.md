## Prompt

Read the attached draft Master Services Agreement. As the consultant signing this, what clauses should I push back on? Rank them by how much they put me at risk.

## Notes

The MSA is designed so a competent reader catches FIVE specific issues, ranked by actual risk to the consultant:

**1. Uncapped indemnification (Section 6.1)**, Highest risk. Consultant indemnifies for IP infringement, negligence, breach, and tax obligations, with NO cap. A single IP claim from a third party could wipe out the consultant's entire net worth. Standard pushback: cap at 1-2x annual fees, exclude third-party IP claims that arise from Client's instructions or materials.

**2. Asymmetric liability cap (Section 7.2 vs 6.2)**, Highest risk after #1. Client's liability is capped at 3 months of fees. Consultant's indemnification is uncapped. The asymmetry alone is a deal-breaker; combined with #1 it's the largest single financial exposure in the document.

**3. 18-month non-compete (Section 9)**, High risk. 18 months is overbroad in most jurisdictions and Delaware has been narrowing enforceability. As written it could prevent the consultant from working for any company in any industry the client operates in. Push to: 6 months, narrowly defined to direct competitors only, or strike entirely (in CA/MN/MA non-competes are largely unenforceable for contractors).

**4. 60-day payment terms (Section 2)**, High operational risk. 60 days is at the long end; common is net-30. For a solo consultant this is a 60-day interest-free loan to the client. Push to net-30 with 1.5% interest on late payments.

**5. Unilateral termination for convenience (Section 5.2)**, Asymmetric. Client can terminate on 14 days notice for ANY reason; Consultant has NO equivalent right. Push for: either both parties have it (mutual), OR raise notice to 30 days, OR require client to pay through end of current month + 30 days.

**Also worth flagging (lower priority but real):**
- Pre-existing IP license (Section 3.2) is perpetual + sublicensable. Consultant loses control of their methodologies forever once any client uses them. Push for: license limited to Work Product use only, no sublicensing.
- Equitable relief without bond (Section 11) is one-sided, Client doesn't have to post bond for an injunction. Push to mutual.

A good answer:
- Identifies indemnification + asymmetric cap as the top financial risks (#1 + #2)
- Names the non-compete as overbroad with specific suggested narrowing
- Notes payment terms are at the long end
- Catches the unilateral termination asymmetry
- Bonus: catches the pre-existing IP sublicense problem

A bad answer:
- Reads the document at face value without flagging the asymmetry between sections
- Treats non-compete as "standard" without engaging Delaware/jurisdictional reality
- Misses indemnification or focuses only on the per-section liability cap without seeing the uncapped indemnification
- Says "have a lawyer review this" without listing the specific clauses
