## Prompt

Do I need umbrella liability insurance? I have $400k in liquid assets and a $1.2M home.

## Context

- Annual household income: $310k
- Existing auto liability: $300k/accident
- Existing homeowners liability: $500k
- Family activities: two kids in youth sports, occasional pool guests, dog (large breed, no bite history)
- Umbrella quotes I've gotten: $400-500/yr for $2M of coverage on top of existing policies

## Notes

Real decision: **buy the $2M umbrella now**. With $1.6M+ of attachable net worth (home equity + savings + future income), the existing auto + home liability limits ($300k-$500k) leave a 6-figure gap that a single bad accident — car crash with injuries, kid's pool guest with a head injury, dog bite, anything — could pierce. At $400/yr for $2M of coverage, the cost is asymptotic to free relative to the protected asset base. The standard rule of thumb is umbrella coverage at or above net worth; $2M is the right tier for this profile. A good answer recommends buying, names the exposure-vs-cost asymmetry, and computes the gap between existing liability limits and net worth. A bad answer either equivocates ("depends on your risk tolerance" — no, the math is clear) or recommends a lower tier without addressing the net worth.
