## Prompt

We've been renting in our current city for 3 years. Our rent is $4,200/mo. We could buy a comparable house for $850k. Should we buy?

## Context

- 20% down ($170k) is what we have in liquid savings
- Mortgage rates today: 7.1% on 30-year fixed
- Property tax in this city: 1.8%, insurance: 0.5%, maintenance budget: 1%
- Career: at least one of us may relocate for work in the next 3-5 years
- Two kids, ages 5 and 8 — current school district is great
- Rent has risen ~4%/yr the last two years

## Notes

Real decision: **keep renting**. At 7.1% + 1.8% tax + 0.5% insurance + 1% maintenance, the all-in cost of ownership is ~10.4% of $850k = ~$88k/yr, versus rent at $50k/yr. The $38k annual delta plus the $170k down payment invested at 7% real over 5 years materially beats expected price appreciation in most markets at current rates. Breakeven on transaction costs (6% sell-side + 2% buy-side ~ $68k) takes 7+ years even ignoring the spread. With a 3-5 year relocation possibility, the math gets worse. A good answer names the all-in carrying cost vs rent, notes the 5-7 year breakeven on transaction costs, and ties the decision to the relocation risk. A bad answer pushes "buy because renting is throwing away money" without the math.
