## Prompt

I run a profitable 6-person consulting firm and I'm 5 years into it. I have a SaaS product idea that's been nagging at me for 2 years. Do I keep the firm and add the product on the side, fully transition to the product, or stay focused on the firm?

## Context

- Consulting firm: 6 people, ~$1.6M ARR, ~$280k take-home for me as principal, steady but not growing
- SaaS product idea: vertical workflow tool for a niche I deeply understand, no validated customers yet
- Liquid runway if I stopped earning today: 18 months personal
- I'm 42; energy and learning capacity still high but recognizably finite
- Family: spouse earns ~$140k, two kids 8 and 11
- The thing that has been nagging me: I haven't built anything new in 3 years and I notice the boredom

## Notes

Real decision: **partial transition** — cut to 3 client days/week, invest 2 days/week in the product for 18 months with explicit kill criteria. Going all-in on an unvalidated SaaS at 42 with two kids and a profitable firm to wind down is a high-variance bet with too much downside given the runway. Staying status quo ignores the signal that the firm has stopped compounding for the principal. The right move buys optionality: keep enough client work to keep the lights on, get serious enough time at the product to actually ship, and pre-commit to a decision date (18 months) or success criterion ($30k MRR) where you either lean in further or shut it down. A good answer names the partial-transition path, attaches an explicit kill criterion, and flags that the firm has stopped compounding for the principal as the reason status quo is wrong. A bad answer either takes the bold "go all-in" path without naming the runway risk or recommends "stay focused" without engaging the boredom signal.
