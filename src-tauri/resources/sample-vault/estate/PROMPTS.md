# AI Ready Life: Estate — Prompt Reference

## Monthly Reviews

1. **Estate Monthly Review:** "Run my full estate monthly review using data from vault/estate/00_current/, vault/estate/00_current/, vault/estate/00_current/, and vault/estate/00_current/. For each property, compute gross scheduled income, effective gross income (vacancy at 5%), net operating income, net cash flow after debt service, cap rate, and cash-on-cash return. Summarize tenant payment status, flag any leases expiring within 90 days, list all maintenance completed and pending, and check insurance renewal dates. Output a structured briefing with one section per domain (cash flow, tenants, maintenance, insurance) and close with a ranked action-item list."

2. **Estate Brief:** "Generate a concise estate brief from vault/estate/00_current/ covering the key metrics I need in under 5 minutes: total portfolio gross income this month, total net cash flow, any properties with negative cash flow or cash-on-cash return below 6%, any overdue rent, and the single most urgent action item across the portfolio. Format as a short executive summary — no tables, bullet points only, maximum 300 words."

3. **Monthly Action Items:** "Pull all open loops, pending maintenance items, and expiring documents across vault/estate/00_current/, vault/estate/00_current/, and vault/estate/00_current/. Sort items by urgency: overdue first, then due within 7 days, then due within 30 days. For each item include the property address, description of the action, deadline, and consequence of missing it. Output a numbered checklist."

## Portfolio Summary

4. **Full Portfolio Summary:** "Build a complete portfolio summary from vault/estate/00_current/. For each property show: address, acquisition date, purchase price, current estimated market value, outstanding mortgage balance, equity (value minus loan balance), gross monthly rent, net cash flow, trailing 12-month NOI, cap rate (NOI / current value), and cash-on-cash return (annual net cash flow / total cash invested). Include a portfolio total row. Flag any property where equity has declined or cap rate has dropped more than 0.5 percentage points from the prior year."

5. **Equity Snapshot:** "Using data from vault/estate/00_current/, calculate total portfolio equity across all properties as of today. For each property: current market value minus outstanding mortgage balance equals equity. Sum all equity values for total portfolio equity. Then compute loan-to-value ratio (mortgage balance / market value) per property and flag any property where LTV exceeds 80%. Show a table with columns: property, value, loan balance, equity, LTV, and equity-to-value %."

6. **Portfolio Cash-on-Cash Return:** "Calculate portfolio-level cash-on-cash return from vault/estate/00_current/. For each property, divide annual net cash flow (after all expenses and debt service) by total cash invested (down payment + closing costs + initial CapEx). Compute the weighted average cash-on-cash return across the portfolio weighting by cash invested. Flag any individual property below 5% CoC return. Output a table with per-property figures and a portfolio total row."

7. **Portfolio Appreciation Analysis:** "Using purchase prices and current estimated values from vault/estate/00_current/, compute total appreciation in dollars and percent for each property since acquisition. Annualize the appreciation rate using the exact number of months held. Compare each property's annualized appreciation to local market benchmarks (3–5% annual is typical in most markets). Flag any property appreciating below 2% annualized. Output a table sorted by annualized appreciation rate, highest to lowest."

## Cash Flow

8. **Cash Flow Deep Dive:** "Review my rental property cash flow using data from vault/estate/00_current/. For each property, build the full cash flow stack: gross scheduled income, minus vacancy allowance (5% default unless a different rate is stored), equals effective gross income; minus operating expenses (property taxes, insurance, property management fee, maintenance reserve), equals net operating income; minus debt service (principal + interest), equals net cash flow. Flag any property where net cash flow is negative or cash-on-cash return has dropped below 5%. Compare this month's NOI to the trailing 3-month average and flag variances greater than 10%. Output a table with one row per property plus a portfolio total row."

9. **NOI Analysis:** "Compute net operating income for each property in vault/estate/00_current/ for the current month and trailing 12 months. NOI = effective gross income minus all operating expenses, excluding debt service. Identify which expense categories are growing fastest (property taxes, insurance, maintenance, management fees). Flag any property where operating expense ratio (total operating expenses / gross income) exceeds 50%. Show a per-property table with monthly NOI, annual NOI run rate, and expense ratio."

10. **Cash Flow Trend:** "Using month-by-month income and expense data in vault/estate/00_current/ and vault/estate/00_current/, chart net cash flow per property for the last 12 months. Identify months where cash flow was negative and explain the cause (e.g., vacancy, large repair). Calculate the average monthly cash flow for each property over the trailing 12 months and compare to the prior 12-month average. Flag any property where average cash flow has declined more than $100/month year-over-year."

11. **Expense Audit:** "Pull all expense records from vault/estate/00_current/ and vault/estate/00_current/ for the current year to date. Categorize expenses by type: mortgage principal, mortgage interest, property taxes, insurance, property management, repairs and maintenance, utilities, landscaping, and CapEx. For each category show: total spent YTD, monthly average, percent of gross rental income. Flag any category running more than 20% above the prior-year pace. Output a categorized expense table sorted by total spend, highest to lowest."

## Tenants

12. **Tenant Status Review:** "Pull all tenant records from vault/estate/00_current/ and provide a status update for each unit. For each tenant show: property address, tenant name, lease start date, lease end date, monthly rent, rent collected this month (yes/no/partial), outstanding balance, and any outstanding notices or issues. Flag: (1) any tenant with rent not collected by the 5th of the month, (2) any lease expiring within 90 days, (3) any tenant with two or more late payments in the last 6 months. Output a table with a status color (green/yellow/red) per tenant."

13. **Lease Expiration Report:** "Review all lease agreements in vault/estate/00_current/ and produce a 90-day lease expiration report. For leases expiring within 30 days: mark as urgent and recommend immediate outreach. For leases expiring in 31–60 days: mark as action-required and recommend sending renewal offer. For leases expiring in 61–90 days: mark as watch-list and recommend drafting renewal terms. For each expiring lease include: tenant name, unit, current rent, market rent estimate, recommended renewal rent (market rent or current rent +3–5%, whichever is higher), and recommended action."

14. **Rent Collection Status:** "Check rent collection status across all units in vault/estate/00_current/ for the current month. List each unit, tenant, rent due, amount received, and date received. Flag any tenant where rent has not been received by the 5th of the month — these trigger the late fee notice process (typically 5% of monthly rent after 5-day grace period). List any outstanding balances from prior months. Output a collection summary table and a list of required follow-up actions."

15. **Rent vs. Market Comparison:** "For each property in vault/estate/00_current/ and vault/estate/00_current/, compare current contracted rent to estimated market rent for that unit type and location. Flag any unit where current rent is more than 10% below market — these are candidates for rent increases at renewal. Also note any Section 8 tenants and compare their Section 8 contract rent to market rate (Section 8 rents typically run within 10–15% of market and provide payment guarantee). Output a table with columns: unit, current rent, market rent, difference ($), difference (%), and recommendation."

## Maintenance

16. **Maintenance Schedule Review:** "Pull all maintenance records and scheduled items from vault/estate/00_current/. Group items by: overdue (past scheduled date), due this month, due next month, and due in 2–3 months. For each item show: property, description, scheduled date, estimated cost, and priority (safety/structural/cosmetic). Apply CapEx reserve benchmarks: flag if the portfolio has not budgeted $1–2% of total property value annually for CapEx. Output a maintenance calendar view and a running CapEx reserve balance."

17. **CapEx Reserve Analysis:** "Using property values from vault/estate/00_current/ and maintenance history from vault/estate/00_current/, analyze CapEx reserve adequacy across the portfolio. For each property, the target annual CapEx reserve is 1.5% of property value. List each major system with its estimated age, expected remaining life, and projected replacement cost: roof ($10,000–$20,000 every 20 years), HVAC ($5,000–$10,000 every 15 years), water heater ($1,000–$2,000 every 10 years), flooring and appliances ($5,000–$10,000 every 10–15 years). Compute the weighted monthly reserve needed for each property and compare to current reserve balance. Flag any property that is underfunded by more than $2,000."

18. **Repair vs. Capital Improvement Classifier:** "Review all maintenance expenses logged in vault/estate/00_current/ for the current year. Apply the IRS $2,500 threshold rule: expenses below $2,500 that restore property to working condition are repairs (fully deductible in the year incurred); expenses above $2,500 that add value, extend useful life, or adapt the property to a new use are capital improvements (must be depreciated). Classify each expense as repair or CapEx, provide the rationale, and output a table. Flag any expense where the classification is ambiguous and provide recommended documentation to support the chosen treatment."

19. **Maintenance Spend Summary:** "Pull all maintenance invoices and logs from vault/estate/00_current/ for the current calendar year. Summarize total maintenance spend per property and across the portfolio. Calculate maintenance spend as a percent of annual gross rent for each property (industry benchmark: 5–10% of gross rent). Flag any property where maintenance spend has exceeded 15% of annual gross rent YTD. List the top 5 single largest expenses, and flag any recurring issue (same system repaired more than twice) as a candidate for full replacement."

## Insurance

20. **Estate Insurance Renewal Tracker:** "Review all landlord and property insurance policies in vault/estate/00_current/. List each policy with: property covered, insurer, policy number, coverage limits (dwelling, liability, loss of rent), annual premium, and renewal date. Flag any policy renewing within 30 days as urgent. Flag any property where loss-of-rent coverage is less than 6 months of gross rent. Flag any policy where dwelling coverage is more than 20% below current replacement cost estimate. Output a renewals table sorted by upcoming renewal date."

21. **Coverage Adequacy Check:** "Using property data from vault/estate/00_current/ and insurance details from vault/estate/00_current/, assess whether each property is adequately insured. Check: (1) dwelling coverage vs. estimated replacement cost (not market value) — flag if gap exceeds 20%; (2) liability limit — minimum $300,000 per property recommended, $1,000,000 if no umbrella policy; (3) loss-of-rent coverage — should cover at least 6 months of gross rent; (4) flood and earthquake exclusions — flag if property is in a flood zone or earthquake-prone area without supplemental coverage. Output a coverage gap table per property."

## Financials

22. **Cap Rate Calculator:** "For each property in vault/estate/00_current/, calculate the current cap rate using this formula: cap rate = annual NOI / current market value. Annual NOI = (monthly gross rent × 12) × (1 − vacancy rate) minus annual operating expenses (taxes, insurance, management fees, maintenance, utilities — excluding debt service). Use current market value estimates from the vault or note when last updated. Compare each property's cap rate to the market average for its asset class and location (single-family residential benchmark: 4–7%). Flag any property with cap rate below 4%. Output a ranked cap rate table."

23. **DSCR Analysis:** "Compute debt service coverage ratio for each property in vault/estate/00_current/. DSCR = annual NOI / annual debt service (principal + interest payments). Most lenders require DSCR of at least 1.25 for cash-out refinancing or new acquisition financing. Flag any property with DSCR below 1.25 — these would not qualify for conventional investment property financing at current income levels. Flag any property with DSCR below 1.0 — these are cash-flow negative before CapEx. Output a DSCR table with NOI, annual debt service, DSCR ratio, and lender qualification status."

24. **Depreciation Schedule:** "Using acquisition data and improvement records from vault/estate/00_current/ and vault/estate/00_current/, build the current depreciation schedule for all rental properties. Residential rental property is depreciated over 27.5 years (straight-line method). Land is not depreciable — use the county assessor's land-to-improvement ratio to separate land value. List each depreciable asset with: description, placed-in-service date, cost basis, annual depreciation, accumulated depreciation to date, and remaining depreciable basis. Flag any capital improvements recorded in vault/estate/00_current/ that have not yet been added to the depreciation schedule."

25. **1031 Exchange Feasibility:** "Review properties in vault/estate/00_current/ and identify any candidates for a 1031 like-kind exchange — typically properties with significant appreciation, low cap rate relative to alternatives, or high management burden. For each candidate: show realized gain (current value minus adjusted cost basis), estimated tax liability without a 1031 (federal capital gains tax at 20% plus state taxes, plus 25% depreciation recapture on accumulated depreciation), and the target equity to deploy in a replacement property. Summarize the 45-day identification window and 180-day closing window requirements and flag which properties are best positioned for an exchange in the next 12 months."
