## You

I need about 8 hours a week of learning to stay on track for the Staff promo packet (Q4), but I am also in a half-marathon training block (race 2026-11-15) and Maya is in 1st grade. Help me fit it without burning out.

## claude · opus-4

Let us protect the one thing that actually moves the promo and let the rest flex. The system design course (~4 hrs/week) is the load-bearing item for the packet. Everything else (leadership course, Spanish, UX with Jordan) is valuable but secondary, so it should give way first on a hard week.

A workable shape for the week:
- Mon/Wed/Fri early mornings, 45 min each, before Maya is up: system design. That is your 4 hrs, on the days you are not doing a long run.
- Tue/Thu mornings are training. Do not stack study on top, you will resent both.
- Sunday evening, 90 min: leadership course plus reading. This is also your weekly review slot.
- Spanish and the UX course ride along: Spanish at dinner with Maya and Jordan (15 min, counts as family time), UX with Jordan one weeknight after Maya is down.

That is roughly 4 + 1.5 + 1 + 1 = ~7.5 hrs without inventing new time.

## You

Long-run days are Saturdays and they wipe me out. How do I not lose the weekend study block?

## claude · opus-4

Treat the long run as the anchor and study around it, not after it.
- Saturday: long run in the morning, then it is recovery and family. No study guilt. Saturday is off for learning.
- Move the Sunday-evening block earlier to Sunday late morning if you are fresher then, and keep it light: review and reading, not new hard material.
- The real risk is week-over-week creep where you skip the Mon/Wed/Fri mornings because you slept poorly. Guard those three by going to bed earlier on Sun/Tue/Thu.

Net: system design stays protected at ~4 hrs even on a heavy training week, the promo timeline holds, and the half-marathon block is not the thing that costs you the Staff packet. I would put the three morning study blocks and the long run on the calendar now so they stop competing in real time. This ties to career/ (the packet) and to your fitness training plan and calendar.
