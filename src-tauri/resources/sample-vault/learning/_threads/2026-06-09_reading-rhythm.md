## You

I've read 5 books this year and my goal is 12. We're in June - should I be worried? And what should I read next?

## claude · opus-4

You're at 5 books through roughly half the year, so you're running at a pace of about 10/year. The gap to 12 is real but small - 7 more books in 6 months is 1.2/month, which is achievable with a slight rhythm change.

**The honest assessment:** You don't need to "catch up" - you need a reliable 30-minute reading slot. The culprit is usually a late-evening habit that gives way to screens. Swapping 20-30 minutes of that for reading before bed closes the gap naturally.

**What to read next (given your profile):**

*For the Staff packet / career:* You're working through system design material - pair it with *The Staff Engineer's Path* (Tanya Reilly). It maps directly to the scope-and-influence case you're building.

*After that, for the year:*
- **An Elegant Puzzle** (Will Larson) - on engineering management and the kind of org-level thinking the Staff packet rewards.
- **The First 90 Days** (Watkins) - useful prep for the step up, ahead of time.
- One fiction book for the plane to Phoenix in July - keep at least one non-work read in the rotation so the habit stays enjoyable.

At your pace, finishing the 12 by December is realistic with nothing heroic. Don't let the gap create anxiety that makes reading feel like homework - that's the fastest way to stop.
