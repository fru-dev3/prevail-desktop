## You

## codex · gpt-5

Happy to help you prepare. I'm not a clinician, so treat this as a way to walk in informed, not as medical advice. Two flags worth a direct question are usually:

- A value that's trending the wrong way across multiple years (a pattern matters more than a single reading)
- Anything outside the reference range tied to a symptom you've actually noticed

For each flagged value, it helps to ask your doctor: is this a one-off or a trend, does it need a recheck, and is there a lifestyle change that moves it before we consider anything else. Which markers were flagged?

## You

LDL cholesterol was a bit high, and vitamin D was low.

## codex · gpt-5

Both are common and usually addressable:

- **LDL slightly high:** ask whether it's high enough to act on given your overall risk, whether a recheck in 3 months after diet/exercise changes makes sense before medication, and what target your doctor wants. Soluble fiber, less saturated fat, and regular activity are the usual first levers.
- **Low vitamin D:** very common, especially with limited sun. Ask whether a supplement is warranted and at what dose, and when to recheck.

Good questions to bring: "Is this a trend or a single reading?", "What would we try first before medication?", and "When should I retest?" Want me to add a follow-up to your open loops so it doesn't slip?
