# AI Ready Life: Health — Prompt Reference

## Weekly & Monthly Reviews

1. **Weekly Health Review:** "Run my weekly health review. Read vault/health/00_current/ for any results newer than 7 days and flag biomarkers outside reference ranges (fasting glucose >99 mg/dL, LDL >100 mg/dL, HDL <40 male/<50 female, A1c >5.6%, TSH outside 0.4–4.0 mIU/L). Check vault/health/00_current/open-loops.md for unresolved items. Scan vault/health/00_current/ for any appointments in the next 14 days. Output a brief with: flagged labs, upcoming appointments, medication refills due within 7 days, and 3 action items ranked by urgency."

2. **Monthly Health Review:** "Run my monthly health review. Aggregate all lab results added to vault/health/00_current/ in the past 30 days and compare each biomarker to both the clinical reference range and my personal baseline from 12 months ago. Cross-reference vault/health/00_current/ for visit outcomes and any new provider orders. Review vault/health/00_current/medications.md for adherence notes and upcoming 90-day Rx renewals (flag anything with <7 days supply remaining). Summarize: biomarker trends (improving/stable/declining), completed and pending preventive care, and 5 prioritized action items for next month."

3. **Wellness Summary:** "Build my monthly wellness summary by synthesizing data from vault/health/00_current/, vault/health/00_current/, and vault/health/00_current/. For each dimension — labs, medications, preventive care, wearables, and mental health — rate status as Green (on track), Yellow (needs attention), or Red (action required). Flag any biomarker that has moved >10% in either direction from my prior result. Produce a one-page summary suitable for sharing with a physician, including a section listing all open follow-up items and their resolution status."

4. **Health Quarterly Brief:** "Generate my quarterly health brief covering the past 90 days. Pull all lab data from vault/health/00_current/, visit notes from vault/health/00_current/, and open loop history from vault/health/00_current/open-loops.md. For labs, compute a 90-day average for each tracked biomarker and compare to the prior quarter. Identify any screening that is overdue based on age-appropriate preventive care cadence (annual physical, dental 2×/year, dermatology annually, colonoscopy at 45+). Output a structured brief: Executive Summary, Biomarker Trends table, Preventive Care Status, and Recommended Actions."

## Labs & Anomalies

5. **Latest Lab Review:** "Review my latest lab results in vault/health/00_current/. For each biomarker, apply these thresholds: fasting glucose 70–99 mg/dL, A1c <5.7%, LDL <100 mg/dL, HDL >40 (male), total cholesterol <200, triglycerides <150, TSH 0.4–4.0 mIU/L, creatinine 0.6–1.2 mg/dL, Vitamin D 30–100 ng/mL. Classify each result as Normal, Borderline (>10% outside range), or Critical (>20% outside range). Output a table with columns: Biomarker | Result | Reference Range | Status | Trend vs. Last Result."

6. **Biomarker Trend Analysis:** "Compare my lab results across the past four panels in vault/health/00_current/, ordered chronologically. For each biomarker tracked in at least two panels, compute the absolute change and percentage change from the earliest to the most recent result. Flag any biomarker showing a consistent directional trend across 3+ panels (e.g., LDL rising over three consecutive draws), even if still within range, as a 'watch' item. Produce a trend summary table and a short narrative interpretation for each flagged biomarker."

7. **Critical Anomaly Scan:** "Scan all lab files in vault/health/00_current/ added within the past 60 days and identify any value that is >20% outside its clinical reference range. Apply thresholds: glucose >119 or <56 mg/dL, A1c >6.8%, LDL >120 mg/dL, total cholesterol >240, triglycerides >180, TSH >4.8 or <0.32 mIU/L, creatinine >1.44 or <0.48 mg/dL, Vitamin D <24 ng/mL. For each critical finding, output: the exact value, the threshold breached, likely clinical implications in plain language, and the recommended next step (repeat test, specialist referral, or urgent care)."

8. **Quarter-over-Quarter Lab Comparison:** "Pull my most recent lab panel and the lab panel from the same quarter last year, both stored in vault/health/00_current/. For each shared biomarker, compute the year-over-year delta and classify the direction as Improved, Stable (within 5% change), or Worsened. Highlight any biomarker that has crossed a clinical threshold in either direction (e.g., moved from Normal to Borderline). Produce a side-by-side comparison table and a brief narrative explaining which metrics warrant physician discussion at my next visit."

9. **Vitamin D & Hormonal Panel Deep Dive:** "Analyze my most recent Vitamin D and hormonal panel results in vault/health/00_current/. Apply these ranges: Vitamin D 30–100 ng/mL (optimal 50–80), TSH 0.4–4.0 mIU/L, free T4 0.8–1.8 ng/dL, testosterone (if present) 300–1000 ng/dL male. Flag any value outside optimal (not just reference) range. Cross-reference vault/health/00_current/ for any physician commentary on prior hormonal results. Output: current values vs. optimal ranges, trend from prior panels, and specific supplementation or follow-up questions to raise with my prescriber."

## Medications & Refills

10. **Medication Refill Audit:** "Read vault/health/00_current/medications.md and identify every prescription with its days-supply, fill date, and refill due date. Apply these refill buffer rules: flag any 90-day Rx whose refill window opens within 7 days and any 30-day Rx whose refill window opens within 3 days. Cross-check vault/health/00_current/ for any medications ordered at recent visits that do not appear in the active medication list. Output a sorted table: Medication | Dose | Days Supply | Last Filled | Refill Due | Status (OK / Refill Soon / Overdue)."

11. **Current Medication List Reconciliation:** "Review vault/health/00_current/medications.md and vault/health/00_current/ to reconcile my active medication list. Identify any medication prescribed at a visit that is not in the current list, any medication in the list with no prescribing visit documentation, and any dosage discrepancies between what was prescribed and what is documented as current. Flag interactions to ask the pharmacist about. Output a clean, reconciled medication list with: drug name, dose, frequency, prescribing provider, indication, and last refill date."

12. **New Medication Log:** "Add a new medication entry to vault/health/00_current/medications.md using this structure: Drug Name, Generic Name, Dose, Frequency, Indication, Prescribing Provider, Start Date, Days Supply, Next Refill Date, Notes. After adding, re-run the refill audit to confirm the new medication's first refill date fits within the monitoring window. Confirm the entry has been saved and show the updated medication table."

## Preventive Care

13. **Preventive Care Gap Analysis:** "Read vault/health/00_current/preventive-care.md and vault/health/00_current/ to audit my preventive care status against age-appropriate guidelines. Required cadence: annual physical (PCP), dental cleaning 2×/year, dermatology skin check annually, eye exam every 1–2 years, colonoscopy at age 45+ then per physician guidance, lipid panel annually if any value was borderline, A1c annually if fasting glucose was 95–99 mg/dL. For each item, compute months since last completed and flag anything overdue by more than 3 months. Output a table: Screening | Last Date | Due Date | Status | Recommended Action."

14. **Upcoming Preventive Appointments:** "Scan vault/health/00_current/ for scheduled future appointments and vault/health/00_current/preventive-care.md for last-completed dates. List all preventive care visits due in the next 90 days, including: type of visit, provider name, scheduled date (if booked) or 'Needs Scheduling' (if not). For each unscheduled item, provide the specific provider or specialty needed and a suggested booking timeframe. Also flag any specialist referrals from vault/health/00_current/ that have not yet been followed up on."

15. **Annual Physical Prep Brief:** "Prepare a pre-visit brief for my upcoming annual physical. Pull all labs from vault/health/00_current/ from the past 12 months and identify the most recent value for each standard panel biomarker. List all active medications from vault/health/00_current/medications.md. Extract all open loops from vault/health/00_current/open-loops.md. Review vault/health/00_current/ for any issues raised at the prior annual physical that should be followed up on. Output a one-page patient brief: Current Medications, Biomarker Summary, Unresolved Issues, and 5 Questions to Ask the Doctor."

## Wearable & Wellness Data

16. **Sleep & HRV Trend Analysis:** "Analyze my wearable data in vault/health/00_current/wearables.md or linked data exports for the past 30 days. Focus on: nightly sleep duration (target ≥7 hours), sleep efficiency (target ≥85%), HRV (flag if 7-day average drops >15% below my 30-day baseline), and resting heart rate (flag if trending up >5 bpm over 2 weeks). Correlate any HRV dips with logged stressors, illness, or alcohol entries if present. Output a 30-day trend summary with weekly averages and a list of nights where sleep quality was significantly below baseline (score <70 if using Oura's system)."

17. **Activity & Recovery Review:** "Review my wearable activity data for the past 14 days in vault/health/00_current/wearables.md. Track: daily step count (target 8,000+/day), active minutes (target ≥150 min/week moderate intensity), and Oura readiness or equivalent recovery score (flag any day below 70/100). Compute: step goal achievement rate (%), average readiness score, and days with <6 hours sleep in the window. Flag any pattern of low readiness paired with high training load as overtraining risk. Output a weekly summary table and 3 recommendations to improve recovery metrics next week."

18. **Wellness Trend Dashboard:** "Build a wellness trend snapshot covering the past 60 days using all available data in vault/health/00_current/. Aggregate: average sleep duration, average HRV, average resting HR, days meeting step goal, and any logged mood or energy scores. Compare the most recent 30 days to the prior 30 days for each metric and classify as Improving, Stable, or Declining. For any declining metric, cross-reference the timeline with any new medications, lab anomalies, or high-stress periods noted in vault/health/00_current/ or open-loops.md. Output a dashboard-style brief with a one-sentence interpretation for each metric."

## Insurance & HSA

19. **HSA Contribution Tracker:** "Read vault/health/00_current/insurance.md and any HSA account statement in vault/health/00_current/ or linked financial data. Compute: total employee HSA contributions YTD, total employer HSA contributions YTD, and combined total vs. the 2025 IRS limit ($4,300 individual / $8,550 family). Calculate the remaining contribution room and the monthly contribution needed to max out by December 31. Flag if on-pace to under-contribute by more than $500. Output: YTD contributed, IRS limit, gap remaining, and recommended monthly contribution increase."

20. **Insurance Coverage Review:** "Review my health insurance details in vault/health/00_current/insurance.md. Extract and summarize: plan type (HDHP/PPO/HMO), annual deductible (individual and family), out-of-pocket maximum, current deductible progress YTD, copay structure for PCP/specialist/urgent care/ER visits, and prescription drug tiers. Determine if my deductible is HDHP-qualifying (≥$1,650 individual / $3,300 family for 2025) to confirm HSA eligibility. Flag any coverage gap (e.g., no mental health parity, limited specialist network) and output a clean coverage summary card."

21. **HSA Investment Optimization:** "Review my HSA balance in vault/health/00_current/insurance.md. Many HSA providers allow investing balances above a $1,000–$2,000 cash threshold. Calculate: current HSA cash balance, estimated annual out-of-pocket medical spend based on vault/health/00_current/ claims history, and whether a portion should be invested rather than held in cash. If the balance exceeds 2× my expected annual out-of-pocket, flag the excess as investable. Recommend an action: invest excess, hold for near-term expenses, or increase contribution rate. Apply the triple-tax-advantage framing: pre-tax in, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawal for qualified medical."

## Appointments & Providers

22. **Provider Directory Review:** "Read vault/health/00_current/ to compile my current provider directory. For each provider who appears in at least one visit note, extract: provider name, specialty, affiliated practice, phone number (if documented), and date of last visit. Flag any provider for whom I have an open referral but no completed visit within 90 days. Flag any specialist relationship that has lapsed >24 months. Output a clean provider table and note any gaps in my care team (e.g., no current cardiologist if triglycerides or BP was flagged)."

23. **Visit Note Summary:** "Read all visit notes in vault/health/00_current/ from the past 90 days. For each visit, extract: date, provider name, chief complaint, assessment/diagnosis, prescribed treatment or medication changes, and follow-up instructions. Compile a chronological visit log and flag any follow-up instruction that has not been completed (e.g., 'repeat labs in 6 weeks,' 'schedule imaging,' 'refer to specialist'). Output a structured visit log with a separate section listing all outstanding follow-up actions and their due dates."

24. **Appointment Scheduling Tracker:** "Scan vault/health/00_current/ for all upcoming scheduled appointments and all provider referrals or recommendations to schedule. Create a master appointment tracker with: appointment type, provider, scheduled date (or 'To Schedule'), urgency tier (Routine / Soon / Urgent), and any prep required (fasting, insurance pre-auth, copay estimate). For any recommended appointment older than 30 days that has not been scheduled, escalate to urgent status. Output the tracker sorted by urgency and then by due date."

## Open Loops

25. **Health Open Loop Audit:** "Read vault/health/00_current/open-loops.md and cross-reference with vault/health/00_current/ and vault/health/00_current/ to validate each open loop's status. For each item, determine: Is it truly unresolved, or was it addressed in a subsequent visit or lab result? Flag any open loop older than 90 days with no documented progress as 'Stalled.' Prioritize remaining open loops by health risk: Critical (symptom or lab needing immediate follow-up), Moderate (pending referral or imaging), Routine (administrative or preventive). Output a ranked open loop list with suggested resolution actions."

26. **Open Loop Resolution Update:** "Update vault/health/00_current/open-loops.md to mark resolved items as closed and add new items surfaced during my most recent visit or lab review. For each newly resolved item, document: resolution date, how it was resolved (e.g., 'labs repeated — now normal,' 'referral completed'), and any ongoing monitoring needed. For each new open loop, document: date identified, source (lab/visit/self-reported), description, priority (Critical/Moderate/Routine), and next action. Confirm the file has been updated and show the current open loop count by priority tier."

27. **Medication Refill Open Loop Sweep:** "Cross-reference vault/health/00_current/open-loops.md with vault/health/00_current/medications.md to identify any medication-related open loops (e.g., prior auth pending, dose adjustment to confirm, pharmacy transfer in progress). For each medication open loop, determine the age of the issue and the current status. Flag any open loop older than 14 days that involves a controlled substance or critical maintenance medication. Output a medication-specific open loop list with escalation recommendations for anything blocking timely refill."
