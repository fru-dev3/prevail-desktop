# AI Ready Life: Career — Prompt Reference

## Weekly & Monthly Reviews

1. **Weekly Career Pulse:** "Run my weekly career pulse check. Read vault/career/00_current/open-loops.md for any unresolved items. Check vault/career/00_current/ for any applications that have been in a single stage for more than 7 days without a response (flag for follow-up). Review vault/career/00_current/goals.md for any milestones due this week. Scan for any networking outreach sent more than 5 business days ago without a reply. Output a brief covering: active applications needing follow-up, overdue career actions, upcoming interviews or deadlines, and 3 prioritized actions for this week."

2. **Monthly Career Review:** "Run my monthly career review. Read vault/career/00_current/ for current role details, compensation, and performance notes. Review vault/career/00_current/ for all applications submitted this month: compute total applications sent, response rate (responses ÷ applications), phone screen conversion rate, and offer rate. Assess vault/career/00_current/skills.md for any skills development progress. Compare current compensation to market rates using the Levels.fyi and Glassdoor benchmarks documented in vault/career/00_current/. Output a structured brief: Application Pipeline Summary, Comp vs. Market, Skills Progress, and 5 Recommended Actions for next month."

3. **Career Quarterly Strategy Review:** "Conduct my quarterly career strategy review. Read vault/career/00_current/goals.md for my 1-year and 3-year career targets. Assess progress against each goal: title progression, compensation target, skills acquisition milestones, and network growth. Review the past quarter's application pipeline in vault/career/00_current/ — compute stage-by-stage conversion rates and identify where candidates drop out. Review vault/career/00_current/skills.md for skills gaps vs. target roles. Determine if the current strategy is on pace or needs adjustment. Output a strategic assessment: Goal Progress Table, Pipeline Health Analysis, Skills Gap Status, and a 90-day action plan with 5 specific next steps."

## Compensation & Market

4. **Total Compensation Analysis:** "Build my total compensation breakdown using vault/career/00_current/. Extract: base salary, target bonus (percentage and expected payout), RSU grant value (annualized: total unvested shares × current stock price ÷ remaining vesting years), ESPP benefit (annualized value of 15% discount on contribution), 401k employer match (annual dollar value), employer health insurance premium contribution, PTO value (days × daily rate), and any other benefits with quantifiable value. Sum all components to compute total compensation. Compare total comp to base salary alone to show the full picture. Output a comp breakdown table with dollar values and percentages of total, plus a one-paragraph narrative on the comp mix."

5. **Comp Gap vs. Market Benchmark:** "Calculate my compensation gap versus market rates for my current role. Read vault/career/00_current/ for: role title, years of experience, location (metro area), and company size. Pull market data documented in vault/career/00_current/market-data.md (sourced from Levels.fyi for tech roles, Glassdoor, and LinkedIn Salary). Identify P25, P50, and P75 total compensation for my role, location, and YOE. Compare my current total comp to each percentile. Flag as underpaid if my total comp is more than 15% below P50. If underpaid, calculate the dollar gap to P50 and P75. Output: market percentile table, my current comp vs. each percentile, gap in dollars and percentage, and a recommended negotiation strategy."

6. **Compensation Negotiation Prep:** "Prepare a negotiation brief for an upcoming compensation review or job offer evaluation. Read vault/career/00_current/ for current comp details and vault/career/00_current/ for any recent offer data. Research market rates: pull P50 and P75 benchmarks for the target role, location, and YOE from vault/career/00_current/market-data.md. Develop a target ask and a walk-away floor. For base salary negotiation, the standard range is P50 as the floor and P75 as the target ask. For equity, benchmark against similar-stage companies on Levels.fyi. Draft 3 specific talking points that anchor the negotiation to market data. Output: target ask (base/bonus/equity breakdown), supporting data points, and a 3-part negotiation script."

7. **Annual Review Preparation Brief:** "Prepare my annual performance review self-assessment using vault/career/00_current/. Extract: key accomplishments from the past 12 months (quantified where possible — revenue impact, cost savings, projects shipped, team size managed), skills developed, and feedback received. Compute YOY compensation change and compare to inflation (target real wage growth ≥2%) and market movement. Identify 3 concrete talking points for merit increase justification and 2 for promotion readiness. If current comp is below P50 for the role and location, include market data to anchor the conversation. Output a self-assessment draft with an accomplishments section, a development section, and a compensation discussion script."

## Market Scan

8. **Job Market Scan for Target Roles:** "Scan the current job market for roles matching my career target profile in vault/career/00_current/goals.md. Target criteria: role title(s), industry, location preference (remote/hybrid/in-office), minimum base compensation, and company size range. Review any saved job listings in vault/career/00_current/pipeline.md. For each role in the target set, assess: company trajectory (growth vs. contraction signals), compensation transparency (posted salary range vs. target), and team/role signals from the job description. Identify the top 5 opportunities based on: comp alignment, role scope match, and company quality. Output a ranked opportunity table with a rationale for each ranking."

9. **Target Company Research Brief:** "Research a specific target company for a potential application. Read vault/career/00_current/ for any prior interactions with this company. Compile: company overview (industry, size, revenue stage, recent funding or earnings), leadership team and culture signals, known compensation ranges for the target role from Levels.fyi or Glassdoor, interview process reputation (source: Glassdoor, Blind), and any mutual connections in my network from vault/career/00_current/network.md. Assess: alignment with my skills and target comp. Output a one-page company brief with: Company Summary, Comp Data, Interview Process, My Connections, and a Go / No-Go recommendation with rationale."

10. **Role Description Match Analysis:** "Compare my current resume or skill profile in vault/career/00_current/ against the job description for a target role. Extract all required and preferred skills, experience requirements, and qualifications from the job description. Score each requirement: Strong Match (I clearly meet this), Partial Match (I partially meet this or have transferable experience), or Gap (I do not currently meet this). Count skills appearing in >60% of similar JDs (pull from vault/career/00_current/market-data.md) that are in my Gap column — these are high-priority development items. Output: skills match table, a match score (percentage of requirements met), a gap list sorted by frequency in the market, and a tailored resume focus recommendation."

## Applications

11. **Application Pipeline Review:** "Read vault/career/00_current/ and compile the current state of all active job applications. For each application, extract: company name, role title, date applied, current stage (Applied / Phone Screen / Technical / Onsite / Offer / Rejected), last contact date, and next action. Compute pipeline health metrics: total active applications, stage distribution, average days per stage, and overall response rate. Flag any application stalled in the same stage for >10 business days as requiring follow-up. Identify any application that has gone to 'Offer' stage and needs immediate attention. Output a pipeline table sorted by stage and last contact date, plus a health metrics summary."

12. **Application Follow-Up Tracker:** "Scan vault/career/00_current/ for all applications where a follow-up is overdue. Define overdue: no response within 7 business days of submitting an application, no response within 5 business days of a phone screen, no response within 3 business days of an onsite or final interview. For each overdue item, draft a brief, professional follow-up message (2–3 sentences) appropriate to the stage. Warm referral applications: escalate to the referrer if >10 business days with no recruiter response. Output: a list of overdue applications with stage, days since last contact, draft follow-up message, and recommended send date."

13. **Application Log Entry:** "Log a new job application to vault/career/00_current/. Capture the following fields: company name, role title, job posting URL, date applied, application method (direct / LinkedIn / referral — if referral, note the contact's name), current stage, compensation range if posted, role location and work model (remote/hybrid/onsite), and any notes about why this role is a target. After logging, update the pipeline summary in vault/career/00_current/pipeline.md with the new total application count and any stage changes from prior entries. Output a confirmation of the logged entry and the updated pipeline count by stage."

14. **Offer Evaluation Framework:** "Evaluate a job offer using vault/career/00_current/ data and market benchmarks. For the offer in question, extract all compensation components: base salary, signing bonus, annual target bonus (percentage and structure), equity grant (shares, vesting schedule, current valuation), benefits value (401k match, health insurance, PTO). Compute total first-year compensation and steady-state annual compensation (excluding signing bonus). Compare total comp to: (1) my current comp from vault/career/00_current/, (2) P50 and P75 market rates for the role and location. Assess non-compensation factors: company stage and trajectory, role scope, reporting structure, growth potential. Output: comp comparison table, a Go / No-Go recommendation, and 3 specific negotiation levers to push on."

## Skills Gap

15. **Skills Gap Analysis:** "Run a skills gap analysis by comparing my current skill inventory in vault/career/00_current/skills.md against the requirements of my target role in vault/career/00_current/goals.md. For each skill listed in target job descriptions (reference vault/career/00_current/market-data.md for skills frequency analysis), classify as: Proficient (can demonstrate with examples), Developing (some exposure, not yet fluent), or Gap (no current experience). Focus on skills appearing in >60% of target JDs as high-priority gaps. For each high-priority gap, estimate the time investment to reach competency and identify the most efficient learning path (certification, project, course, on-the-job acquisition). Output: skills matrix table, gap priority ranking, and a 90-day learning roadmap."

16. **Skill Development Roadmap:** "Build a 90-day skill development roadmap based on my gap analysis in vault/career/00_current/skills.md. For each identified priority skill gap, specify: the learning resource (specific course, certification, or project), estimated time commitment per week, target completion date, and how I will demonstrate competency (portfolio project, certification credential, or on-the-job application). Prioritize skills that appear in >60% of target role JDs and where there is a clear near-term application opportunity. For technical skills, include a hands-on project milestone. Output a week-by-week learning calendar for 90 days with specific resource links and completion milestones."

17. **Certification & Learning Tracker:** "Read vault/career/00_current/skills.md for any in-progress certifications or learning programs. For each, extract: program name, provider, start date, expected completion date, cost, and relevance to target roles. Flag any program that is overdue for completion or has not had logged progress in >30 days. For completed certifications, confirm they have been added to my resume and LinkedIn profile. Assess whether the skills acquired since the last review have closed any of the gaps identified in vault/career/00_current/skills.md. Output a learning tracker table and a gap closure percentage estimate."

## Network

18. **Network Review & Outreach Prioritization:** "Read vault/career/00_current/network.md to review my professional network. Identify contacts who are: (1) at target companies from my job search list, (2) in a position to provide referrals (hiring manager, team member, or recruiter at a target company), or (3) a connector to other relevant contacts. Warm referrals increase callback rates approximately 5× vs. cold applications — prioritize contacts who can submit internal referrals. For each high-value contact, assess recency of last interaction and flag any relationship that has gone cold for >6 months as needing a reconnection message. Output: a prioritized outreach list with contact name, company, relationship strength, last contact date, and recommended outreach approach."

19. **Network Outreach Message Draft:** "Draft a personalized outreach message to a specific professional contact documented in vault/career/00_current/network.md. The message should be: concise (under 150 words), specific about why I am reaching out now, reference a genuine shared connection or prior interaction, and include a single, easy-to-fulfill ask (a 20-minute call, an introduction to someone, a referral submission). Do not ask for a job directly — ask for advice or an introduction. The tone should match the relationship strength: warm (close contact) or professional (acquaintance). Output the draft message and a suggested send date."

20. **Referral Request Strategy:** "Identify the best candidates in my network to request internal referrals from for specific target companies. Read vault/career/00_current/network.md and cross-reference with active target companies in vault/career/00_current/pipeline.md. For each target company with a contact in my network, assess: (1) contact seniority and closeness to the target team, (2) last interaction recency, (3) likelihood of willingness to refer. Draft a referral request message for the top 3 contacts that: explains the specific role, provides a 2–3 sentence rationale for why I am a strong fit, and makes the referral submission as easy as possible (attach resume, note the JD link). Output: ranked referral contact list and a draft message for the top contact."

## Offers & Negotiation

21. **Offer Comparison — Current vs. New:** "Compare my current total compensation to a new job offer using vault/career/00_current/ and vault/career/00_current/. For each compensation element, compute the dollar-for-dollar difference: base salary delta, bonus delta (expected value, not target), equity delta (unvested equity at current job that would be forfeited plus new grant annualized value), signing bonus (amortized over 2 years), and benefits delta (health insurance cost, 401k match, PTO days × daily rate). Compute total first-year and second-year comp under each scenario, accounting for forfeited unvested equity in year 1. Flag if the new offer requires forfeiting >$20,000 of unvested equity. Output: side-by-side comp table for Year 1 and Year 2, net gain/loss, and a break-even timeline analysis."

22. **Negotiation Counter-Offer Script:** "Prepare a negotiation counter-offer strategy for a specific job offer in vault/career/00_current/. Identify the 3 highest-leverage negotiation points: base salary, signing bonus, and equity grant. For base: target P75 for the role/location/YOE using market data from vault/career/00_current/market-data.md and anchor the ask to specific benchmarks. For signing: request 50–100% of unvested equity being forfeited at current employer. For equity: request a larger grant or an accelerated vesting schedule. Draft a professional email counter-offer (under 300 words) that: expresses genuine enthusiasm, anchors each ask to market data or specific context, and frames the counter as a collaborative discussion rather than an ultimatum. Output the counter-offer strategy and the draft email."

## Open Loops

23. **Career Open Loop Audit:** "Read vault/career/00_current/open-loops.md and cross-reference with vault/career/00_current/ and vault/career/00_current/ to assess each item's current status. Classify: Active (in progress with a clear next action), Stalled (no movement in >10 business days), or Resolved (closed). For each stalled item, diagnose the blocker and recommend a specific unblocking action. Flag any open loop involving a time-sensitive application deadline, an offer expiration, or a compensation review with a decision deadline. Output a ranked open loop list sorted by urgency with next action, owner, and deadline for each item."

24. **Career Action Items This Week:** "Compile all career action items due this week by scanning vault/career/00_current/open-loops.md, vault/career/00_current/, and vault/career/00_current/goals.md. Include: follow-up messages due, interview prep sessions needed, applications to submit by a deadline, networking outreach to send, and any skills development milestones. Rank each item by urgency and estimated time to complete. For any item estimated at <15 minutes, flag it as 'quick win' and recommend completing it first. Output a daily action plan organized by day of the week with estimated time for each task."
