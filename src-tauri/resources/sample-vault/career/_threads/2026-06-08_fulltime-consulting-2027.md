## You

Pressure-test my plan to go full-time consulting in 2027. Today the consultancy does ~$3,500/mo recurring across Acme, Bluepeak, and Hopeline. My rule is I only leave TechFlow if recurring revenue clears $8,000/mo. I make $145k base there plus RSUs, and the health plan covers Jordan and Maya. What am I not seeing?

## claude · opus-4

Three things have to be true at once before this is safe, and revenue is only the first.

1. Revenue. $3,500 to $8,000/mo is a 2.3x jump in recurring, not project, income. Acme is $2,000, Bluepeak $1,000, Hopeline $500. To clear $8,000 you need roughly two more Acme-sized retainers, or one large one plus the Northwind prospect in your opportunities list. Set a milestone: by Q2 2027, three consecutive months above $8,000 before you give notice.

2. Insurance. Leaving TechFlow means leaving the employer BCBS plan that covers Jordan and Maya. A comparable family marketplace plan in Austin can run $1,500 to $2,200/mo with a real deductible. That is a direct haircut on the $8,000. So your true replacement threshold is closer to $9,500 to $10,000/mo gross to net the same household cash. Jordan is at Designify at $95k, so check whether her employer plan can carry the family. That single answer can move your threshold by $2,000/mo.

3. Tax and runway. As a 1099 business you owe self-employment tax (~15.3 percent) plus income tax, with no employer withholding. Maria should set quarterlies. Net, plan to keep ~30 percent of consulting revenue aside. Your emergency fund is $32k, which is a solid buffer but it is the family emergency fund, not a business runway. Build a separate 3 month business runway before you leave.

Want me to turn the $8,000 rule into an insurance-adjusted threshold and a checklist?
