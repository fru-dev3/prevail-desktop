## Reviews and Audits

1. **Full Insurance Coverage Audit:** "Run a comprehensive insurance coverage audit across all policies in vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, and vault/insurance/00_current/. Output a per-policy summary table followed by a gap analysis section."

2. **Coverage Summary Dashboard:** "Build a single-page coverage summary using all policies in vault/insurance/. For each coverage category (health, life, auto, home/renters, umbrella, disability) show: policy in force (yes/no), current coverage limit, annual premium, and renewal date. Highlight any coverage category where no policy is in force — these are uncovered risks. Sum total annual insurance premiums and show insurance spend as a percent of gross annual income (industry benchmark: 10–15% of income for comprehensive coverage). Output a clean dashboard table with one row per coverage category."

3. **Insurance Brief:** "Generate a concise insurance brief covering: (1) any policy renewing within 30 days from vault/insurance/; (2) any critical coverage gap identified in the last audit; (3) the single highest-priority insurance action item. Format as a short executive summary — bullet points only, maximum 200 words."

4. **Annual Insurance Review:** "Conduct a full annual insurance review using all vault/insurance/ data. Assess: (1) have my coverage needs changed (income change affecting life insurance target, new vehicle, home purchase, net worth increase affecting umbrella limit); (2) are premiums competitive — flag any policy where premium increased more than 10% at renewal without a corresponding coverage increase; (3) are all beneficiary designations current for life insurance; (4) is the umbrella policy limit still equal to or greater than total net worth; (5) has health deductible spending been tracked YTD. Output a structured annual review with a recommended action for each area."

## Renewals

5. **Renewal Watch Report:** "Pull all insurance policy renewal dates from vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, and vault/insurance/00_current/. Sort by renewal date. Flag as urgent any policy renewing within 30 days — these require immediate review and decision. For each upcoming renewal note: policy type, insurer, current premium, whether to re-shop vs. auto-renew, and the deadline to make a decision without a coverage lapse. Output a renewal calendar."

6. **Open Enrollment Readiness:** "If health insurance open enrollment is approaching (typically November 1 – December 15 for ACA marketplace; varies for employer plans), run an open enrollment readiness check using vault/insurance/00_current/. Review: current plan (deductible, out-of-pocket max, monthly premium, in-network provider status for my doctors and specialists), YTD healthcare spending vs. deductible, FSA or HSA balance and contribution level, and whether a plan change would result in net savings. For employer plans: compare all available tiers using total cost of insurance (annual premium + expected out-of-pocket) not just the monthly premium. Output a plan comparison table and a recommendation."

7. **Auto Insurance Renewal Review:** "When my auto insurance approaches renewal per vault/insurance/00_current/, run a renewal analysis. Check: (1) has my annual mileage changed significantly since the policy was written — if I am driving less than 7,500 miles/year, usage-based or pay-per-mile insurance may save 10–30%; (2) have I added any safety features to the vehicle (anti-theft, dashcam) that qualify for discounts; (3) bundling discount — am I bundling auto with home/renters for the maximum multi-policy discount (typically 5–15%); (4) is my current deductible appropriate given my emergency fund level — higher deductible with adequate savings lowers premium. Quote at least 2 competitors before renewing if the premium increased more than 10%."

## Coverage Analysis

8. **Umbrella Liability Gap Analysis:** "Using net worth data and existing liability coverage limits from vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, and vault/insurance/00_current/, assess umbrella coverage adequacy. The core principle: umbrella coverage should equal or exceed total net worth, because plaintiffs can target all personal assets in a judgment. Show: current auto liability limit, current home/renters liability limit, current umbrella limit, total liability coverage, estimated current net worth, and gap (if any) between umbrella limit and net worth. Also flag if I have any elevated liability risk factors (rental properties, swimming pool, frequent driving, social media presence, dog). Recommend appropriate umbrella limit and estimated annual premium (typically $150–$300 per $1M of coverage)."

9. **Life Insurance Adequacy Analysis:** "Using income, dependents, and debt data alongside life insurance details from vault/insurance/00_current/, assess whether my life insurance coverage is adequate. Apply the 10–12× gross income rule of thumb as a baseline, then adjust for: mortgage payoff amount, years of income replacement needed until youngest child reaches 22, total outstanding debt (excluding mortgage), estimated college funding gap, and surviving spouse's own income. Show: current face value, target coverage using the above factors, gap in dollars, and estimated additional premium to close the gap. Also flag: term expiration date — if the policy term expires before the youngest child reaches 22, flag as a coverage gap requiring action."

10. **Renters Insurance Contents Coverage Check:** "Using personal property details and the renters insurance policy from vault/insurance/00_current/, assess whether contents coverage is adequate. Conduct a rough contents inventory: electronics (laptop, TV, gaming systems, phones), furniture, clothing and jewelry, kitchen appliances, and specialty items (musical instruments, sports equipment, art). Estimate replacement cost (not actual cash value) of all contents. Compare to current personal property coverage limit. Flag if coverage limit is more than 20% below estimated replacement value. Also check: does the policy provide replacement cost value (RCV) or actual cash value (ACV)? RCV is preferred — ACV pays depreciated value, which can be 40–60% less for older items. Recommend increasing coverage if underinsured."

## Health Insurance

11. **Health Deductible Tracker:** "Using health insurance details from vault/insurance/00_current/ and any tracked medical expenses, calculate YTD progress toward the annual deductible and out-of-pocket maximum. Show: annual deductible, amount met YTD, amount remaining. Show: annual out-of-pocket maximum, amount met YTD, amount remaining. If the deductible is close to being met (within 80% of the full deductible), flag this as a potential opportunity to accelerate elective procedures or prescriptions this calendar year before the deductible resets on January 1. Also flag if an HSA is available and whether I am contributing the maximum ($4,150 individual / $8,300 family for 2025). Output a deductible and OOP max tracker."

12. **FSA Deadline Alert:** "Check the FSA details in vault/insurance/00_current/ for any use-it-or-lose-it deadline. Standard FSA plans have a December 31 deadline (some employers offer a 2.5-month grace period to March 15, or a $640 rollover option). Show: current FSA balance, plan deadline or grace period, and remaining days to spend. If the balance is greater than $200 with fewer than 60 days remaining before the deadline, flag as urgent and list eligible FSA expenses to use the funds on: prescription eyeglasses, contact lenses, dental care, over-the-counter medications, first aid supplies, and medical copays. Output a balance alert and an eligible spending checklist."

13. **Network Provider Verification:** "Using the health insurance plan details from vault/insurance/00_current/, verify that my primary care physician, specialists, and preferred hospital are in-network for the current plan year. Out-of-network care can cost 2–4× more than in-network care, and balance billing from out-of-network providers can result in bills of thousands of dollars above the out-of-pocket maximum. Flag any provider in my regular care network that is not confirmed as in-network for the current year. Output a provider network status table with action items for any out-of-network flags."

## Life Insurance

14. **Life Insurance Policy Review:** "Pull life insurance details from vault/insurance/00_current/ and conduct a full policy review. Show: insurer, policy type (term/whole/universal), face value, annual premium, policy term and expiration date, current cash value if applicable (whole/universal), named beneficiaries and contingent beneficiaries, and any riders (disability waiver, accelerated death benefit, etc.). Flag: (1) if beneficiary designations are not updated after major life events (marriage, divorce, birth of child); (2) if the policy term expires before the youngest dependent reaches 22; (3) if no secondary beneficiary (contingent beneficiary) is named. Output a policy detail summary and a flag list."

15. **Term vs. Permanent Insurance Analysis:** "Using income, assets, dependents, and current policy details from vault/insurance/00_current/, analyze whether my current mix of term and permanent life insurance is appropriate for my situation. Term insurance is generally recommended when the coverage need is temporary (income replacement during working years, mortgage payoff, child-raising years). Permanent insurance may be appropriate if: I have a permanent coverage need (estate planning, business succession, lifelong dependent), or if I have maxed all other tax-advantaged savings vehicles. Show: current coverage mix, total annual premium, coverage gap to 10–12× income target, and a recommendation. Output a structured recommendation with rationale."

## Auto Insurance

16. **Auto Policy Deep Dive:** "Pull auto insurance details from vault/insurance/00_current/ and conduct a full coverage review. For each insured vehicle show: vehicle (year, make, model), liability limits (bodily injury per person / per accident, property damage), collision deductible, comprehensive deductible, uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage, medical payments or personal injury protection, annual premium, and renewal date. Check: (1) is liability coverage at least 100/300/100? — minimum state limits leave significant personal asset exposure; (2) is collision coverage worth keeping on older vehicles (if vehicle value is below $4,000, dropping collision and comprehensive saves premium cost that likely exceeds the coverage benefit); (3) is the uninsured motorist limit equal to the bodily injury liability limit? Output a per-vehicle coverage table and recommendations."

17. **Mileage and Usage Update:** "Review the mileage estimate on my auto insurance policy in vault/insurance/00_current/ and compare it to actual annual mileage. Insurers base premiums partly on annual mileage — if I am driving significantly less than the stated estimate (e.g., remote work reduced commuting), I may qualify for a lower rate tier or a usage-based insurance program (pay-per-mile). Many insurers offer usage-based programs (Progressive Snapshot, Allstate Drivewise, Root) that can save 10–30% for low-mileage or safe drivers. Flag if stated policy mileage is more than 3,000 miles above estimated actual mileage. Output a mileage comparison and a recommendation to contact the insurer."

## Home and Renters Insurance

18. **Dwelling Coverage Adequacy:** "Using the home insurance policy from vault/insurance/00_current/ and current home details, verify that dwelling coverage reflects the current replacement cost — not market value. Replacement cost is the cost to rebuild the home from the ground up at current construction costs, which often differs significantly from market value. Key benchmarks: average construction cost per square foot varies by region ($100–$300+/sq ft). Flag if dwelling coverage is more than 20% below estimated replacement cost. Also check: is there an inflation guard or extended replacement cost endorsement? Without it, a significant gap can develop over time as construction costs rise. Output a dwelling coverage adequacy assessment."

19. **Liability Limit Benchmark:** "Review liability limits across all applicable policies in vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, and vault/insurance/00_current/. The recommended minimum standalone liability for home and auto is $300,000 each; with an umbrella policy, the underlying limits can remain at $300,000 while the umbrella provides excess coverage up to $1–5M. Check: (1) are the underlying home and auto liability limits high enough to trigger the umbrella policy if needed? (most umbrellas require at least $250,000–$300,000 underlying); (2) does the total liability tower (home + auto + umbrella) equal or exceed my estimated net worth? Output a liability tower summary and flag any gap."

## Claims

20. **Claims Log Review:** "Pull all insurance claims history from vault/insurance/ and provide a claims summary. For each claim: policy type, date of claim, description, claim amount, settlement amount, and claim status (open/closed). Flag any open claims and note required follow-up actions. Assess claims history impact on renewal premiums: most insurers surcharge or non-renew after 2+ claims within 3 years. If I have 2 or more claims in the last 3 years on any single policy, flag the risk of premium increase or non-renewal at next renewal. Output a claims log table and a premium-impact assessment."

21. **Claims Preparation Guide:** "For a potential or recent insurance claim, walk me through the documentation needed to maximize claim settlement efficiency. For property claims (home, renters, auto): photograph all damage before any repairs, get 2–3 repair estimates, document all losses with receipts or replacement cost estimates, track all additional living expenses if displaced. For auto claims: exchange insurance information and photograph the scene, file a police report if required (typically for damage over $1,000 or any injury), notify insurer within 24–48 hours. For health claims: verify the claim was submitted in-network, check the Explanation of Benefits for accuracy, appeal denials within 30–60 days (most plans require written appeal). Output a claim-type-specific documentation checklist."

## Open Loops

22. **Insurance Open Loops Review:** "Scan all open loops, pending renewals, unfiled claims, and coverage gap items across vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, vault/insurance/00_current/, and vault/insurance/00_current/. 23. **Premium Optimization Review:** "Using all policy details from vault/insurance/, identify opportunities to reduce total annual insurance spend without reducing material coverage. Check: (1) multi-policy bundling discount — are auto and home/renters with the same insurer? Bundling typically saves 5–15%; (2) deductible optimization — could increasing deductibles from $500 to $1,000 or $2,500 on policies I have never claimed reduce premiums meaningfully (only if emergency fund covers the higher deductible); (3) any unused riders or endorsements paying for coverage not needed; (4) employer benefits — does my employer offer voluntary life, disability, or legal insurance at group rates cheaper than individual market; (5) annual payment discount — paying annual vs. monthly typically saves 3–5%. Estimate total annual savings for each opportunity. Output a ranked savings opportunity list."

24. **Disability Insurance Gap Check:** "Assess whether I have adequate disability insurance coverage — the most commonly overlooked coverage for working-age adults. Short-term disability: covers the first 90–180 days of inability to work. Long-term disability: typically covers 60–70% of gross income beyond the short-term period, commonly until age 65. Check vault/insurance/ for any disability policies. If no individual long-term disability policy is in force, flag this as a critical gap: a 35-year-old has a 25% chance of being disabled for 90+ days during their working career. If an employer group LTD policy is in force, note: group LTD benefits are taxable if the employer paid the premium, reducing the net benefit to approximately 45–50% of gross income. Recommend individual LTD coverage to supplement if applicable."

25. **Insurance Document Vault Check:** "Audit the completeness of insurance documentation across vault/insurance/. For each policy in force, verify that the following are stored: current declarations page (dec page), policy number and contact number for claims, coverage limits summary, beneficiary designations (for life), and last renewal invoice. Flag any policy where documentation is incomplete or the dec page is more than 13 months old (suggesting renewal was processed but updated docs were not saved). Output a documentation completeness checklist per policy category."
