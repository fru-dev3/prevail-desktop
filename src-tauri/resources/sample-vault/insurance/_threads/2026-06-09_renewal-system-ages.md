## You

My Allstate homeowners renews 2026-09-15. The HVAC is a 2016 Carrier unit that's near end of life, and the roof is from 2018. Will those ages hurt the renewal?

## claude · opus-4

They are the two systems carriers look at most, so it is worth getting ahead of both.

- Roof (2018): at 8 years it is still in a normal range. In Austin hail country it becomes a bigger factor around 12 to 15 years, but it should not trigger a problem at this renewal.
- HVAC (2016): this is the one that matters now. At end of life it is exactly the kind of component an inspection or surcharge can flag, and an unexpected failure is also a potential claim path.

Your dwelling coverage is $485,000 with a $2,500 deductible and a separate Texas wind/hail deductible of $4,850, premium $2,150/yr. None of that changes just from system age, but a renewal inspection could ask for a repair condition or nudge the premium.

## You

So should I replace the HVAC before the renewal?

## claude · opus-4

Yes, and the timing lines up well. Replacing the Carrier unit (about $9,000, tracked in the wealth domain) before the 2026-09-15 renewal does two things:

1. Removes the most likely aging-systems flag ahead of any renewal inspection.
2. Avoids a peak-summer emergency failure in July or August, which is both a comfort and a claims-risk issue.

In the wealth domain the recommendation is already to fund the HVAC from the emergency fund or a short auto-invest pause, not from investments, and the $1M umbrella is a separate, cheaper item that does not compete with it. So replacing the HVAC now is consistent across both domains.

## You

Anything else to do before September?

## claude · opus-4

A short pre-renewal checklist:

- Document the new HVAC install (date, model, invoice) and keep the roof age on file. Replaced systems can help, not just hurt.
- Reconfirm the $4,850 Texas wind/hail deductible is still the right tradeoff against the premium.
- Ask Allstate about bundling: your auto (GEICO) renews first on 2026-07-01, and moving auto to Allstate or confirming a multi-policy discount could offset any home increase. Compare against staying with GEICO.
- Add the $1M umbrella now rather than waiting for the home renewal. It sits above your auto and homeowners liability limits and is the current coverage gap.

I have logged the HVAC documentation and the umbrella as open items, both cross-referenced to the wealth domain.
