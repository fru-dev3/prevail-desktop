# AI Ready Life: Vision — Prompt Reference

## Monthly Scorecard

1. **Life Domain Scorecard:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for my current domain scores and vault/vision/00_current/ for my annual targets. Score each of the 8 life domains on a 1–10 scale: Health, Wealth, Career, Relationships, Finances, Home, Learning, and Personal Growth. For each domain, justify the score with 2–3 specific pieces of evidence from vault data — not vague feelings. Flag any domain scoring below 5 as a system drain, since low-energy domains reduce capacity in adjacent domains. Calculate the overall life score as the simple average of all 8 domains and compare to last month's score. Output a full scorecard table with month-over-month delta and the single lowest-scoring domain highlighted for focused attention."

2. **Monthly Vision Brief:** "Generate a concise monthly vision brief using vault/vision/00_current/, vault/vision/00_current/, and vault/vision/00_current/ as sources. Structure it in four sections: (1) Life score summary — current 8-domain average and the highest and lowest scoring domains. (2) Quarterly OKR status — each active key result on a 0.0–1.0 progress scale. (3) Annual goal health — any annual goal currently flagged as off-track. (4) Top priority — the single focus that will have the highest cross-domain impact this month. Keep the brief under 350 words and close with a 'Monthly Intention' — one sentence capturing the theme for the month ahead."

3. **Domain Energy Audit:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for my domain scores and vault/vision/00_current/ for my stated priorities. For each of the 8 domains, assess energy direction: is this domain energizing me (net positive), neutral, or draining me (net negative)? Domains scoring below 5 and trending downward are active drains on the whole system — research on psychological well-being shows that chronic low-scoring domains suppress performance in adjacent domains by 15–30%. Identify the domain with the steepest negative trend over the past 90 days and output a 3-step intervention plan to move it from draining to neutral within 60 days."

## Quarterly Planning

4. **OKR Draft for Upcoming Quarter:** "Using vault/vision/00_current/ for my annual objectives and vault/vision/00_current/ for prior quarter results, draft OKRs for the next quarter. Each Objective should be qualitative and inspiring — a state of being or achievement, not a task. Each Objective should have exactly 3 Key Results that are quantitative, measurable on a 0.0–1.0 scale, and binary done-or-not-done in nature. Apply the 70% rule: if 100% achievement of every KR feels certain, the targets are too conservative — a well-calibrated OKR should feel like a stretch. Output the draft OKRs per domain and flag any annual goal from vault/vision/00_current/ that has no corresponding quarterly KR — these are vision-to-action gaps."

5. **Quarterly OKR Progress Review:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for the current quarter's active OKRs. For each Key Result, assess progress on a 0.0–1.0 scale as of today. Calculate days elapsed in the quarter as a percentage of total quarter days — this is your expected progress baseline. Flag any KR where actual progress is more than 20 percentage points below the time-elapsed baseline as off-track. For off-track KRs, output a recovery analysis: what would need to happen in the remaining days to reach 0.7 (the minimum meaningful outcome), and is that recovery feasible given current pace? Output a full OKR progress dashboard with status per KR."

6. **Quarter-End OKR Retrospective:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for the completed quarter's OKRs. For each Key Result record: final score (0.0–1.0), key factor that drove success or shortfall, and one learning to carry into the next quarter. Calculate the quarter's average KR score — a healthy average is 0.6–0.7; consistently above 0.9 indicates targets are too easy; consistently below 0.4 indicates targets are too ambitious or resources are misaligned. Categorize each KR as Won (≥0.7), Partial (0.4–0.69), or Miss (<0.4). Output the retrospective table and draft 2–3 process improvements to apply in the upcoming quarter based on the patterns observed."

7. **Quarterly Focus Prioritization:** "Using vault/vision/00_current/ for annual objectives and vault/vision/00_current/ for current domain scores, identify the single highest-leverage quarterly focus area. Apply the constraint that effort is finite — spreading focus evenly across all domains typically yields mediocre results in all. Use a priority scoring framework: weight domain current score (inverse — lower score = higher priority = 30%), annual goal proximity to deadline (30%), and cross-domain multiplier effect (a win in this domain lifts 2+ other domains = 40%). Output the top priority domain and a one-paragraph explanation of why this is the highest-leverage investment for the next 90 days."

## Annual Review

8. **Annual Goal Health Check:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for all stated annual goals. For each goal calculate: percent of year elapsed, estimated progress toward goal completion (0–100%), and projected year-end achievement at current pace using linear extrapolation. Flag any goal where projected year-end achievement is below 60% — these are at-risk annual goals. For at-risk goals, apply the SMART criteria retrospectively: is the goal Specific enough to measure, Measurable with a clear metric, Achievable given current resources, Relevant to my core priorities, and Time-bound with an internal deadline? Flag any SMART criterion that is weak and recommend a goal rewrite for the at-risk items."

9. **Annual Theme Alignment Check:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for my stated annual theme — the one word or phrase that captures the year's intention. Review all active quarterly OKRs in vault/vision/00_current/ and my top projects. For each OKR and project, assess whether it advances the annual theme (aligned), is neutral to it (acceptable), or contradicts it (misaligned). Flag any significant time investment that contradicts the annual theme — these represent attention leaks. Calculate an alignment score: (aligned + neutral items) ÷ total items × 100%. Flag scores below 70% as a theme drift problem and recommend 1–2 specific re-alignments."

10. **Year-to-Date Accomplishment Ledger:** "Compile a year-to-date accomplishment ledger using vault/vision/00_current/ for completed KRs, vault/vision/00_current/ for domain score improvements, and cross-references to other vaults for supporting evidence. For each life domain, list: top 3 concrete accomplishments with evidence, biggest lesson or insight, and biggest setback with what was learned from it. Calculate: total KRs completed at ≥0.7, total domain score improvement since January 1, and the single accomplishment with the highest cross-domain positive impact. Output the ledger in a structured format suitable for reflection, with a one-paragraph personal narrative at the end."

## Goal Tracking

11. **Stalled Goal Intervention:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ and vault/vision/00_current/ for all active goals and their associated quarterly KRs. Identify any goal where the associated KR has scored below 0.3 for two consecutive quarters — this is a persistently stalled goal. For each stalled goal conduct a root-cause analysis using five categories: (1) Insufficient motivation — does this goal still genuinely matter? (2) Skill gap — is a missing capability blocking execution? (3) Resource gap — is the bottleneck time, money, or tools? (4) Bad leading indicators — am I tracking the right behaviors? (5) Environmental barrier — does my context make execution harder than it needs to be? Output a diagnosis and a specific reframe or action for each stalled goal."

12. **Goal-to-Action Bridge Audit:** "For each annual goal in vault/vision/00_current/, verify that a concrete action bridge exists. Apply the bridge requirement: every annual goal must have an active quarterly KR in vault/vision/00_current/ that moves it forward, every quarterly KR must have at least one weekly behavior or habit that generates measurable progress, and every weekly behavior must have a scheduled time block. Flag any annual goal with no active quarterly KR as a vision orphan. Flag any quarterly KR with no weekly behavior defined as an execution gap. Output a bridge audit table showing each annual goal, its quarterly KR, and its weekly behavior — with gaps highlighted."

13. **Leading vs. Lagging Indicator Review:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ and vault/vision/00_current/ for all active goals and KRs. For each KR, classify the metric being tracked as a leading indicator (a behavior I control — e.g., 'exercise 4×/week') or a lagging indicator (an outcome I influence — e.g., 'lose 20 lbs'). Flag any KR that tracks only lagging indicators — without leading indicators, there is no early-warning system for course correction. For each lagging-only KR, suggest 2 specific leading indicators to track that have a documented causal relationship with the lagging outcome. Output a revised KR list with leading indicators added for all flagged items."

14. **Milestone Log and Celebration Capture:** "Log a completed milestone to vault/vision/00_current/. For each milestone capture: the goal it advances, the milestone description, completion date, what made it possible (the key enabling factor), and how it changes the projected year-end outcome for the associated annual goal. After logging, recalculate the KR progress score for the associated objective. If the milestone completion brings a KR to 0.7 or above, flag the KR as Won and output a brief celebration note — research shows that acknowledging wins activates the dopamine loop that sustains long-term goal pursuit. Output the updated KR dashboard."

## Domain Scoring

15. **Health Domain Deep Dive:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for my current health domain score and vault/vision/00_current/ for any health-related annual goals. Justify the current score with evidence across 4 health dimensions: physical fitness (exercise consistency), nutrition and weight, sleep quality and duration, and mental health and stress. For each dimension, identify one leading indicator I am tracking and whether it is trending up, flat, or down. Apply the compound effect principle: health improvements have a documented positive spillover into career performance (up to 20% productivity gain) and relationship quality. Flag any dimension with a downward trend for 30+ days as a priority intervention. Output a health domain brief with a 30-day improvement focus."

16. **Wealth Domain Scorecard:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for my current wealth domain score and cross-reference wealth data from vault/. Score the wealth domain across 4 sub-dimensions: net worth growth rate (target: outpace inflation by at least 3%), savings rate as a percentage of gross income (target: minimum 20%), investment return performance vs. benchmark, and financial runway in months (liquid assets ÷ monthly expenses). Calculate a composite wealth sub-score for each and average for the overall wealth domain score. Flag any sub-dimension below a healthy threshold and output a wealth domain health brief with the single highest-leverage action to improve the score in the next 90 days."

17. **Career Domain Scorecard:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for my current career domain score and vault/vision/00_current/ for career-related annual goals. Score the career domain across 4 sub-dimensions: role fulfillment (1–10 satisfaction), market value trajectory (compensation vs. market rate), skill development pace (are high-demand skills being added), and career capital accumulation (experiences, credentials, and network building toward target role). Flag any sub-dimension below 6 as an active career risk. Calculate the composite career domain score and compare to last quarter. Output a career domain brief and identify the single sub-dimension where a 90-day focused effort would have the largest career capital impact."

## Values Alignment

18. **Values Alignment Audit:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for my stated core values and all active quarterly OKRs in vault/vision/00_current/. For each OKR, assess whether it expresses, ignores, or conflicts with my stated values. A value-expressive goal provides intrinsic motivation and sustains effort through difficulty; a value-conflicting goal creates psychological friction that drains willpower. Flag any active KR that conflicts with a stated core value — these are the most likely candidates for underperformance despite apparent effort. Output a values-to-goals alignment matrix and recommend whether to: reframe the conflicting goal, pause it, or remove it and replace with a value-aligned alternative."

19. **Values Drift Detection:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for my stated core values and vault/vision/00_current/ for the past 3 months of domain scores and quarterly OKR choices. Identify patterns that reveal values drift — cases where my actual time and energy allocation (evidenced by domain scores and KR choices) diverges from my stated values. For example, if I state that family is a core value but the Relationships domain consistently scores below 6 while Career consistently scores above 8, this signals a values-stated vs. values-lived misalignment. Output a values drift analysis with specific evidence for each detected misalignment and a rebalancing recommendation."

20. **Values-Based Decision Filter:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for my stated core values and apply them as a decision filter to a current decision I am facing (state the decision). Evaluate each option against each core value: does this option express the value (score +1), is it neutral (score 0), or does it conflict with the value (score -1)? Sum the scores across all values for each option. This is not a mechanical algorithm — use the scores as a starting point for reflection and flag any option that scores highly on most values but conflicts sharply with one core value (these are the hardest and most important trade-offs). Output the values decision matrix and a recommendation with the most important tension clearly named."

## Strategy

21. **Highest-Leverage Focus Finder:** "Using vault/vision/00_current/ for domain scores and vault/vision/00_current/ for annual goals, identify the one focus area that will generate the highest cross-domain impact in the next 90 days. Apply the constraint multiplier test: which domain, if improved from its current score to a 7+, would unlock progress in the most other domains? (Health unlocking career and relationship capacity is a common example; financial stability unlocking risk-taking in career and relationships is another.) Score each domain on its cross-domain multiplier potential and output a ranked priority list with justification. State the single recommended focus and the expected cross-domain ripple effects."

22. **Time Allocation vs. Priority Alignment:** "Using vault/vision/00_current/ for stated priorities and vault/vision/00_current/ for domain scores, estimate my current time allocation across the 8 life domains based on logged activities and domain scores as proxies. Compare actual time allocation to my stated priority ranking. Flag any mismatch where a stated top-3 priority domain is receiving bottom-3 time allocation — this is the most common cause of vision-reality gaps. Output a time allocation vs. stated priority comparison table and calculate the reallocation needed to bring the top 2 priority domains to at least 30% combined share of discretionary time."

23. **Constraint Analysis — What Is the Limiting Factor:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/, vault/vision/00_current/, and vault/vision/00_current/ to identify the single binding constraint on overall life progress — the one bottleneck that, if removed, would accelerate progress in the most domains simultaneously. Apply the Theory of Constraints principle: the system's throughput is determined by its weakest link. Evaluate common life constraints: time (scheduling and capacity), energy (health and recovery), clarity (uncertain goals or values), skills (capability gaps), relationships (network limitations), and capital (financial resources). Identify the primary constraint, estimate its impact across domains, and output a focused constraint-removal plan for the next 30 days."

24. **Annual Theme Setting Workshop:** "Lead me through an annual theme-setting process using vault/vision/00_current/ and vault/vision/00_current/ as context. Step 1: Review last year's theme and assess whether I lived it or drifted from it. Step 2: Identify the 3 life domains most in need of attention in the year ahead based on domain scores and life-stage priorities. Step 3: Identify the common thread — the meta-quality or posture that would advance all 3 priority domains simultaneously. Step 4: Distill that thread into a single word or short phrase that is evocative, personal, and actionable enough to serve as a filter for decisions throughout the year. Output the proposed annual theme with a 3-sentence rationale and 5 specific decision examples where the theme would serve as a useful filter."

## Open Loops

25. **Vision Open Loops Triage:** "Scan vault/vision/ across all subdirectories for any flagged action items, incomplete planning entries, or unresolved decisions. Categorize each by type: (1) Stalled KR — a quarterly key result with no progress logged in more than 14 days, (2) Vision orphan — an annual goal with no active quarterly KR, (3) Missing scorecard update — a domain score not updated in more than 30 days, (4) Retrospective gap — a completed quarter with no retrospective documented, (5) Values conflict — an active OKR flagged as conflicting with a stated core value. Output a triage list sorted by impact on overall vision health, with estimated resolution time per item."

26. **Vision System Health Check:** "Perform a structural audit of the vision vault: vault/vision/00_current/, vault/vision/00_current/, and vault/vision/00_current/. Check: (1) All 8 life domains have a score updated within the last 30 days. (2) Every annual goal has at least one active quarterly KR. (3) Current quarter OKRs have been reviewed and scored in the last 14 days. (4) Last quarter's retrospective is documented in vault/vision/00_current/. (5) My stated annual theme has been applied as a filter to at least one decision this month. Score each check Pass/Fail and calculate an overall vision system health score as (passes ÷ 5) × 100%. Output the scorecard with specific remediation steps for each failure."

27. **Cross-Domain Goal Conflict Detector:** "Read vault/vision/00_current/ for all annual goals across all life domains. Identify any goals that are structurally in tension with each other — where fully achieving Goal A makes it harder to achieve Goal B. Common examples: an aggressive career advancement goal conflicts with a relationship depth goal if both require significant time investment; a wealth accumulation goal conflicts with a personal freedom goal if wealth requires high-hour employment. For each detected conflict, assess severity (minor tension vs. fundamental trade-off) and output a conflict register with a recommended resolution: modify one goal, sequence them, or explicitly accept the trade-off and build it into quarterly planning."
