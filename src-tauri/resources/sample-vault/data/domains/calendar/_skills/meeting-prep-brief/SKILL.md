# Meeting prep brief

Run the hour before a meeting that actually matters.

1. **The point.** From the invite on data/calendar.ics and prior threads, state the one decision or outcome this meeting must produce. If there isn't one, it should have been a memo.
2. **Context in hand.** Pull the relevant history — last meeting's notes, open loops, the numbers that will come up — so you aren't reconstructing it live.
3. **Your position.** Write where you stand and the one thing you need from the room. Decide your position before, not during.
4. **Open questions.** List the two or three questions only this meeting can answer. Lead with them so the time isn't eaten by status.

Output: a one-screen brief — the decision, your position, the questions, and your single ask.
