## You

We're planning a Phoenix, AZ long weekend in mid-July - Alex, Jordan, and Maya (6). It doubles as a check-in on the rental condo. Budget is $1,800 for the whole trip. Help me plan it.

## claude · opus-4

Phoenix in July is hot, but a long weekend works well if you plan around the heat and use the trip to check on the condo. Here's how I'd structure the $1,800:

**Budget breakdown:**
| Category | Est. Cost |
|---|---|
| Flights (3 pax, AUS->PHX round trip) | $600-900 |
| Lodging (3 nights, family-friendly with a pool) | $480-600 |
| Food (3 days, mix of dining + grocery) | $280-320 |
| Activities + transit | $120-160 |
| Buffer | $100-120 |
| **Total** | **$1,580-2,100** |

You're workable at $1,800 - book flights before they drift, and pick a place with a pool to keep Maya happy in the heat.

**The rental check-in:** Coordinate with Sun Valley PM (your 8% manager) before you go so you can do a quick walkthrough of the condo while the lease is mid-term (runs through 2027-02). Keep receipts - a check-in visit may make part of the trip a deductible Schedule E expense; ask Maria how much qualifies.

**What to do with Maya (heat-aware):**
- **Mornings outdoors, afternoons indoors.** Plan the active stuff before 10am.
- **Phoenix Zoo** - go early; ~$30 adult, less for kids, and the splash areas help.
- **Arizona Science Center** - a great air-conditioned afternoon a 6-year-old will love.
- **Pool time at the rental** - the cheapest and most reliable Maya-pleaser.

**Logistics:** Mid-July is peak heat (110F+), so hydrate hard and never leave anyone in a parked car. Schedule the condo walkthrough for a morning so the afternoon is free for the pool.
