# AI Ready Life: Wealth — Prompt Reference

## Weekly & Monthly Reviews

1. **Weekly Wealth Pulse:** "Run my weekly wealth pulse check. Scan vault/wealth/00_current/ for any account balance changes since last Monday and flag any unexplained movement >$500 in a single account. Check vault/wealth/00_current/cash-flow.md for any spending category that has exceeded its monthly budget by >20%. Review vault/wealth/00_current/ for any position that has moved >5% in price since last week. Output a brief covering: account balance snapshot, budget alerts, investment moves to watch, and any pending transfers or scheduled payments in the next 7 days."

2. **Monthly Wealth Review:** "Run my monthly wealth review. Read vault/wealth/00_current/ for all statements issued this month and extract ending balances for each account. Compute net worth as total assets minus total liabilities using vault/wealth/00_current/net-worth.md. Compare this month's net worth to last month and to 12 months ago. Review vault/wealth/00_current/cash-flow.md for savings rate (target ≥20% of gross income). Flag any liability that grew rather than shrank month-over-month. Output a structured brief: Net Worth Summary, Cash Flow Analysis, Investment Snapshot, and 3 Priority Actions."

3. **Wealth Quarterly Brief:** "Generate my quarterly wealth brief covering the past 90 days. Aggregate net worth data from vault/wealth/00_current/net-worth.md for each month in the quarter and plot the trajectory. Compute: savings rate for the quarter, investment account growth rate, debt reduction progress, and year-to-date progress toward each savings goal in vault/wealth/00_current/goals.md. Compare actual vs. target for each metric. Flag any goal where current pace puts me >10% behind target by year-end. Output an executive summary, a metrics table, and a prioritized action plan for next quarter."

## Net Worth

4. **Net Worth Snapshot:** "Calculate my current net worth by reading vault/wealth/00_current/net-worth.md. Sum all assets: checking, savings, brokerage, retirement accounts (401k, Roth IRA, HSA), real estate equity (market value minus mortgage balance), and vehicle equity. Sum all liabilities: mortgage balance, auto loans, student loans, credit card balances, and any other outstanding debt. Compute net worth = total assets − total liabilities. Compare to last month and last year. Flag any asset that declined >5% or any liability that grew. Output a balance sheet table and a one-paragraph narrative on the overall trajectory."

5. **Net Worth Trend Analysis:** "Pull net worth snapshots from vault/wealth/00_current/net-worth.md for the past 12 months (or as far back as data exists). Compute month-over-month change and 12-month cumulative change. Identify the single largest driver of net worth growth (e.g., market appreciation, debt paydown, savings contributions) and the single largest drag. Calculate the annualized growth rate and project net worth 5 years forward at the current rate. Flag if the 5-year projection falls short of my FIRE number (25× annual expenses). Output a trend table and a forward projection with low/base/high scenario estimates."

6. **Debt-to-Asset Ratio Review:** "Read vault/wealth/00_current/net-worth.md and compute the debt-to-asset ratio (total liabilities ÷ total assets). Compare to prior quarter. Break down liabilities by type: mortgage, auto, student, and revolving credit. Flag if revolving credit balances have increased month-over-month (signal of lifestyle drift). For each debt category, report the current balance, interest rate, and minimum monthly payment. Identify which debts qualify for avalanche payoff priority (highest APR first) and compute the total interest saved if accelerated payoff is applied to the highest-rate balance."

## Cash Flow

7. **Cash Flow & Budget Variance Report:** "Analyze my cash flow for the current month using vault/wealth/00_current/cash-flow.md. For each spending category, compare actual spend to the monthly budget. Flag any category where actual spend exceeds budget by >20% as a 'Yellow Alert' and any exceeding budget by >50% as a 'Red Alert.' Compute total income, total expenses, and net monthly cash flow (surplus or deficit). Calculate my savings rate: (gross income − total expenses) ÷ gross income × 100. Flag if savings rate falls below 15%. Output a budget variance table and a cash flow summary."

8. **Savings Rate Optimization:** "Read vault/wealth/00_current/cash-flow.md and compute my current savings rate (total monthly savings ÷ gross monthly income). Break savings into buckets: emergency fund, retirement accounts (401k, Roth IRA), taxable investments, and ESPP. Identify the largest discretionary spending categories and estimate the savings rate improvement if each were reduced by 10%. Calculate how many months it would take to reach my emergency fund target (3–6× monthly expenses) at the current savings rate vs. an accelerated rate. Output a savings rate dashboard and a 3-scenario projection."

9. **Spending Category Deep Dive:** "Review the past 3 months of spending data in vault/wealth/00_current/cash-flow.md and identify the top 5 expense categories by total spend. For each, compute the 3-month average, the highest single-month spend, and the trend (rising/flat/declining). Flag any category showing a rising trend for 2+ consecutive months. Cross-reference any category marked as discretionary and estimate the annual cost run rate. For each flagged category, suggest a specific reduction target and the annual savings impact. Output a category analysis table and a list of the 3 highest-impact cost-reduction opportunities."

## Investments

10. **Portfolio Allocation Drift Check:** "Read vault/wealth/00_current/ for my current brokerage and retirement account holdings. Extract the current market value and percentage allocation for each asset class (US equity, international equity, bonds, cash, alternatives). Compare current allocation to my target allocation documented in vault/wealth/00_current/target-allocation.md. Flag any asset class that has drifted >5% from target. Identify the specific buy/sell actions needed to rebalance. Compute the tax impact of rebalancing in taxable vs. tax-advantaged accounts. Output: current vs. target allocation table, drift flags, and rebalancing instructions."

11. **Investment Performance Review:** "Analyze my investment account performance for the current quarter using vault/wealth/00_current/ and vault/wealth/00_current/. For each account (401k, Roth IRA, brokerage, HSA), compute: beginning balance, ending balance, contributions in the period, and net investment gain/loss. Calculate the time-weighted return for the period and compare to relevant benchmarks (S&P 500 total return, total bond market return). Flag any account where my return trails the benchmark by >2 percentage points. Output a performance summary table with a brief narrative on relative performance drivers."

12. **ESPP Analysis & Decision:** "Review my ESPP enrollment and purchase history in vault/wealth/00_current/ or vault/benefits/00_current/. Extract: current contribution rate (%), offering period start/end dates, purchase price discount (target 15%), lookback provision details, and number of shares purchased in the most recent period. Calculate the guaranteed return at purchase assuming a 15% discount with lookback. Flag any disqualifying disposition risk: shares sold within 2 years of offering date or 1 year of purchase date are taxed as ordinary income rather than capital gains. Recommend: hold vs. immediate sell decision with tax implications for each path. Output a decision brief with net after-tax return estimates."

13. **RSU Vesting Schedule & Tax Planning:** "Read my RSU grant details in vault/wealth/00_current/ or vault/benefits/00_current/. For each grant, extract: grant date, total shares granted, vesting schedule (cliff and ratable), shares vested to date, shares unvested, current stock price, and fair market value at each vest date. RSUs are taxed as ordinary income at vest — calculate the estimated tax liability for each vesting event in the next 12 months. Flag any single vest event exceeding $10,000 in income as requiring estimated payment adjustment. Recommend a hold vs. sell strategy for vested shares based on concentration risk (flag if RSU stock >10% of portfolio). Output a vesting schedule table and tax planning summary."

## Retirement

14. **Retirement Contribution Tracker:** "Read vault/wealth/00_current/ and vault/wealth/00_current/ for all retirement account contribution data. For each account (401k, Roth IRA, HSA), compute: YTD contributions, employer contributions (if applicable), total YTD, and the 2025 IRS limit (401k: $23,500 / $31,000 if 50+; Roth IRA: $7,000 / $8,000 if 50+; HSA: $4,300 individual / $8,550 family). Calculate the monthly contribution needed to max each account by December 31. Flag any account where I am on pace to under-contribute by more than $500. Output a contribution tracker table and a 'gap to max' analysis."

15. **Retirement On-Track Analysis:** "Using vault/wealth/00_current/ retirement account balances and vault/wealth/00_current/goals.md, assess whether I am on track for my retirement target. Apply the 25× rule: FIRE number = 25 × annual expenses. Compare current retirement portfolio value to age-based milestones (commonly: 1× salary by 30, 2× by 35, 3× by 40, 4× by 45, 6× by 50, 7× by 55, 8× by 60, 10× by 67). Compute projected balance at target retirement age using a 7% real return assumption. Flag if projected balance at retirement is <80% of FIRE number. Output: current balance, milestone comparison, projection at retirement age, and contribution increase needed to close any gap."

16. **Retirement Account Allocation Review:** "Read my retirement account holdings in vault/wealth/00_current/ for each tax-advantaged account (401k, Roth IRA). List all fund holdings with current market value, expense ratio, and percentage of the account. Flag any fund with an expense ratio >0.5% as a cost drag candidate (compare to a Vanguard or Fidelity index equivalent with <0.10% expense ratio). Assess overall equity/bond allocation against a glide path appropriate for my retirement horizon. Output: holdings table with expense flags, current allocation vs. target glide path, and specific fund swap recommendations with projected annual fee savings."

## Debt

17. **Debt Avalanche Payoff Plan:** "Read all debt entries in vault/wealth/00_current/net-worth.md. List each debt with: creditor, current balance, interest rate (APR), minimum monthly payment, and payoff date at minimum payment. Sort by APR descending to construct the avalanche payoff order. Calculate the total interest paid under the minimum-payment plan vs. the avalanche plan using a fixed extra monthly payment of $500. Show the payoff date and interest savings for each scenario. Flag any debt with APR >10% as high-priority. Output: debt summary table, avalanche order, and a monthly payment schedule for the top-priority debt."

18. **Mortgage Progress Review:** "Extract mortgage details from vault/wealth/00_current/net-worth.md or vault/wealth/00_current/. Report: current outstanding balance, original loan amount, interest rate, remaining term, monthly payment breakdown (principal vs. interest), and equity percentage (current equity ÷ estimated market value). Compute: how many months of extra principal payments would be needed to pay off the mortgage 5 years early, and the total interest saved by doing so. Flag if PMI is still being charged and compute when the 80% LTV threshold will be crossed at the current payoff pace. Output a mortgage summary and early-payoff scenario analysis."

## Savings Milestones & Goals

19. **Emergency Fund Status:** "Read vault/wealth/00_current/goals.md and vault/wealth/00_current/ for liquid savings account balances. Compute the emergency fund target: 3–6× monthly expenses (pull monthly expense total from vault/wealth/00_current/cash-flow.md). Compare the current liquid savings balance to the 3-month minimum and 6-month target. Calculate months of expenses currently covered and the dollar gap to each threshold. If below 3 months, calculate the monthly contribution needed to reach 3 months within 6 months. Output: current liquid savings, monthly expense base, coverage months, gap to target, and a savings ramp plan."

20. **Savings Goal Progress Tracker:** "Read all savings goals in vault/wealth/00_current/goals.md. For each goal, extract: goal name, target amount, current saved amount, target date, and monthly contribution rate. Compute: percentage of goal funded, months to completion at current rate, and whether current pace achieves the goal by the target date. Flag any goal that is >3 months behind schedule. For the top 3 underfunded goals, recommend a specific monthly contribution increase and the payoff date improvement. Output a goal tracker table sorted by urgency and a prioritized funding recommendation."

## Estate Planning

21. **Estate Document Audit:** "Read vault/wealth/00_current/ to audit the status and currency of all estate planning documents. For each document type — will, revocable trust, durable power of attorney, healthcare directive/living will, HIPAA authorization — confirm: document exists, date last signed, and whether it needs updating due to life changes (marriage, divorce, new child, change in assets >$100k, relocation to new state). Flag any document older than 5 years or missing entirely. Output a document status table with a recommended action for each item."

22. **Beneficiary Designation Review:** "Review all beneficiary designations across accounts in vault/wealth/00_current/ and vault/wealth/00_current/. For each account (401k, Roth IRA, life insurance, brokerage TOD, HSA, bank POD), extract: primary beneficiary name and percentage, contingent beneficiary name and percentage, and date last updated. Flag any account with no beneficiary designation, a designation that conflicts with the estate plan (e.g., ex-spouse still listed), or a primary beneficiary who is a minor with no trust named as successor. Output a beneficiary table and a list of update actions by account."

## Open Loops

23. **Wealth Open Loop Audit:** "Read vault/wealth/00_current/open-loops.md and cross-reference with vault/wealth/00_current/, vault/wealth/00_current/, and vault/wealth/00_current/ to validate each item's status. Classify each open loop as: Active (in progress), Stalled (no movement in >30 days), or Resolved (addressable now based on available data). Flag any stalled financial action item with a dollar value >$1,000 as high priority. Output a ranked open loop list with: description, age, status, financial impact estimate, and recommended next step."

24. **Financial Action Item Weekly Sweep:** "Scan vault/wealth/00_current/open-loops.md and vault/wealth/00_current/cash-flow.md for any action items due this week. Include: pending transfers to execute, bills due, investment contributions to make, documents to submit, and any accounts requiring attention (low balance, fraud alert, statement ready). For each item, confirm the due date and the financial amount at stake. Flag any item whose deadline passes without action as incurring a cost (interest charge, missed contribution deadline, late fee). Output a sorted action list with amounts and deadlines."
