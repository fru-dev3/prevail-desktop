## You

The next RSU vest is August 15 - the annual tranche, about $12,500 gross. What should I do with it after taxes?

## claude · opus-4

At the 22% supplemental withholding rate, you'd net roughly **$9,750** after federal withholding. (Texas has no state income tax, which helps.)

Here's how I'd think about it in priority order:

**1. Emergency fund first (if HVAC happened).** If you used the emergency fund to cover the HVAC, use part of the RSU proceeds to top it back toward $32,000. If June cash flow covered the HVAC, the fund stayed intact and you skip this step.

**2. Earmark the RSU shares themselves** - sell on vest day, don't hold. You already have $145k of W2 income tied to TechFlow. Holding the RSU shares adds concentration risk: your paycheck *and* your equity would decline together if the company has a bad year. Sell and diversify.

**3. Where the ~$9,750 goes:**
   - If the emergency fund is whole: put most of it into the taxable brokerage (VTI/BND per your current allocation) and route a slice toward bonds to nudge the allocation back to target.
   - If any HVAC outlay is still being rebuilt: refill the emergency fund toward $32,000 first, then invest the remainder.

**One tax note:** the 22% supplemental withholding is a flat rate and often under-withholds relative to your actual marginal rate (likely 22-24%). Worth running the numbers in November to see if a Q4 estimated payment is needed, especially with the consulting 1099 income on top.

## You

Should I put the brokerage money into the same VTI/BND split I already have?

## claude · opus-4

Yes, same split makes sense - VTI 80% / BND 20% keeps you on track toward the 75/20/5 target allocation.

One small nudge: your current allocation is 80% equities / 13% bonds / 7% cash. You're slightly over-weight equities and slightly under-weight bonds. Routing new brokerage contributions toward BND (or its equivalent, BNDX if you want international bond exposure) is the low-friction way to drift back toward target without selling anything.

So for the brokerage allocation: weight this tranche a bit more toward BND than your standing split - say a 70/30 VTI/BND mix for this tranche - to gently pull the overall toward 75/20 without rebalancing.

Don't overthink it - at your net worth (~$423,000), the allocation drift matters less than keeping the savings habit and avoiding single-stock concentration. The important thing is that the RSU gets sold and reinvested broadly rather than held or spent.
