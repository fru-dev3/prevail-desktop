# AI Ready Life: Records — Prompt Reference

## Monthly Reviews

1. **Records Monthly Review:** "Pull all document expiry data from vault/records/00_current/, subscription data from vault/records/00_current/, and legal document status from vault/records/00_current/ to produce my monthly records brief. For IDs, flag any document expiring within 6 months. For subscriptions, flag any renewing in the next 30 days that cost more than $50/year and has not been used in the past 30 days. For legal documents, check whether my will, POA, and healthcare directive have been reviewed since the last qualifying life event. Output a three-section brief with a combined count of urgent action items at the top."

2. **Records Brief:** "Generate a weekly records brief using vault/records/ as the source of truth. Structure it in four sections: (1) Expiring documents — any ID, license, or credential expiring within 90 days with days remaining and renewal lead time. (2) Subscription renewals — any auto-renewing subscription in the next 14 days with the annual cost. (3) Legal document flags — any document overdue for review based on life events. (4) Storage compliance — confirm that all critical documents satisfy the 2-location rule (one physical, one cloud). Keep the entire brief under 300 words and close with a single 'Top Action This Week' callout."

3. **Annual Document Audit:** "Conduct a full records audit across vault/records/00_current/, vault/records/00_current/, and vault/records/00_current/. For each category report: total items tracked, items requiring action in the next 90 days, items with incomplete metadata (missing expiry date, storage location, or last-reviewed date), and items violating the 2-location storage rule. Calculate a records completeness score as (items fully documented ÷ total items) × 100%. Flag the category with the lowest completeness score for immediate remediation. Output a full audit report with an action-item count per category."

## Expiring Documents

4. **Document Expiry Dashboard:** "Read vault/records/00_current/ and build a comprehensive expiry dashboard for all tracked government IDs and credentials. For each document calculate days remaining to expiry and apply these renewal lead-time benchmarks: U.S. Passport — begin renewal process 13 weeks before expiry (8 weeks if expediting); Global Entry — submit renewal 6 months before expiry; Driver's License — submit 30–60 days before expiry based on state rules; TSA PreCheck — renew up to 6 months early. Flag any document where days remaining is less than the recommended renewal lead time as Overdue to Act, within 2 weeks of lead time as Act Now, and within the lead-time window as On Radar. Output the dashboard sorted by urgency."

5. **Passport Validity Check for Travel:** "Read vault/records/00_current/ for my passport details — issue date, expiry date, and any visa pages. Many countries require 6 months of passport validity beyond the entry date — calculate the effective usable window. Flag if my passport will fall within the 6-month validity buffer during any upcoming travel logged in vault/records/. If the passport is expiring within 12 months, output the exact steps and timeline for renewal: standard processing is 6–8 weeks, expedited is 2–3 weeks, and appointment-based same-day service is available for emergencies. State the current State Department processing time from my last data point."

6. **Global Entry and Trusted Traveler Renewal Tracker:** "Read vault/records/00_current/ for my Global Entry, TSA PreCheck, and NEXUS enrollment details. Flag Global Entry as critical because renewal applications can take weeks to process, and the Known Traveler Number (KTN) must remain active for PreCheck to work. If Global Entry is within 6 months of expiry, output a step-by-step renewal checklist including interview scheduling. If TSA PreCheck is close to expiry, confirm whether renewing Global Entry automatically extends PreCheck benefits."

7. **REAL ID Compliance Check:** "Read vault/records/00_current/ for my driver's license details. Verify whether my license is REAL ID-compliant — indicated by a star in the upper corner. 8. **Driver's License Expiry and State Renewal Guide:** "Read vault/records/00_current/ for my driver's license expiry date and issuing state. Calculate days remaining. ## Subscriptions

9. **Subscription Spend Audit:** "Read vault/records/00_current/ and compile a full subscription ledger. Categorize each subscription by type: productivity, entertainment, software, professional, health, and home services. For each subscription report: name, monthly cost, annual cost, billing cycle, last-used date, and renewal date. Calculate total monthly subscription spend and total annual spend. Flag any subscription meeting two criteria: annual cost above $50 AND last used more than 30 days ago — these require explicit value justification or cancellation. Output a spend summary table by category and a list of flagged subscriptions with recommended action."

10. **Subscription Renewal Alert:** "Scan vault/records/00_current/ for any subscription with an auto-renewal date in the next 30 days. For each upcoming renewal report: service name, renewal date, annual cost, and whether it was used in the past 30 days. For subscriptions renewing in the next 7 days that cost more than $99/year and have not been used in 30 days, flag as urgent cancel candidates — most services require 24–48 hours notice before renewal to avoid charges. Output a renewal calendar table sorted by renewal date and include a cancel-or-keep recommendation for each flagged item."

11. **Subscription Value Justification Review:** "For every subscription in vault/records/00_current/ costing more than $50 per year, conduct a value justification assessment. Score each on three criteria: (1) Usage frequency — used weekly = 3, monthly = 2, rarely = 1, never = 0. (2) Replaceability — hard to replace = 3, alternatives exist = 2, free alternative exists = 1. (3) Impact if cancelled — high impact = 3, moderate = 2, low = 1. Subscriptions scoring below 4 total are cancellation candidates; 4–6 are review candidates; 7–9 are keep. Output a scored table, list all cancel candidates, and calculate the annual savings from removing them."

12. **Subscription Overlap Detector:** "Read vault/records/00_current/ and identify redundant subscriptions providing overlapping functionality. Common overlap categories: cloud storage (iCloud + Google Drive + Dropbox), music streaming (Spotify + Apple Music), video streaming (Netflix + Hulu + Disney+), password managers (1Password + LastPass), and productivity (Notion + Evernote + Roam). For each detected overlap, calculate the combined annual cost of redundant services and recommend which single service to consolidate to. Output an overlap report with estimated annual savings from consolidation."

For each document verify: the named agent is still the correct person and is willing to serve, the document was executed after my last move to a new state (POA and healthcare directive laws vary by state), the document is stored in at least two locations with one copy held by the named agent, and my physician has a copy of the healthcare directive on file. ## Storage and Security

18. **2-Location Storage Compliance Audit:** "Read vault/records/ across all subdirectories and verify compliance with the 2-location storage rule: every critical document must be stored in two physically separate locations — for example, a home safe plus an encrypted cloud folder, or a bank safe deposit box plus a trusted family member's secure storage. For each document in vault/records/00_current/ and vault/records/00_current/, check whether two storage locations are recorded. Calculate the compliance percentage: (documents with 2 locations ÷ total documents) × 100%. Flag any critical document — passport, will, POA — that is stored in only one location as high-priority non-compliant. Output a compliance report."

21. **Password and Account Security Audit:** "Read vault/records/00_current/ and vault/records/00_current/ for any accounts and services logged.
