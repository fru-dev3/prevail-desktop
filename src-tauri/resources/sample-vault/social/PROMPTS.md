# AI Ready Life: Social — Prompt Reference

## Monthly Reviews

1. **Relationship Monthly Review:** "Pull all contact records from vault/social/00_current/ and all outreach logs from vault/social/00_current/ to produce my monthly relationship health report. For each relationship tier, calculate: total contacts in tier, percentage of contacts touched within their check-in interval (Close = 14–30 days, Strong = 45–60 days, Active = 90 days, Dormant = 120+ days), and the number currently overdue for a check-in. Flag any contacts who have crossed into Dormant status this month — greater than 120 days since last interaction — as at-risk relationships requiring immediate attention. Output a tier health table followed by a ranked list of the top 5 most overdue contacts."

2. **Social Monthly Brief:** "Generate a monthly social brief using vault/social/00_current/ and vault/social/00_current/ as sources. Structure it in four sections: (1) Relationship tier health — contacts current vs. overdue per tier. (2) This month's outreach highlights — 3 most meaningful interactions logged. (3) Upcoming milestones — birthdays, work anniversaries, or major life events in the next 30 days. (4) Network diversity flag — if more than 70% of my strong-tier contacts are concentrated in a single employer or industry, flag as a network concentration risk. Keep the brief under 400 words and close with a 'Top 3 Outreaches This Week' callout."

3. **Quarterly Relationship Audit:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ and assess relationship trajectory for every contact in my Close and Strong tiers over the past 90 days. For each contact calculate: number of interactions, average days between interactions, and interaction depth trend (moving deeper or becoming more surface-level over time). Flag contacts where interaction frequency has decreased by more than 40% compared to the prior 90 days — these are relationship cooling signals. Produce a health trajectory table showing current tier, interaction frequency, and a trend direction indicator (warming/stable/cooling) for each contact."

## Outreach Queue

4. **Weekly Outreach Queue Builder:** "Using vault/social/00_current/ and the check-in interval rules — Close tier every 14–30 days, Strong every 45–60 days, Active every 90 days — identify every contact who is overdue for a check-in as of today. Rank the overdue list by days overdue weighted by tier priority: Close contacts score 3x, Strong score 2x, Active score 1x. From this ranked list, select the top 5 contacts for this week's outreach queue. For each, provide: name, tier, last contact date, days overdue, and a suggested outreach angle based on recent context from their contact record in vault/social/00_current/. Output the queue as a 5-item action list."

5. **Overdue Contact Alert:** "Scan vault/social/00_current/ for all contacts where the days since last interaction exceeds their target check-in interval. Generate three alert tiers: Critical — Close-tier contacts overdue by more than 14 days (relationship at active risk). Warning — Strong-tier contacts overdue by more than 20 days or Active-tier contacts overdue by more than 30 days. Watch — any contact approaching their interval deadline within 7 days. For each Critical contact output the last interaction summary from vault/social/00_current/ to inform the re-engagement approach. Output the full alert list sorted by severity."

6. **Dormant Contact Re-engagement Plan:** "Identify all contacts in vault/social/00_current/ who have crossed the 120-day Dormant threshold. For each dormant contact provide: name, last interaction date, relationship context, and a tailored re-engagement angle. Use these triggers to personalize: recent job change, published content or work, industry news in their field, or a shared experience or connection. A generic 'just checking in' has low response rates — each message must reference something specific to their world. Output a re-engagement brief per contact with a draft opening sentence. Prioritize contacts with the highest prior relationship strength score."

7. **Reconnect Trigger Detector:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ and cross-reference each contact's profile for recent trigger events that create a natural outreach window: new job or promotion, new city or relocation, major content they published, an award or recognition, a shared news event in their industry, or a mutual connection change. A trigger-based outreach has 3× higher response rates than an unprompted check-in. Flag all contacts with an active trigger in the last 30 days. For each flagged contact, output: the trigger event, days since it occurred, the optimal outreach window (within 5 days of the trigger is best), and a suggested opening line referencing the trigger."

## Birthdays and Milestones

8. **Birthday and Milestone Watch:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ and identify all contacts with a birthday or significant milestone — work anniversary, graduation, major life event — occurring in the next 30 days. Apply the 48-hour outreach rule: reach out within 48 hours of a milestone, never on the day itself (same-day outreach reads as transactional rather than genuine). For each upcoming event output: contact name, event type, event date, days until event, optimal outreach date (2 days before), and a suggested message tone — warm and personal for close contacts, professional and brief for weaker ties. Sort by event date ascending."

9. **Birthday Message Drafts:** "For the top 5 upcoming birthdays in vault/social/00_current/ in the next 14 days, draft a personalized birthday outreach message for each. The message should: reference something specific to that person's recent work, life, or shared history — not a generic wish, be appropriately brief (2–4 sentences), match the communication channel they prefer (noted in their contact record), and include a genuine offer of value, catch-up, or conversation starter. Output 5 ready-to-send message drafts, each with a note on recommended send date (2 days before the birthday, not on the day)."

## Relationship Health

10. **Relationship Health Scorecard:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ for all Close and Strong tier contacts. Calculate a Relationship Health Score for each using three factors: Frequency (last contact within interval = 3 points, within 2× interval = 2, within 3× interval = 1, beyond 3× = 0), Depth of last interaction (meaningful/personal = 3, professional exchange = 2, surface/transactional = 1, social media only = 0), and Reciprocity (they initiated last = 3, mutual = 2, I always initiate = 1, no reciprocation = 0). Total score 0–9. Flag scores below 4 as relationship-health-at-risk. Output a full scorecard table sorted by score ascending."

11. **Network Concentration Risk Analysis:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ and analyze the composition of my Strong and Close tier contacts across three dimensions: employer (what percentage work at the same company as me), industry (what percentage are in my primary industry), and geography (what percentage are in the same metro area). Flag if any single dimension exceeds 70% — this represents dangerous network concentration that leaves me vulnerable if that employer, industry, or location undergoes disruption. Output a concentration analysis with percentages per dimension and specific recommendations for network diversification targets — specific types of contacts to add."

12. **Introduction Capital Tracker:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ for all logged introductions — instances where I connected two contacts who did not know each other. For each introduction record: the two parties, the date, the context, and whether the introduction resulted in a meaningful outcome. Introduction-making is one of the highest-trust signals in a network and builds significant social capital. Calculate: total introductions made this year, my introduction-to-follow-up rate, and any introductions where I am owed a reciprocal introduction. Flag the top 3 contacts I should introduce to each other based on mutual value alignment, and draft an introduction rationale for each."

13. **Referral Readiness Check:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ and identify contacts from whom requesting a referral or recommendation would be appropriate. The referral-readiness threshold requires at least 3 meaningful prior exchanges, the last interaction within 60 days, and a clear mutual value history. Flag any contact in my active outreach log in vault/social/00_current/ where I have asked for a referral too early — fewer than 3 meaningful exchanges. For contacts who meet the referral-readiness threshold, output a prioritized list with: relationship strength score, last interaction date, and the specific type of referral or introduction they are best positioned to provide."

## Outreach and Messaging

14. **Personalized Outreach Drafter:** "Using the contact record for [name] in vault/social/00_current/ and the interaction history in vault/social/00_current/, draft a personalized outreach message. The message must: open with a specific reference to their recent work, news, or life event — not a generic opener, state a clear and genuine reason for reaching out, include a low-friction call to action (a question, a resource share, or a short coffee chat invite), and match the appropriate length for the relationship depth — 2 sentences for a weaker tie, 4–5 for a close contact. Output the draft message plus a recommended send channel (email, LinkedIn, text) based on their contact preferences."

15. **Coffee Chat Invite Sequence:** "Draft a 3-part coffee chat invite sequence for a contact I want to deepen a relationship with. Message 1 (Initial ask): warm opener referencing something specific to their world, a genuine reason why I want to connect, and a clear low-friction ask — virtual coffee, 20 minutes. Message 2 (Follow-up, 7 days if no response): lighter touch, one sentence acknowledging they may be busy, re-extending the offer with an alternative format (async voice note, quick email exchange). Message 3 (Final, 14 days): graceful close — no pressure, leave the door open for future connection. Output all 3 messages ready to send, with recommended timing noted."

16. **Reconnection Message for Long Gap:** "For a contact in vault/social/00_current/ where last interaction was more than 6 months ago, draft a re-engagement message that acknowledges the gap without over-apologizing — over-apologizing for lapsed contact is awkward and puts the burden on the other person. The message should: open with something genuinely interesting about their recent work or life, make a specific reference that proves you have been paying attention even if not in direct contact, offer a clear value (share an article, make an introduction, express genuine curiosity about a project they are working on), and close with a soft ask. Output the draft message and a one-line rationale for why this approach will land better than a generic reconnect."

## Logging

17. **Interaction Log Entry:** "Log an interaction with [name] to vault/social/00_current/ using the standard interaction schema: contact name, date, medium (in-person, call, video, email, text, social comment), duration in minutes if applicable, topics discussed (2–3 bullet points), emotional tone of the interaction (energizing/neutral/draining), any commitments made by either party, and a follow-up due date if applicable. After logging, recalculate the contact's relationship health score and update their last-contact date in vault/social/00_current/. If this interaction closes an overdue check-in, remove the contact from the overdue list."

18. **Batch Interaction Logging:** "I have had multiple interactions this week that need to be logged to vault/social/00_current/. For each, capture: contact name, date, medium, key topics, any commitments or next steps, and follow-up date if applicable. After logging all interactions, run a batch update: refresh last-contact dates for all logged contacts in vault/social/00_current/, remove them from the overdue queue if applicable, and check whether any interaction created an introduction opportunity or a referral opening. Output a batch log confirmation showing updated relationship health scores for each contact touched."

## Contacts

19. **New Contact Onboarding:** "Add a new contact to vault/social/00_current/ with the full standard schema: full name, relationship context (how we met, mutual connections), relationship tier assignment (Close/Strong/Active), preferred communication channel, check-in interval in days, birthday or known milestones, current employer and role, geographic location, key professional interests, and notes on the first interaction. After adding, check whether this contact diversifies or concentrates my network on the employer, industry, or geography dimensions from my concentration analysis. Flag if the addition increases any concentration dimension above 70%."

20. **Contact Tier Reassignment Review:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ and identify contacts whose assigned relationship tier may no longer match the actual relationship depth. Apply these reassignment signals: a Close-tier contact you have not seen in 6+ months may belong in Strong; a Strong contact who has become a regular collaborator or friend may belong in Close; an Active contact with no meaningful interaction in 12 months may belong in Dormant or should be archived. Flag all contacts meeting any reassignment signal. For each, output: current tier, proposed new tier, and the specific interaction pattern that drove the recommendation. Output a reassignment candidate list."

21. **Network Growth Targeting:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ and identify gaps in my network based on my career goals and vision from vault/vision/00_current/. If my target role requires relationships with hiring managers in a specific industry, or my investment goals require connections to real estate operators or lenders, or my business goals require introductions to potential customers — flag each gap. For each identified gap, specify: the type of connection needed, the best acquisition channel (conference, LinkedIn, mutual introduction, community), and 2–3 specific people or organizations I should try to connect with based on my existing network's reach. Output a network gap and growth target report."

## Goals

22. **Monthly Outreach Goal Progress:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ for all logged interactions in the current month. Compare total interactions against my monthly outreach goal from vault/social/00_current/ config. Calculate: total interactions logged, breakdown by tier (Close/Strong/Active), breakdown by interaction medium (in-person, call, email, text), and percentage of goal achieved. Flag if the month is more than 50% complete and goal achievement is below 40% — this signals a pace problem. Output a progress bar visual (text-based) and a recommendation on which tier to focus on for the remaining days to hit the monthly goal."

23. **Genuine Outreach Quality Audit:** "Read vault/social/00_current/ for all logged interactions in the past 30 days. For each interaction, assess the quality dimension using the logged context notes. Classify each as: High-quality (specific topic discussed, value exchanged, commitment or follow-up generated), Medium-quality (brief catch-up, friendly but no depth), or Low-quality (transactional, social media comment, birthday reaction only). Calculate the distribution across quality levels. Flag if more than 50% of interactions are low-quality — network maintenance requires depth not just frequency. Output a quality distribution report and recommend 3 contacts for a deeper engagement this week."

## Open Loops

24. **Social Open Loops Triage:** "Scan vault/social/ across all subdirectories for any flagged action items, pending follow-ups, or unresolved commitments. Categorize each by type: (1) Overdue check-in — contact past their interval threshold, (2) Committed follow-up — I promised to send something, make an introduction, or follow up on a topic, (3) Pending introduction — I identified two contacts to connect but have not sent the intro yet, (4) Unanswered outreach — I sent a message but have not followed up after 7+ days, (5) Logged interaction with no follow-up date despite a commitment being made. Output a triage list sorted by days overdue with recommended next action per item."

25. **Relationship System Health Check:** "Perform a structural audit of the social vault: vault/social/00_current/ and vault/social/00_current/. Check: (1) Every Close and Strong tier contact has a last-contact date recorded within their interval. (2) Every logged interaction in vault/social/00_current/ has a follow-up date recorded if a commitment was made. (3) The network concentration score on employer and industry dimensions is below 70%. (4) I have made at least 1 introduction in the past 30 days. (5) My weekly outreach pace is on track for my monthly goal. Score each check Pass/Fail and output a relationship system health scorecard with specific remediation steps for each failure."
