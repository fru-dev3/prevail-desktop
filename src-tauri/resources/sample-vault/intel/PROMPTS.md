# AI Ready Life: Intel — Prompt Reference

## Daily and Weekly Briefings

1. **Morning Brief:** "Run my morning intel brief using the latest content from vault/intel/02_briefs/. Scan for stories across my core interest areas: AI and machine learning, personal finance and markets, technology and product releases, macro economics, and regulatory developments. Apply source credibility tiers: prioritize Tier 1 sources (primary sources, major financial publications such as WSJ, FT, Bloomberg) over Tier 2 (reputable analysis, established tech media such as TechCrunch, The Information) over Tier 3 (opinion and commentary — label clearly as such). Output in this exact format: (1) Lead Story — 1 concise paragraph with why it matters to me; (2) Top 5 Headlines — each with a 1-sentence summary and relevance tag; (3) One Thing To Act On; (4) One Thing To Watch. Maximum 400 words total."

2. **Weekly Intel Digest:** "Generate my weekly intel digest by reviewing all items in vault/intel/02_briefs/ from the past 7 days. Identify the top 5 stories that had the most meaningful implications for my interests: AI developments, personal finance, markets, technology, and macro. For each story: write a 2–3 sentence summary distinguishing what happened (news), why it matters (analysis), and what I should do differently if anything (implication). Flag any story that requires action within the next 7 days. Close with a 'Watch List for Next Week' — 3 topics or events to track. Output a structured digest with clear section headers."

3. **Intel Review:** "Pull all unreviewed items from vault/intel/02_briefs/ and run a full intel review. Sort items by signal strength: high-signal items with direct action implications first, informational items second, background/context items third. Apply my niche filter (AI, personal finance, tech, markets) — flag and deprioritize anything that falls outside these domains unless it has macro-level implications. For high-signal items, extract the single most important implication for me. Output a tiered briefing: Action Items, Key Signals, Background Reading."

4. **Daily Briefing Summary:** "Summarize today's top stories across vault/intel/02_briefs/ into a 5-minute read. Distinguish between three types of content: (1) News — what actually happened (objective facts only, no interpretation); (2) Analysis — why it matters, with source credibility tier noted; (3) Implications — what this means I should think about or do differently. For every story, state the original source and tier (Tier 1/2/3). Cap output at 500 words. Prioritize market-moving events (S&P 500 moves greater than 1.5%, Fed rate decisions, major earnings releases, CPI/jobs/GDP reports) and AI capability announcements."

## Source Scanning

5. **Topic Source Scan:** "Scan vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/ for all stored content on a specified topic. Organize findings chronologically: what happened first, what happened next, and where things stand now. For each item note the original source, publication date, and credibility tier. Identify any contradictory claims across sources and flag them explicitly. Distinguish confirmed facts from speculation and analysis. Close with a 2–3 sentence synthesis: the current state of this topic based on all available evidence. Output a structured topic scan with dated entries."

6. **Source Quality Audit:** "Review the sources currently referenced in vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/. Classify each source by tier: Tier 1 (primary sources, peer-reviewed research, major financial publications with editorial standards — WSJ, FT, Bloomberg, Reuters, AP), Tier 2 (reputable analysis and established tech/finance media — The Information, TechCrunch, Ars Technica, Morning Brew, Axios Pro), Tier 3 (opinion, commentary, social media, newsletters without editorial oversight — label all output from these as opinion, not fact). Flag any sources that have been flagged for inaccuracy, bias, or misleading headlines in the last 12 months. Recommend 3 sources to add for better coverage of any gap areas in my current source mix."

7. **Trending Topic Radar:** "Using vault/intel/02_briefs/ from the past 14 days, identify topics that are gaining momentum — appearing in more sources, more frequently, with increasing prominence. Rank by momentum score (frequency × recency × source tier). Flag any topic that went from zero mentions to 3+ mentions in the past week as a fast-emerging signal. For each trending topic: provide a 2-sentence summary of the core development driving the trend, note whether this is a genuine signal or noise (distinguishing based on source tier and corroboration), and identify whether it has implications for my finances, career, or AI interests. Output a ranked trending topics list."

## Topic Deep Dives

8. **AI Sector Deep Dive:** "Conduct a deep dive on recent AI developments using vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/. Cover: (1) new model releases or significant capability announcements from major labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind, Meta, Mistral) in the last 30 days; (2) regulatory developments (EU AI Act implementation milestones, US executive orders, state-level AI legislation); (3) enterprise adoption signals (major company AI deployment announcements); (4) AI safety and alignment developments; (5) notable AI products or features relevant to personal productivity. For each item distinguish between confirmed releases, announced roadmap items, and rumors. Source and tier every claim. Output a structured AI sector briefing."

9. **Market and Macro Deep Dive:** "Using vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/, compile a market and macro deep dive for the past 30 days. Cover: (1) S&P 500 performance and any single-day moves greater than 1.5% with causal explanation; (2) Fed rate decisions or significant FOMC communications and market interpretation; (3) inflation data (CPI/PCE releases) vs. expectations; (4) labor market data (jobs reports, unemployment rate); (5) any significant earnings releases from major companies; (6) geopolitical or macro events with market implications. For each data point: report the actual figure, the consensus expectation, and the market reaction. Output a structured macro briefing with a 'What This Means' section at the end."

10. **Company Tracker:** "Using vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/, pull all stored intelligence on a specified company from the past 90 days. Organize by category: earnings and financial performance, product launches and roadmap, leadership changes, regulatory or legal developments, competitive moves, and analyst sentiment shifts. For each item note date, source, and source tier. Identify the single most significant development and its likely implication for the company's trajectory. Flag any items that appear in only one source — these should be treated as unverified until corroborated. Output a structured company intelligence brief."

11. **Regulatory and Policy Watch:** "Using vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/, compile a regulatory and policy intelligence brief covering developments relevant to AI, personal finance, and technology in the past 30 days. For each development: describe the proposed or enacted rule, identify the effective date and jurisdiction, assess the likely impact (high/medium/low) on my interests, and note whether any action is required on my part (e.g., new tax rule, AI regulation affecting tools I use). Distinguish between proposed rules (still in comment period), enacted rules (signed into law), and implemented rules (in effect). Source and tier every item. Output a policy watch briefing."

## Filtering and Priorities

12. **Signal vs. Noise Filter:** "Review the items in vault/intel/02_briefs/ for the current week and apply a strict signal-to-noise filter. Signal criteria: the story has a direct implication for my financial situation, investment portfolio, career in AI/tech, tools I use, or requires a decision or action within the next 30 days. Noise criteria: celebrity news, culture war content, sports outcomes, and international political drama with no financial or tech implication. For items that pass the signal filter, rate the urgency (high/medium/low) and action required (yes/no). Discard noise items. Output a filtered signal list with urgency ratings and action flags."

13. **Priority Story Extraction:** "From vault/intel/02_briefs/, extract only the highest-priority stories for the current period — the ones I absolutely cannot miss. Apply this threshold: a story qualifies as priority if it involves a major AI model release or capability leap, a Fed rate decision or significant policy shift, an S&P 500 move greater than 1.5%, a major regulatory development affecting AI or personal finance, or breaking news about a company I am invested in or work for. For each priority story: write 2–3 sentences summarizing the development and its direct implication for me. Maximum 5 priority items — if more items qualify, rank by direct personal relevance and take only the top 5."

14. **Personal Finance Intelligence Filter:** "Filter vault/intel/02_briefs/ for all items relevant to personal finance, investing, and wealth building. Include: interest rate changes, inflation data, stock market developments, retirement account rule changes, tax law updates, Social Security or Medicare developments, major economic indicators. For each item: note the actual data point or decision, what it means for a typical personal investor, and any action I should consider (e.g., rebalancing, contribution changes, refinancing opportunity). Flag any item that requires action within the next 30 days. Output a personal finance intelligence summary sorted by action urgency."

15. **AI Tools and Products Filter:** "Filter vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/ for intelligence specifically about AI tools, products, and applications that could affect my personal productivity, content creation workflow, or software development. Cover: new AI product releases or significant feature updates from tools I use (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Cursor, Perplexity, Notion AI, etc.), new entrants worth evaluating, pricing changes, capability comparisons, and user reviews from credible sources. For each item: note the tool, the development, and a one-sentence assessment of whether this warrants a trial, a switch, or is just noise. Output a filtered AI tools intelligence brief."

## Sources

16. **Source Inventory Review:** "List all intelligence sources currently catalogued in vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/. For each source show: name, URL or access method, credibility tier (1/2/3), content category (AI, finance, macro, tech, general news), frequency (daily/weekly/monthly), and last accessed date. Flag any source not accessed in more than 30 days — either reactivate or remove from the active source list. Identify coverage gaps: are there domains I care about with fewer than 2 Tier 1 or Tier 2 sources? Recommend 3 specific sources to add to close the highest-priority gap. Output a source inventory table with gap analysis."

17. **Source Recommendation:** "Based on my tracked interest areas — AI, personal finance, technology, macro economics, and markets — recommend 5 new sources to add to my intel system that are not already in vault/intel/02_briefs/. For each recommended source: provide the name, URL, credibility tier, content type, publication frequency, and a one-sentence case for why it would improve my intelligence coverage. Prioritize sources with strong editorial standards and a track record of breaking important stories ahead of the broader media cycle. Include at least 2 Tier 1 sources and at least 1 source that provides primary data (e.g., Fed, BLS, Census, primary research)."

## Saving and Archiving

18. **Digest Archive:** "Archive the current intel digest from vault/intel/02_briefs/ to vault/intel/01_prior/. Before archiving: tag the digest with date, key topics covered (for future search), and a 1-sentence summary of the most significant item. Confirm that any high-signal items have been extracted to an action list or watch list before archiving. Update the archive index in vault/intel/01_prior/ with the new entry. Output a confirmation of the archive action and a count of total digests now in archive."

19. **Saved Stories Review:** "Pull all saved and starred stories from vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/. Group by topic area: AI, personal finance, macro, technology, regulatory. For each saved story: show date, source, headline, and the reason it was saved (if noted). Flag any saved story that is now outdated or superseded by newer developments. Identify any saved story that I marked for follow-up action and verify whether the action was taken. Output a saved stories review table with follow-up status."

## Companies and People

20. **Earnings Calendar Alert:** "Using vault/intel/02_briefs/, flag any upcoming earnings releases from companies I track or am invested in. For each upcoming earnings event: company, reporting date, analyst consensus EPS estimate, consensus revenue estimate, and key metrics to watch. For recently reported earnings: actual vs. estimate on EPS and revenue, management guidance for next quarter, and the market reaction (stock price change the day of / day after). Identify whether the result changes any investment thesis I hold. Output an earnings calendar with actual vs. estimate comparisons for recently reported companies."

21. **Person Tracker:** "Pull all recent mentions of a specified person from vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/ covering the past 90 days. Organize by category: public statements (speeches, interviews, social posts), actions taken (decisions, investments, hires, departures), and third-party coverage (reporting, analysis, criticism). For each item note date, source, and source tier. Flag any item that appears in only one Tier 3 source as low-confidence. Synthesize a current view of this person's stated position or trajectory in 3–5 sentences. Output a structured person intelligence brief."

22. **Competitor or Industry Scan:** "Using vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/, run an intelligence scan on a specified industry or competitive landscape. Cover the last 30 days. For each major player or development: what moved (funding, product, partnership, regulation, personnel), what it signals about the direction of the industry, and whether it creates any opportunity or risk for me professionally or financially. Distinguish confirmed events from analyst speculation. Flag the single most important competitive shift in this period. Output a structured industry intelligence brief with a 'So What' implications section."

## Open Loops

23. **Intel Open Loops Review:** "Scan all flagged action items, watch-list items, and follow-ups across vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/. Produce a master intel action list sorted by urgency. For each item include: topic, the specific action or monitoring task, deadline or event date, and the decision or outcome I am waiting for. Group into: must-follow-up this week, watching through the next 30 days, and longer-horizon watch items. Output a numbered checklist."

24. **Intel System Health Check:** "Audit the health of my overall intel system using vault/intel/02_briefs/ and vault/intel/01_prior/. Check: (1) frequency — is a digest being generated at least weekly? Flag if more than 10 days since the last digest; (2) source diversity — do digests draw from at least 3 different Tier 1 or Tier 2 sources? Flag if all items consistently come from 1–2 sources; (3) archive hygiene — is the archive indexed and searchable? Flag any month with more than 10 unindexed digests; (4) action rate — are items flagged for action actually being resolved? Flag if more than 5 unresolved action items are more than 30 days old; (5) topic coverage gaps — are any of my tracked domains (AI, finance, macro, tech) missing from digests for more than 14 days? Output a system health scorecard."

25. **Weekly Watch List:** "Based on the events and developments tracked in vault/intel/02_briefs/ this week, generate a 'Watch List for Next 7 Days' covering the most important items to monitor. Include: scheduled economic data releases (CPI, jobs, FOMC meetings, GDP), earnings releases from companies I track, expected AI announcements or product launches, regulatory decisions or vote dates, and any developing story where a significant update is expected. For each watch item: describe what to watch for, when to expect it, and what the key scenarios and implications are (what happens if the outcome is better than expected vs. worse than expected). Output a structured 7-day watch list."
