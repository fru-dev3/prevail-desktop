# AI Ready Life: Business — Prompt Reference

## Monthly Reviews

1. **Monthly Business Review:** "Run my monthly business review. Read vault/business/00_current/ for total revenue this month, then read vault/business/00_current/ for total operating expenses, and calculate gross margin (revenue minus COGS) and net margin (gross profit minus operating expenses). Flag if gross margin falls below 60% or net margin falls below 20%, which are healthy thresholds for a service business. Check vault/business/00_current/ for any compliance deadlines within the next 30 days. Review vault/business/00_current/ for any contractor who has crossed the $1,000 annual payment threshold requiring a 1099-NEC. Output a one-page business brief with a P&L summary, three financial health indicators in green/yellow/red, and a prioritized action list for the month."

2. **Business Brief:** "Give me my business brief for this period. Read vault/business/00_current/ for my active business entities, operating model, and current revenue goals. Pull this month's actuals from vault/business/00_current/ and vault/business/00_current/ and compare to the same period last month. Calculate month-over-month revenue growth rate and flag if it is below 5% for two consecutive months. Check vault/business/00_current/ for the next compliance event (annual report, BOI filing, or registered agent renewal) and how many days away it is. Identify the top 3 business actions that need my attention this week based on urgency and financial impact. Output as an executive summary with three sections: financial snapshot, compliance status, and top actions."

3. **Business Synthesis:** "Run my business synthesis across all entities and revenue streams. Read vault/business/00_current/ for my entity structure and each entity's operating model. Cross-reference vault/business/00_current/ to identify which entity generated the most revenue this month and which is below target. Compute the combined net profit across all entities and the effective net margin. Read vault/business/00_current/ to confirm that no contractor is misclassified — verify each active contractor relationship passes the IRS behavioral control test (contractor controls how work is done) and financial control test (contractor sets their own fees and works for multiple clients). Flag any contractor relationship that shows signs of employee-like control. Deliver a synthesis report with entity-level P&L, a classification risk assessment, and 2 strategic recommendations."

## P&L

4. **P&L Summary:** "Build my P&L summary for the current month and quarter to date. Read vault/business/00_current/ for all revenue line items and classify them by type (product sales, service fees, licensing, royalties). Read vault/business/00_current/ for all expense line items and classify by category (COGS, marketing, software subscriptions, contractor payments, professional services, other operating). Calculate: gross revenue, COGS, gross profit, gross margin %, total operating expenses, net profit, and net margin %. Benchmark gross margin against the 60% healthy threshold and net margin against the 20% threshold. If I am on accrual basis, note any revenue recognized this month that has not yet been invoiced. Output a structured P&L statement with variance from last month and a three-sentence narrative on the key drivers."

5. **Revenue Goal Tracking:** "How am I tracking against my monthly revenue goal? Read vault/business/00_current/ for my stated monthly and annual revenue targets. Read vault/business/00_current/ for all revenue received and recognized this month through today's date. Calculate the percentage of the monthly goal achieved, the daily revenue run rate required to hit the goal by month end, and whether the current run rate (total revenue to date divided by days elapsed) meets that requirement. Flag if I am more than 20% behind pace. Identify which revenue stream is most underperforming relative to its sub-target. Output: a goal-vs-actual table, a pace-to-goal indicator, and one specific action to accelerate the lagging revenue stream."

6. **Month-over-Month Revenue Comparison:** "Compare revenue this month to last month and explain the variance. Read vault/business/00_current/ for both months and compute: total revenue each month, revenue by source/stream each month, and the dollar and percentage change for each stream. Flag any stream where revenue declined more than 10% month-over-month. Identify any one-time or non-recurring items in either month that distort the trend (e.g., a large one-off project payment). Calculate the core recurring revenue for each month excluding non-recurring items and assess the true underlying growth rate. Output a variance table by revenue stream, a highlight on the biggest positive and negative mover, and a one-paragraph trend narrative."

7. **Margin Health Check:** "What is my current profit margin and is it healthy? Read vault/business/00_current/ for total revenue and vault/business/00_current/ for COGS and operating expenses. Compute gross margin % and net margin % and benchmark each against the service business thresholds of 60% gross and 20% net. Identify the top 3 expense categories by dollar amount and calculate each as a percentage of revenue. Flag any expense category that grew more than 15% month-over-month. Compute self-employment tax exposure: if I am a sole proprietor or single-member LLC taxed as a sole proprietor, apply the 15.3% SE tax rate to net self-employment income and note that half is deductible. Output a margin dashboard with each metric, benchmark status, and the top 2 levers to improve net margin."

## Revenue & Pipeline

8. **Pipeline Summary:** "Build my pipeline summary as of today. Read vault/business/00_current/ for any deals or opportunities currently tracked in the pipeline section. For each opportunity, extract: client or prospect name, deal value, current stage, probability of close, and expected close date. Calculate total pipeline value and weighted pipeline value (deal value multiplied by close probability). Flag any deal that has not had activity logged in more than 14 days as stale. Identify which deal, if closed, would most improve this month's revenue-to-goal gap. Output a pipeline table sorted by expected close date, a weighted pipeline total, a stale deal alert list, and a recommended next action for the highest-value opportunity."

9. **Outstanding Proposals Review:** "What proposals are outstanding and what is the total value at risk? Read vault/business/00_current/ for all proposals submitted but not yet accepted or declined. For each proposal, note: client name, proposed value, date submitted, and the number of days since submission. Flag any proposal older than 14 days with no response as requiring follow-up. Calculate the total value of outstanding proposals. Assess which proposals are most likely to close based on client signals logged in the notes. For the top 2 highest-value stale proposals, draft a one-sentence follow-up message I can use to re-engage the prospect. Output a proposal tracker table plus follow-up drafts."

## Invoices

10. **Overdue Invoice Alert:** "What invoices are overdue and what is my total accounts receivable exposure? Read vault/business/00_current/ for all invoices issued in the past 90 days. For each invoice, extract: client name, invoice amount, invoice date, due date, and payment status. Flag any invoice where the due date has passed and payment has not been received. Calculate total overdue AR balance. Flag any client where AR is more than 45 days outstanding as requiring escalation beyond a standard reminder. For each overdue invoice, recommend a specific action: send a payment reminder email, assess a late fee per the contract, or escalate to collections. Output an AR aging table grouped by 0–30, 31–45, and 45+ days overdue, with a dollar total per bucket and a recommended action per overdue invoice."

11. **Outstanding Receivables Summary:** "What is my outstanding receivables total and how does it affect my cash position? Read vault/business/00_current/ for all unpaid invoices and group them by client and days outstanding. Calculate total outstanding AR. Cross-reference with vault/business/00_current/ to identify any upcoming expenses due within the next 30 days that exceed my current cash balance minus the outstanding AR. Flag any cash timing mismatch where I have bills due before the AR is expected to be collected. Identify my single largest outstanding invoice and assess what can be done to accelerate that payment (e.g., offer 2% early payment discount, send a payment portal link). Output an AR summary, a cash timing risk flag, and a recommended action for the top 3 unpaid balances."

## Expenses

12. **Expense Categorization Review:** "Categorize and analyze my expenses for last month. Read vault/business/00_current/ for all transactions in the prior calendar month. Group each expense by IRS Schedule C category: advertising, contract labor, office expenses, professional fees, software/subscriptions, travel, meals (50% deductible), and other. Sum each category and calculate its percentage of total revenue. Flag any category that grew more than 20% versus the prior month without a clear business justification logged in the notes. Identify any uncategorized transactions and suggest the most appropriate category for each based on the payee name and amount. Output a categorized expense table, a total expense-to-revenue ratio, and 3 specific expenses I should consider cutting or renegotiating."

13. **Recurring Subscriptions Audit:** "What are my recurring business subscriptions and are they all justified? Read vault/business/00_current/ and extract all subscription-based charges (monthly or annual recurring payments). For each subscription, note: vendor name, monthly cost, annual cost, category, and last known usage indicator. Flag any subscription with an annual cost above $200 where I have not logged active usage in the last 30 days. Calculate total annual subscription spend. Rank subscriptions by cost and identify the top 3 that are candidates for downgrade, consolidation, or cancellation. Output a subscription inventory table sorted by annual cost, a total annualized spend figure, and 3 cancellation or renegotiation recommendations with estimated annual savings."

14. **Contractor Payment Summary:** "What have I paid each contractor year to date and who will need a 1099? Read vault/business/00_current/ for all active and inactive contractor records. For each contractor, extract: full legal name, business entity type (individual vs. LLC), total payments year to date, and payment method (check vs. ACH vs. PayPal). Flag any individual contractor (not a C-corp or S-corp) who has received more than $1,000 year to date as being on track for a 1099-NEC requirement at the $600 annual threshold. Confirm I have a W-9 on file for every contractor above $400 year to date. Calculate total contractor spend as a percentage of revenue. Output a contractor payment table, a 1099 eligibility tracker, and a W-9 status column with any missing W-9s flagged as urgent."

## Compliance

15. **Compliance Status Review:** "Check my compliance status across all business entities. Read vault/business/00_current/ for the compliance calendar and records for each LLC. For each entity, extract: state of formation, annual report due date and whether it has been filed this year, registered agent name and whether service of process information is current, operating agreement last-amendment date (flag if more than 2 years old), and BOI (Beneficial Ownership Information) report status under FinCEN. Flag any overdue filing or any item due within 30 days. Output a compliance status table with one row per entity, columns for each compliance item, green/red status per item, and a prioritized filing checklist sorted by due date."

16. **Annual Report Due Date Check:** "What is my annual report due date for each business entity and have they been filed? Read vault/business/00_current/ for all registered entities and their state of formation. For each entity, identify the state's annual report (or biennial report) due date, the associated fee, and whether the filing has been confirmed as submitted this year. Flag any entity where the report is overdue or due within 45 days. Confirm the registered agent address on file with the state matches the current registered agent in vault/business/00_current/. If any annual report is overdue, identify the state's late fee schedule and estimate the penalty exposure. Output a filing calendar table with entity name, state, due date, fee, and filed status, plus a total estimated penalty exposure for any overdue items."

17. **Sales Tax Nexus Assessment:** "Do I have sales tax obligations in any state beyond my home state? Read vault/business/00_current/ for all sales and transactions this year and identify the state of the customer or client for each transaction. For each state where I have more than $10,000 in cumulative sales or more than 10 transactions this year, flag it as a potential economic nexus state (the common threshold is $100,000 in annual sales or 200 transactions, but flag early at $10,000 for monitoring). Check vault/business/00_current/ for any states where I am already registered to collect sales tax. Identify any state where I may have crossed or be approaching the nexus threshold without registration. Output a nexus risk table by state with sales volume, transaction count, threshold distance, and a recommended action (register, monitor, or no action needed)."

## Contractors

18. **Contractor Classification Risk Audit:** "Which of my contractor relationships could the IRS classify as employee relationships? Read vault/business/00_current/ for each active contractor and assess them against the IRS three-category test. Check behavioral control: does the contractor control how and when the work is done, or do I dictate that? Check financial control: does the contractor set their own rates, work for multiple clients, and bear their own business expenses? Check relationship type: is there a written independent contractor agreement, no employee benefits, and a project-based (not indefinite) engagement? Flag any contractor who fails two or more criteria as high-risk for reclassification. Note that misclassification carries liability for back payroll taxes plus a 20% penalty. Output a risk matrix with one row per contractor and a low/medium/high reclassification risk rating."

19. **1099 Preparation Checklist:** "Which contractors will need a 1099-NEC this tax year, and do I have everything I need to file? Read vault/business/00_current/ and vault/business/00_current/ (expense side) for all contractor payments made this calendar year. List every individual contractor or unincorporated business paid more than $600 year to date. For each, verify I have: a completed W-9 with their legal name, address, and TIN (SSN or EIN), confirmation of entity type (individual, sole prop, LLC taxed as disregarded entity — all require 1099; C-corp and S-corp generally do not), and total annual payments confirmed. Note that 1099-NEC forms are due to recipients by January 31 and to the IRS by January 31 (if filing electronically). Flag any contractor where W-9 is missing or where the TIN needs verification. Output a 1099-readiness checklist."

## Open Loops

20. **Business Open Loop Triage:** "What business action items are unresolved and how long have they been sitting? Read vault/business/open-loops.md and extract every open item with its creation date, category (revenue, compliance, contractors, expenses, legal, operations), and any last-update note. Calculate days open per item. Flag any compliance-related open loop as high-priority regardless of age, and flag any item open more than 21 days in other categories as stale. For each stale item, assess whether it is blocked (needs input from a third party), deferred (intentionally waiting), or overdue (should have been acted on already). Output a triage table sorted by category priority (compliance first, then revenue, then other), days open, and a recommended next step for the top 5 items."

21. **Business Monthly Action Plan:** "Consolidate all business signals into a prioritized action plan for this month. Read vault/business/00_current/ for pipeline and receivables gaps, vault/business/00_current/ for any overdue or anomalous expenses, vault/business/00_current/ for upcoming deadlines, vault/business/00_current/ for any 1099 or classification issues, and vault/business/open-loops.md for stale items. Identify the top 5 business actions that would most directly protect revenue, reduce risk, or improve cash flow this month. For each action, specify the task, the risk or opportunity it addresses, the time required, and a definition of done. Output as a numbered action plan sorted by urgency with a recommended day of the week to execute each task."
